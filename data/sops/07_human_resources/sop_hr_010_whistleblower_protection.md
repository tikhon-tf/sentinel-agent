# Standard Operating Procedure: Whistleblower Protection

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. ("Meridian" or the "Company") is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct, regulatory compliance, and operational integrity. This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the formal framework through which employees, contractors, and associated third parties may report suspected violations of law, regulation, or Company policy without fear of retaliation. This SOP codifies the Company’s obligations under SOC 2 criteria related to the communication of ethical requirements and the establishment of mechanisms for confidential reporting of suspected breaches. The paramount objective is to ensure that all concerns are addressed promptly, thoroughly, and equitably, safeguarding both the reporter and the organization.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all officers, directors, full-time and part-time employees, temporary staff, independent contractors, consultants, vendors, and business partners (collectively, "Covered Persons") of Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. and its wholly-owned subsidiaries across all global offices, including Boston, London, Berlin, Singapore, and Toronto.

The subject matter covered includes, but is not limited to, good-faith reporting of:
- Financial fraud, accounting irregularities, or internal accounting controls deficiencies.
- Violations of the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).
- Violations of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).
- Misuse or impermissible disclosure of Protected Health Information (PHI).
- Non-compliance with SOC 2 control criteria, specifically those related to monitoring and whistleblowing.
- Bribery, corruption, or violations of the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA).
- Harassment, discrimination, or workplace violence.
- Violations of Federal Reserve guidance SR 11-7 concerning model risk management in HealthPay Financial Services.
- Safety violations or threats to patient safety involving the Clinical AI Platform.
- Retaliation against individuals who have previously reported concerns.

**Exclusions:** This SOP does not supersede standard managerial guidance for routine Human Resources grievances (e.g., standard performance feedback, minor interpersonal conflicts unconnected to policy violations). Such issues should be addressed via the Employee Relations process under SOP-HR-005.

### 3.2 Specific Role Descriptors

- **Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO):** Serves as the policy owner and the final arbiter for disciplinary actions up to and including termination in retaliation cases. Maintains the whistleblower hotline availability in breakrooms and on the intranet.
- **Chief Compliance Officer (CCO):** Oversees the operational integrity of the Investigation Process. Reviews all Reports of financial or regulatory non-compliance. Ensures the Case Management System meets SOC 2 audit trail requirements.
- **General Counsel:** Determines legal privilege for investigations. Approves engagement of external legal counsel or forensic auditors. Manages disclosures to regulatory agencies.
- **Intake Officer:** A dedicated role within the Compliance Department, responsible for retrieving submissions from EthicsPoint within 4 business hours of receipt.

## 4. Policy Statements

Meridian Health Technologies strictly prohibits retaliation against any Covered Person who, in good faith, reports a suspected violation or participates in an investigation. The company is committed to transparent and rigorous investigation protocols.

1.  **Commitment to Non-Retaliation:** Meridian will not tolerate retaliatory acts, including demotion, termination, salary reduction, job reassignment, blacklisting, or harassment against a Reporter or witness. Any employee found to have engaged in retaliation shall be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination of employment and forfeiture of unvested equity.
2.  **Good Faith Standard:** Reports must be made in good faith, based on reasonable belief. A Report may ultimately be unsubstantiated, but as long as it is made in good faith, the Reporter is fully protected under this SOP.
3.  **Confidentiality:** Meridian shall protect the confidentiality of the Reporter and the subject of the investigation to the maximum extent possible under the law, consistent with the need to conduct an adequate investigation. Breach of confidentiality by an Investigation Lead or Intake Officer is a terminable offense.
4.  **Anonymity:** Meridian fully supports the right to report violations anonymously. No effort will be made by the Company to de-anonymize a Reporter via technical means (e.g., IP tracing of web submissions), except where mandated by lawful court order. Retaliation against a person *suspected* of being an anonymous Reporter is prohibited.
5.  **Zero-Tolerance for False Accusations:** Knowingly making a false accusation, tampering with evidence, or misleading an investigation constitutes a Bad Faith Report and will result in severe disciplinary action, independent of the subject matter of the original Report.

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Reporting Channels

Covered Persons may submit a Report through the following approved channels. All channels are monitored 24/7/365.

**5.1.1 Meridian Ethics Hotline (Primary)**
- **Web:** navigate to `meridian.ethicspoint.com`. The portal supports multi-language submissions, automatically encrypting data in transit using TLS 1.3.
- **Telephone:** 1-833-MDTN-ETH (1-833-633-6384) for North America; +44-800-046-5568 (UK); +49-800-182-0396 (DE). Translation services are available for over 100 languages.
- **Mechanism:** Upon submission, the system generates a unique Report Key and Password. The Reporter must save this information exclusively; Meridian cannot reset or retrieve this combination. This key allows the anonymous reporter to create a secure "Post Office" box within EthicsPoint to receive follow-up questions from the Intake Officer.

**5.1.2 Direct Internal Reporting**
Covered Persons may report directly to the Chief Compliance Officer, General Counsel, or CHRO. Direct reports may be made via email to `compliance@meridian.health` or by scheduling a confidential meeting. However, direct reporting removes the technical guarantee of anonymity provided by EthicsPoint.

**5.1.3 Board-Level and Audit Committee Channel**
For concerns involving senior executive misconduct or allegations against the CCO or General Counsel, a dedicated email address is provided: `auditcommittee@meridian.health`. This inbox is accessible only by the Chair of the Audit Committee and external legal counsel, bypassing internal management.

### 5.2 Intake and Acknowledgement

The Intake Officer follows a strict SLA for processing incoming Reports.

- **Step 1: Retrieval.** Reports are pulled from the Navex EthicsPoint administration panel every 4 hours during business days (8:00 AM – 6:00 PM ET) and every 8 hours during weekends and company holidays. Automated alerts from the system are sent to the CCO's mobile device for any report categorized as "Financial Fraud" or "Patient Safety."
- **Step 2: Acknowledgement.** Within 24 hours of retrieval, the Intake Officer must post a standardized Acknowledgement Notice to the Reporter’s secure Post Office box (or reply via email, if a direct report). This notice thanks the Reporter and provides the case reference number.
- **Step 3: Logging.** The Report is formally entered into the Navex EthicsPoint CMS. Metadata fields are populated for: jurisdiction (GDPR/HIPAA flagging), business unit involved (Clinical AI, HealthPay, MedInsight), and sensitivity rating.

### 5.3 Triage and Classification

Within 72 hours of the Acknowledgement Notice, the CCO (or designated Intake Manager) shall triage the Report and classify it into one of three severity Tiers.

| Tier | Classification Criteria | Example Allegations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1 (Critical)** | Imminent threat to life/safety, significant regulatory breach, executive misconduct, active intrusion/breach in progress. | Patient harm via Clinical AI error, CISO misconduct, active bribery, large-scale PHI exfiltration. |
| **Tier 2 (Serious)** | Potential regulatory violation, fraud exceeding $50,000, hostile work environment involving protected classes, retaliation claims. | Accounting irregularities in HealthPay, SOC 2 control bypass, FCPA violations, HIPAA breach impact under 500 individuals. |
| **Tier 3 (Standard)** | Policy violations, unsubstantiated concerns with no clear regulatory hook, minor operational non-compliance. | Expense report discrepancies under $1,000, non-compliance with SOP-HR-005 (employee relations), attendance policy abuses. |

### 5.4 Investigation Process

**5.4.1 Assignment of Investigation Lead**
- **Tier 1:** General Counsel assumes Accountable role immediately. Assigns investigation to an external law firm specialized in the relevant area (e.g., Qui Tam, FDA, DOJ) within 8 hours. CISO activates "Litigation Hold" on all relevant Meridian systems (O365, Slack, AWS instances) per SOP-IT-SEC-015.
- **Tier 2:** CCO assigns an internal Investigation Lead, typically from the Compliance Department, within 48 hours. If the Report involves HR misconduct, the CHRO designates a Lead from the Employee Relations Center of Excellence.
- **Tier 3:** Intake Officer assigns the Report to the relevant departmental manager for fact-finding, overseen by HR, within 5 business days.

**5.4.2 Investigation Plan**
The Investigation Lead must draft a formal Investigation Plan within 5 business days of assignment for Tiers 1 and 2. The Plan must be approved by the General Counsel. It includes:
- Allegation summary.
- List of specific policy sections or laws potentially violated.
- List of data sources to be reviewed (email, files, badge logs, clinical logs, transaction logs).
- List of witnesses to interview.
- Estimated timeline.

**5.4.3 Evidence Collection**
All interviews must be documented using a standard "Investigation Memo" template (Form HR-010-A). Interviewees shall be read the "Confidentiality and Non-Retaliation Admonition" statement verbatim. Forensic acquisition of digital assets must be executed by the CISO's team under a chain-of-custody protocol, ensuring hash verification of all forensic images.

**5.4.4 Determination and Reporting**
- The Investigation Lead produces a final Investigation Report detailing findings. The threshold for findings is "Preponderance of Evidence."
- **Substantiated:** Allegation is supported by evidence.
- **Unsubstantiated:** Insufficient evidence to support the allegation.
- **Exonerated:** Evidence proves the allegation is definitely false, but was reported in good faith.
- **Bad Faith:** Evidence proves the allegation was deliberately fabricated.

### 5.5 Remediation and Closure

Upon a finding of "Substantiated," the General Counsel and CHRO shall determine corrective actions, which may include:
- Disciplinary action against the subject (written warning, mandated training, suspension, termination).
- Remediation of process gaps (policy updates, control implementation).
- Clawback of incentive compensation per the Meridian Clawback Policy (SOP-FIN-022).
- Disclosure to regulatory bodies.

The CMS record is updated with the final determination. The Reporter is notified of the investigation closure and general findings, consistent with privacy restrictions, via their secure Post Office box. The case is closed in the CMS, but the audit log remains immutable for a minimum of 7 years per data retention schedules.

## 6. Controls and Safeguards

### 6.1 Technical Controls (SOC 2 CC6.1 — Logical and Physical Access Controls)

- **RBAC for Case Management:** Access to the Navex EthicsPoint CMS is strictly limited via Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) integrated with Meridian’s Azure Active Directory. Only the CCO, Intake Officer, CHRO, and designated Legal Counsels have user accounts. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) via Okta Verify is mandatory.
- **Database Encryption:** The CMS database is encrypted at rest using AES-256. Key management is handled via AWS Key Management Service (KMS) with automatic key rotation every 365 days.
- **Immutable Logging:** All actions within the CMS (viewing a case, downloading an attachment, changing a status) generate immutable audit events which are shipped in real-time to a separate, write-only Meridian Splunk index. This index is air-gapped from administrative access by the CISO’s direct reports; only the Internal Audit team has read-access.

### 6.2 Administrative Controls (SOC 2 CC2.2 — Internal Communication)

- **Segregation of Duties:** The individual responsible for taking the Report (Intake Officer) cannot serve as the Investigation Lead for the same Report. No person who is a potential subject or witness in a Report may access the CMS record for that Report. Access is programmatically blocked via an "entity-lock" script based on UPN (User Principal Name).
- **Confidentiality Agreement:** All individuals involved in the investigation process (intake, forensics, legal) are required to sign a specific "Whistleblower Confidentiality Oath" (Form HR-010-B) annually, reinforcing the criminal penalties associated with breaching whistleblower confidentiality.
- **Data Minimization:** Interview transcripts and forensic evidence must be redacted to remove PII/PHI of non-relevant third parties before being uploaded to the CMS.

### 6.3 Physical Controls

- **Secure Evidence Storage:** Physical evidence (e.g., hand-written notes, USB drives) is logged into the Legal Department’s tamper-evident evidence locker (Securitall Model 800) in the Boston HQ vault room. Access requires dual biometric authentication (General Counsel + CCO).

### 6.4 Anti-Retaliation Monitoring (SOC 2 CC4.1 — Monitoring of Activities)

- **Trigger-Based Audits:** Upon the closure of a Tier 1 or Tier 2 Report, Human Resources Business Partners (HRBPs) must conduct "status checks" on the Reporter (if known) at 30, 90, and 180 days post-closure. These checks review compensation changes, performance review scores, promotion status, and badge-swipe patterns for indicators of constructive discharge or adverse treatment.
- **Termination Lock:** The Workday HRIS system is configured to flag any termination request for a known Reporter within 12 months of a case closure. This termination requires direct approval from the CHRO and CCO before processing.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Escalation Triggers

The Investigation Lead is required to initiate an immediate escalation to the CCO and General Counsel under the following conditions:
- The investigation reveals potential criminal conduct requiring disclosure to law enforcement.
- The investigation implicates a Covered Person at the Senior Vice President level or above.
- The investigation identifies a systemic vulnerability in the Clinical AI Platform directly threatening patient safety (e.g., hallucination rates exceeding the safety threshold defined in SOP-AI-002).
- A Reporter reports active retaliation prior to case closure.

### 8.2 Handling Conflicts of Interest

If the CCO, CHRO, or General Counsel is the subject of a Report, or has a direct reporting relationship with the subject creating a conflict, the following bypass applies:
- **Conflicted Party:** CCO.
- **Escalation Path:** Intake Officer reports directly to the Chair of the Audit Committee.
- **Conflicted Party:** General Counsel.
- **Escalation Path:** CCO engages independent external counsel (pre-designated firm: Ropes & Gray LLP).

### 8.3 Exception to Timelines

There are no exceptions to the Non-Retaliation Policy. Any deviation from the specific timelines mandated in Section 5 (e.g., Triage or Investigation Plan delivery) must be approved in writing by the CCO via a "Case Extension Request" form submitted inside the CMS. The justification must be technical (e.g., awaiting forensic extraction from a foreign jurisdiction) and not resourced-based.
