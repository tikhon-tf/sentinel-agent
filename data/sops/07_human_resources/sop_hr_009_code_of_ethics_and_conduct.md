## SOP-HR-009 | Version 5.2

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the foundational ethical principles, behavioral standards, and compliance obligations governing all operations at Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. (“Meridian” or the “Company”). As an AI-powered healthcare fintech organization entrusted with protected health information (PHI) for approximately 12 million patients, processing $4.2 billion in healthcare payments annually, and deploying high-risk AI systems under the EU AI Act, Meridian's license to operate depends fundamentally on the ethical integrity of its workforce, contractors, and business partners.

This SOP serves to:

- Define the ethical standards that align with Meridian's mission to deliver clinical AI, financial services, and analytics products that improve patient outcomes while safeguarding data privacy and security.
- Establish clear expectations for conduct that comply with applicable laws, regulations, and industry frameworks including HIPAA, GDPR, SOC 2 Type II (Trust Services Criteria), the EU AI Act, FDA regulations, and SR 11-7.
- Provide a framework for identifying, disclosing, and resolving conflicts of interest, ethical dilemmas, and potential violations.
- Protect Meridian's reputation, assets, and stakeholders from the consequences of unethical or illegal behavior.
- Demonstrate to clients, regulators, auditors, and the public that Meridian maintains a robust ethical culture consistent with SOC 2 Common Criteria 1 (CC1): Control Environment.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all individuals and entities associated with Meridian Health Technologies, Inc., including:

| Category | Coverage Detail |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Employees** | All full-time, part-time, and temporary employees across all global offices (Boston, London, Berlin, Singapore, Toronto) and remote workers worldwide. |
| **Contractors and Consultants** | Independent contractors, consultants, and agency personnel engaged by any Meridian business unit. |
| **Board Members** | Members of the Meridian Board of Directors and its subcommittees, including the AI Governance Committee. |
| **Interns and Trainees** | Paid and unpaid interns, fellows, and trainees. |
| **Third-Party Business Partners** | Vendors, resellers, channel partners, and service providers with access to Meridian systems, data, or facilities, to the extent specified in applicable contractual agreements. |
| **Subsidiaries and Affiliates** | Any entity under the direct or indirect control of Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. |

**Geographic Applicability:** All offices, co-working spaces, remote work environments, and any location where Meridian business is conducted.

**Data and System Applicability:** All Meridian systems including the Clinical AI Platform, HealthPay Financial Services, MedInsight Analytics, Meridian SaaS Platform, corporate IT systems, and any system processing Meridian data.

### 1.3 Governance Alignment

This SOP is a component of Meridian's System of Internal Controls and supports compliance with the following SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria:

- **Common Criteria 1 (CC1): Control Environment** — Specifically CC1.1 (Standards of Conduct), CC1.2 (Board Oversight), CC1.3 (Management's Philosophy and Operating Style), and CC1.5 (Individual Accountability).
- **Common Criteria 2 (CC2): Communication and Information** — Specifically CC2.2 (Internal Communication of Objectives).
- **Logical and Physical Access Controls** — Where ethical obligations intersect with system access and data protection.

## 4. Policy Statements

The following high-level policy commitments constitute the ethical foundation of Meridian Health Technologies and are binding on all Personnel.

### 4.1 Commitment to Integrity
Meridian conducts its business with honesty, fairness, and transparency. All business decisions must be free from improper influence, deception, or concealment. Personnel shall not engage in any dishonest or fraudulent conduct, including falsification of records, expense reports, time entries, or business metrics.

### 4.2 Commitment to Legal and Regulatory Compliance
All Personnel must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards in every jurisdiction where Meridian operates. Special emphasis applies to:
- **HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules** — Safeguarding PHI and honoring individual rights (See SOP-IS-015: HIPAA Privacy Rule Compliance).
- **GDPR and UK GDPR** — Protecting personal data of EU and UK data subjects (See SOP-IS-022: Data Subject Rights Management).
- **EU AI Act and EU MDR** — Ensuring clinical AI systems meet regulatory requirements for safety and transparency.
- **Anti-Bribery Laws** — FCPA, UKBA, and applicable local laws.
- **Antitrust and Competition Laws** — Maintaining fair competition.
- **FDA Regulations (21 CFR Part 820 and Part 11)** — Quality system and electronic records compliance for regulated clinical software.

### 4.3 Commitment to Privacy and Data Protection
Meridian recognizes that the data entrusted to it—particularly patient health information—is a sacred responsibility. All Personnel must handle PHI and personal data in strict accordance with Meridian's Data Protection and Privacy Policy (SOP-IS-018), the principle of data minimization, and the defined purpose limitations for each Meridian product.

### 4.4 Commitment to a Speak-Up Culture
Meridian fosters an environment where Personnel feel safe raising ethical concerns without fear of retaliation. The Company maintains multiple confidential and anonymous reporting channels (Section 5.4). Retaliation against any Reporting Person is strictly prohibited and constitutes a Category A violation subject to immediate disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 4.5 Commitment to Fair Dealing
Meridian competes vigorously on the merits of its AI-driven clinical and financial products. Personnel shall not engage in unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices. Competitor intelligence shall be gathered only through lawful and ethical means.

### 4.6 Commitment to Responsible AI
Consistent with the Meridian AI Governance Charter (SOP-AI-001), all AI systems developed or deployed by Meridian shall adhere to principles of fairness, transparency (including explainability), accountability, human oversight, safety, and robustness. Ethical development includes active bias monitoring using the Meridian Algorithmic Fairness Workbench.

### 4.7 Commitment to Accurate Books and Records
All business transactions must be recorded accurately and completely in Meridian's books, records, and accounts in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP) and Meridian's Financial Controls Policy.

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Code of Ethics Acknowledgement Procedure

**Objective:** Ensure that every individual within scope formally acknowledges their understanding and commitment to this SOP.

**Procedure:**
1. **Distribution:** Upon initial onboarding (and at each annual refresh), the CHRO (or designee) distributes the current version of SOP-HR-009 via Meridian's Learning Management System (LMS: Workday Learning) and via electronic mail to all active Personnel.
2. **Review Period:** Personnel are provided a minimum of 10 business days from the date of LMS assignment to review the SOP in full.
3. **Acknowledgement:** Each individual must complete the "SOP-HR-009 Annual Acknowledgement" form within Workday Learning by the specified deadline. The form requires:
   - Digital attestation that the individual has read and understood the SOP.
   - Disclosure of any actual or potential COI (Section 5.2) via the embedded COI Disclosure Form.
   - Confirmation of completion of the annual Code of Ethics training module (Section 9).
4. **Tracking and Escalation for Non-Completion:**
   - **Day -5 before deadline:** Automated Workday reminder generated.
   - **Deadline:** Workday flags non-completion.
   - **Day +1:** Direct manager receives automated notification from Workday with instruction to contact the employee.
   - **Day +5:** CHRO Officer receives consolidated non-compliance report. A formal "Notice of Non-Compliance" is issued to the employee and their manager via ServiceNow HR Case Management.
   - **Day +15:** Continued non-compliance constitutes a violation of this SOP and may result in disciplinary action per SOP-HR-003 (Employee Corrective Action).
5. **Recordkeeping:** Completed acknowledgements are retained within Workday for the duration of employment plus 6 years, consistent with regulatory audit requirements.

### 5.2 Conflicts of Interest (COI) Procedure

**Objective:** Identify, disclose, evaluate, and mitigate all actual, potential, or perceived Conflicts of Interest in a consistent, documented, and auditable manner.

#### 5.2.1 Types of Reportable Conflicts

Personnel must disclose the following categories of interests or relationships:

- **Outside Employment and Consulting:** Any employment, consulting, advisory, or board relationship with any entity (for-profit or non-profit) operating in the healthcare technology, clinical AI, or healthcare financial services sectors.
- **Financial Interests:** Any significant financial interest (>1% equity ownership, or >$10,000 investment) in a customer, competitor, supplier, or business partner of Meridian.
- **Family Relationships:** Any familial or close personal relationship with a Meridian employee where one party has a direct or indirect supervisory, evaluative, or financial influence over the other.
- **Corporate Opportunities:** Any situation where an individual diverts a business opportunity properly belonging to Meridian to their personal benefit or to an external entity in which they hold an interest.
- **Supplier and Vendor Relationships:** Any familial or close personal relationship with a principal, owner, or key decision-maker of a Meridian supplier, vendor, or contractor.
- **Gifts and Entertainment exceeding thresholds** — See Section 5.3.

#### 5.2.2 Disclosure Procedure

| Step | Action | Responsible Party | Timeline | System/Tool |

### 5.3 Gifts and Entertainment Procedure

#### 5.3.1 General Principles

Personnel shall not solicit or accept any Gift, Entertainment, or Hospitality (“G/E/H”) that could reasonably be perceived to influence—or create the appearance of influencing—a business decision. All G/E/H must be:
- **Legitimate:** Associated with a bona fide business purpose (e.g., demonstration of Meridian's Clinical AI Platform capabilities at a conference).
- **Reasonable in Value:** Not extravagant given the context and local business norms.
- **Transparent:** Fully and accurately recorded in Meridian's books, disclosed to the individual's manager, and reported on expense reports with sufficient detail (recipient, affiliation, business purpose, itemized value).
- **Compliant with Recipient's Policies:** Meridian Personnel must respect the gift and ethics policies of the recipient's organization, particularly for government officials, healthcare professionals, and employees of federally regulated entities.

#### 5.3.2 Quantitative Thresholds and Approval Matrix

| Recipient Category | Item Category | Maximum Value (Per Item) | Maximum Cumulative Value (Per Recipient, Per 12-month Period) | Required Approval | Prohibited Items |
|--------------------|---------------|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **U.S. Government Official** (Federal, State, Local) | Any | $20 (Per 5 CFR § 2635.204) | $50 | Legal + CCO | Cash, gift cards, travel, meals exceeding statutory limit. |
| **Foreign Government Official** (Non-U.S.) | Any | $50 | $100 (Annual) | Legal + CCO + CEO | Cash, gifts during active procurement, anything violating local law or FCPA. |
| **Healthcare Professional (HCP) / Customer (U.S.)** | Promotional Item (e.g., branded pen, USB drive — but only encrypted drives permitted) | $25 | $75 | Manager | Cash, gift cards, entertainment (sporting events, concerts) not associated with a formal educational program. |
| **Healthcare Professional (HCP) / Customer (U.S.)** | Modest Meal | $50 (per meal) | $250 | Manager | Alcohol is prohibited at all Meridian-sponsored HCP meals unless permitted by explicit policy (currently: prohibited). |
| **Commercial Customer / Vendor (Non-Government)** | Promotional Item | $100 | $250 | Manager | Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards). |
| **Commercial Customer / Vendor** | Entertainment (Sporting, Cultural) | $200 (per event, face value) | $400 | Director-level or above | Events where Meridian is not present. |
| **Commercial Customer / Vendor** | Travel / Lodging | Only if directly related to a contractually obligated training or evaluation. Pre-approval required. | N/A | VP-level + CCO | First-class travel, luxury hotels (5-star). Marriott/Bonvoy or equivalent business class (3-4 star) is the maximum permitted standard. |

#### 5.3.3 Recordkeeping

All G/E/H above $25 must be recorded on the individual's expense report (via Concur) with the G/E/H classification code, business justification, and recipient details. G/E/H provided to or received from a single source exceeding $250 in a fiscal quarter triggers a quarterly review report generated by Concur and routed to the CCO for compliance audit.

#### 5.3.4 Gifts of AI or Software

Provision of Meridian software licenses, AI model access, API credits, or evaluation environments must be governed by a written agreement executed by authorized Meridian signatories. Such items shall never be provided as an informal "gift" or outside of a contractual framework.

### 5.4 Reporting Violations (Speak Up) Procedure

#### 5.4.1 What to Report

Personnel must report any conduct that they reasonably believe constitutes:
- A violation of this SOP (SOP-HR-009).
- A violation of any Meridian policy, procedure, or standard.
- A violation of any applicable law, regulation, or ethical standard.
- Fraud, theft, embezzlement, or bribery.
- Harassment, discrimination, or retaliation.
- Intentional misuse or unauthorized access to Meridian systems or data (See SOP-IS-007: Acceptable Use Policy).
- Knowingly shipping a clinical AI product with a critical safety defect.

#### 5.4.2 Reporting Channels

Meridian provides multiple reporting channels. Personnel may choose the channel with which they feel most comfortable:

| Channel | Method | Confidential | Anonymous (where legally permissible) | Managed By |
|---------|--------|---------------|---------------------------------------|------------|
| **Direct Management** | Verbal or written communication to direct manager, skip-level manager, or any VP. | Not anonymous; manager must escalate facts, not speculate. | N/A | Reporting Person's manager, escalating to CCO. |
| **Ethics & Compliance Office** | Direct email to compliance@meridianhealthtech.com or phone at (888) 555-MHT-ETHICS. | Confidential to the extent possible during investigation. | Not anonymous but identity protected on a need-to-know basis. | CCO Team |
| **Meridian Integrity Helpline** (Third-party, Convercent/OneTrust Ethics) | Web Portal: meridianhealthtech.ethicspoint.com || Toll-Free Hotline: 1-800-NNN-ETHX (available 24/7, multi-lingual, operators in English, German, and Mandarin available) | Case intake is confidential. Reporter may choose to remain anonymous, and Convercent will not reveal identity to Meridian without consent (except where mandated by law). | Anonymous permitted. #### 5.4.3 Investigation Procedure

1. **Intake and Logging:** All reports received via the Integrity Helpline are auto-logged into the Meridian GRC case management system (ServiceNow GRC — Investigations Module) within 1 hour. Reports received via other channels are manually logged within 24 hours by the CCO office.
2. **Triage:** Within 2 business days, the CCO (or Audit Committee Chair, for board reports) conducts an initial triage to classify the matter based on severity:
   - **Severity 1 (Critical):** Involves CEO, CFO, or a Board Director; potential criminal conduct (bribery, fraud); active, ongoing risk to patient safety or PHI confidentiality affecting >5,000 individuals.
   - **Severity 2 (High):** Involves VP or above; potential significant regulatory non-compliance (HIPAA, GDPR, EU AI Act).
   - **Severity 3 (Medium):** Conflict of Interest, harassment allegation, or other code violations not meeting Severity 1/2 criteria.
   - **Severity 4 (Low):** Minor policy infraction.
3. **Investigation Team Assignment:** The CCO assigns an impartial investigation team. Severity 1 and 2 matters automatically require engagement of external counsel (currently, Fenwick & West LLP) for independent investigation. Severity 3 matters are investigated internally by the E&C team (or HR, in consultation with E&C for harassment). Severity 4 matters are resolved by the direct manager in consultation with HR.
4. **Investigation:** The investigation is conducted promptly, thoroughly, and impartially. A formal investigation notice is sent to involved parties via secure communication. Standard evidence gathering includes interviews, document review, system log analysis (coordinated with CISO), and forensic analysis of Meridian-issued devices (where authorized and consistent with privacy expectations).
5. **Reporting:** A written investigation report is prepared and delivered to the EC (for Severity 1-3) within 30 calendar days of case initiation. The EC reviews findings, makes a determination (Substantiated / Not Substantiated / Inconclusive), and refers for sanction if applicable.
6. **Closure and Feedback:** The Reporting Person is informed that the matter has been reviewed and concluded (without disclosing confidential personnel actions), provided they have left contact information. The case is formally closed in ServiceNow GRC.

### 5.5 Non-Retaliation Safeguard Procedure

Meridian strictly prohibits Retaliation. A claim of retaliation is itself a violation and is handled as a Severity 2 (High) matter under the Investigation Procedure (Section 5.4.3). In addition to the investigation, upon a substantiated finding of retaliation, the following actions shall occur within 10 business days of the EC determination:
- The retaliator shall be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including immediate termination for cause.
- The victim of retaliation shall receive documented remediation, which may include reversal of any adverse action (e.g., reinstatement, correction of performance evaluation, payment of lost wages), facilitated by the CHRO.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Exception Request Procedure

A deviation from a procedural step within this SOP (e.g., an extension to a COI disclosure deadline, an exception to a Gift threshold) must follow this procedure:

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Requestor completes the "Ethics Exception Request Form" in ServiceNow HR Case Management, articulating the specific policy element, the requested deviation, the compelling business justification, the risk assessment, and any proposed compensating controls. | N/A — initiated by requestor |
| 2 | Requestor's VP-level manager reviews and either denies or endorses the request with documented rationale. | 5 business days |
| 3 | CCO and relevant functional stakeholder (e.g., CFO for financial thresholds; CPO for data handling exceptions) conduct a joint review. For any exception involving a potential conflict of interest of the requestor, an independent reviewer from Legal is assigned. | 10 business days |
| 4 | Decision rendered: Approved (with documented expiration date), Approved with Compensating Controls, or Denied. | Communicated within 5 business days of review completion. |

### 8.2 Approving Authority for Exceptions

| Exception Category | Approving Authority |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| COI Mitigation Plan Modification | CCO |
| Gift/Entertainment threshold (single event up to 150% of listed max per item) | Requestor's VP + CCO |
| Training deadline extension (up to 30 days) | CHRO |
| Investigation timeline extension (>45 days) | General Counsel |
| Exception for CEO or a Board Director | Ethics Committee, with written opinion from General Counsel (or external counsel), and the CEO (for Board Director) / Board Chair (for CEO) approval. |

All exceptions are logged and reviewed quarterly by the EC to identify patterns that may warrant an update to this SOP.
