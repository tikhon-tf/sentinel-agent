# Standard Operating Procedure: Workplace Health and Safety

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the framework, governance, and operational protocols for Meridian Health Technologies, Inc.’s Workplace Health and Safety (WHS) program. The purpose of this document is to ensure a safe, healthy, and compliant work environment for all employees, contractors, visitors, and third-party personnel across all global offices (Boston, London, Berlin, Singapore, Toronto) and for remote employees operating within their home work environments.

This SOP defines the systematic approach to hazard identification, risk assessment, incident management, emergency preparedness, ergonomic standards, occupational health monitoring, and continuous improvement. It formalizes Meridian’s commitment to physical and psychological safety as a foundational operational imperative, consistent with our obligations under the SOC 2 Common Criteria for security and availability, and supports the physical safety component of ISO 27001:2022 Annex A Control A.7.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to:

| Scope Category | Coverage Detail |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **Personnel** | All full-time and part-time employees, independent contractors, temporary workers, interns, and volunteers. |
| **Locations** | Meridian-owned and leased facilities in Boston (HQ), London, Berlin, Singapore, and Toronto; co-working spaces utilized by Meridian personnel; and remote/home office work environments for employees enrolled in Meridian’s Remote Work Program. |
| **Activities** | All business operations conducted during regular business hours, after-hours work, business travel, and company-sponsored events. This includes laboratory safety for R&D testing environments, office ergonomics, and field safety for personnel conducting on-site client assessments. |
| **Exclusions** | Manufacturing floor safety (Meridian does not operate manufacturing facilities). Fleet vehicle safety is governed separately under SOP-FIN-022 (Company Vehicle and Travel Policy). |

### 1.3 Policy Hierarchy

This SOP is subordinate to the Meridian Code of Conduct and the Global Employment Standards Policy (SOP-HR-001). In the event of conflict between this SOP and local statutory requirements in any jurisdiction where Meridian operates, the more stringent requirement shall apply.

## 2. Definitions and Acronyms

| Term / Acronym | Definition |

| **HIRA** | Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment — the formal process for identifying workplace hazards and evaluating associated risks. |
| **Incident** | Any unplanned event resulting in or having the potential to result in injury, illness, property damage, or environmental harm. Includes near-misses. |

| **Recordable Incident** | An incident meeting the recording criteria defined by applicable occupational safety regulatory bodies (e.g., OSHA in the United States, HSE in the United Kingdom). |

| **First Aid Case** | An incident requiring first-aid treatment only, not meeting the threshold for recordable classification. |
| **Ergonomic Assessment** | A structured evaluation of an employee’s workstation, tools, and work practices to identify musculoskeletal disorder (MSD) risk factors and recommend corrective controls. |
| **Emergency Control Organization (ECO)** | The designated team of trained personnel responsible for coordinating emergency response within a specific Meridian facility. |
| **Floor Warden** | An ECO member assigned to a specific floor or zone, responsible for evacuation coordination and roll-call verification. |
| **First Aid Officer** | A designated individual holding a current first-aid certification appropriate to the jurisdiction, responsible for rendering initial medical assistance. |
| **MeridianSafe** | The internal digital platform used for WHS incident reporting, risk register management, training assignment, and compliance dashboarding. Accessible via the Meridian intranet portal. |
| **RTO** | Recovery Time Objective — the targeted duration of time within which a disrupted capability must be restored. |
| **RPO** | Recovery Point Objective — the targeted point in time to which data must be recovered following a disruption. |

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Hazard Identification and Risk Assessment (HIRA)

This procedure governs the systematic identification of workplace hazards and the evaluation of associated risks.

#### 5.1.1 HIRA Schedule

| Assessment Type | Frequency | Responsibility |
|-----------------|-----------|----------------|
| Facility Walkthrough Inspection | Monthly | Regional Office Manager |
| Comprehensive Facility HIRA | Annually (Q1) | VP Global Facilities |
| Remote Workstation Virtual Assessment | Upon onboarding, then biennially | People Partners |
| Hazard-Specific Assessment (e.g., chemical, electrical) | Upon introduction of new hazard | VP Global Facilities |
| Post-Incident Targeted HIRA | Within 10 business days of any Recordable Incident | CHRO |

#### 5.1.2 HIRA Methodology

Meridian employs a five-step HIRA cycle:

1. **Hazard Identification:** Walkthrough inspection using the MeridianSafe mobile checklist module. Hazards are categorized as Physical, Ergonomic, Chemical, Biological, Psychosocial, or Environmental.
2. **Risk Evaluation:** Each identified hazard is assigned a Risk Score using the Meridian 5x5 Risk Matrix.

   | Likelihood | 1 (Rare) | 2 (Unlikely) | 3 (Possible) | 4 (Likely) | 5 (Almost Certain) |
   |------------|:--------:|:------------:|:------------:|:----------:|:-------------------:|
   | **Severity 5 (Catastrophic)** | 5 (Moderate) | 10 (High) | 15 (Critical) | 20 (Critical) | 25 (Critical) |
   | **Severity 4 (Major)** | 4 (Moderate) | 8 (Moderate) | 12 (High) | 16 (Critical) | 20 (Critical) |
   | **Severity 3 (Moderate)** | 3 (Low) | 6 (Moderate) | 9 (High) | 12 (High) | 15 (Critical) |
   | **Severity 2 (Minor)** | 2 (Low) | 4 (Low) | 6 (Moderate) | 8 (Moderate) | 10 (High) |
   | **Severity 1 (Negligible)** | 1 (Low) | 2 (Low) | 3 (Low) | 4 (Low) | 5 (Moderate) |

3. **Control Selection:** Controls follow the Hierarchy of Controls — Elimination, Substitution, Engineering Controls, Administrative Controls, and Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), in descending order of preference.
4. **Implementation:** Corrective actions are assigned an owner and due date in MeridianSafe. Due date is based on residual risk:
   - **Critical (15-25):** Within 48 hours; interim controls within 24 hours.
   - **High (8-14):** Within 15 business days.
   - **Moderate (4-7):** Within 60 business days.
   - **Low (1-3):** Scheduled at next planned maintenance cycle.
5. **Verification:** Closed actions are verified by the Regional Office Manager within 5 business days of closure.

### 5.2 Incident Reporting and Investigation

#### 5.2.1 Incident Definitions

| Incident Type | Definition | Examples |
|---------------|------------|----------|
| **Injury/Illness** | Work-related harm to a person. | Cut requiring medical attention; respiratory irritation from chemical exposure; MSD diagnosed by physician. |
| **Property Damage** | Damage to Meridian assets or third-party property. | Broken laboratory equipment; water-damaged ceiling from HVAC leak. |
| **Near-Miss** | An event with potential for injury or damage but none occurred. | Unsecured shelving falling in an unoccupied area; tripping hazard identified before fall. |
| **Environmental Release** | Unpermitted release of a hazardous substance. | Laboratory solvent spill reaching floor drain. |
| **Security Incident** | Physical security breach or threat. | Unauthorized access to a restricted area; verbal threat of violence. |

#### 5.2.2 Reporting Workflow

| Step | Action | Responsible Party | Timeline |
|------|--------|-------------------|----------|
| 1 | Notify immediate supervisor and Regional Office Manager verbally or via Teams/Slack. | Affected Person or Witness | Immediately |
| 2 | Submit Incident Report via MeridianSafe portal. Form I-14-A (Injury/Illness) or I-14-B (Near-Miss/Property Damage). Provide witness details and photographic evidence if available. | Affected Person or Witness (or Supervisor if person is incapacitated) | Within 4 hours of incident |
| 3 | Supervisor reviews report, confirms classification, and acknowledges receipt in MeridianSafe. | Direct Supervisor | Within 8 hours of report submission |
| 4 | Investigation is initiated. Investigation team is assigned based on severity: <br> • **LTI or Critical:** CHRO, VP Facilities, Director Security, Legal Counsel. <br> • **Recordable, Non-LTI:** VP Facilities, Regional Office Manager, People Partner. <br> • **First Aid / Minor Damage:** Regional Office Manager. | Regional Office Manager / CHRO | Within 24 hours |
| 5 | Root cause analysis conducted using the "5 Whys" methodology or Ishikawa diagram as appropriate. Findings documented in MeridianSafe Investigation Module (Form I-14-C). | Investigation Team | Within 10 business days |
| 6 | Corrective actions assigned with owner, due date, and verification criteria. | Investigation Lead | At investigation closure |
| 7 | Verification of corrective action effectiveness. | Regional Office Manager | 30 days post-closure |

#### 5.2.3 Regulatory Reporting

For jurisdictions requiring external reporting to occupational safety regulators (e.g., OSHA in the US, HSE in the UK, MOM in Singapore):

- **Reportable incidents** (fatalities, specified injuries, occupational diseases): The VP Global Facilities, in coordination with Legal Counsel, shall file the mandated regulatory notification within the statutory deadline (typically 8-24 hours depending on jurisdiction).
- **Annual summary reporting**: The People Operations Manager shall compile and, where required, post an annual summary of workplace injuries and illnesses by February 1 of the subsequent year.

### 5.3 Ergonomics Program

#### 5.3.1 Workstation Assessment

All employees shall receive a baseline ergonomic assessment:

| Employee Category | Assessment Method | Performed By | Timeline |
|-------------------|-------------------|--------------|----------|
| New Hire — Office-based | In-person workstation evaluation using Form E-01 | Certified Ergonomic Assessor (contracted via Facilities) | Within first 2 weeks |
| New Hire — Remote | Virtual ergonomic consultation via approved telehealth platform | Contracted Occupational Health Provider (ErgoPlus Solutions) | Within first 30 days |
| Existing Employee — Requested | Re-assessment triggered via MeridianSafe service request | Certified Ergonomic Assessor | Within 15 business days of request |
| Existing Employee — Medical Recommendation | Accommodation-focused assessment in coordination with People Partner | Specialized Ergonomic Consultant | Within 10 business days of medical documentation receipt |

#### 5.3.2 Ergonomic Equipment Standards

The standard workstation configuration includes:

- Adjustable sit-stand desk (electric, minimum 3-stage frame).
- Ergonomic task chair meeting ANSI/BIFMA X5.1-2017 or equivalent standard; features include adjustable lumbar support, seat depth, armrest height, and tilt tension.
- Dual monitor configuration with fully articulating monitor arms.
- Articulating keyboard tray with negative tilt capability.
- Task lighting with adjustable color temperature (3000K–5000K).

**Remote Work Provisioning:** Employees in the Remote Work Program (see SOP-HR-009, Remote Work and Flexible Arrangements) are eligible for a one-time ergonomic equipment stipend of USD $750 (or local currency equivalent), processed through the standard expense reimbursement workflow.

### 5.4 Emergency Procedures

Each Meridian facility maintains a site-specific Emergency Response Plan (ERP). The ERP is a controlled document, versioned, and stored on MeridianSafe with printed copies posted in each break room and near each elevator lobby.

#### 5.4.1 Emergency Types and Response

| Emergency Type | Initial Action | Signal |
|----------------|----------------|--------|
| **Fire / Smoke** | Immediately evacuate via nearest safe exit. Do not use elevators. Proceed to Assembly Point. | Continuous alarm tone + strobe lights. |
| **Medical Emergency** | Call site-specific emergency number (posted on Emergency Action Plan). Notify First Aid Officer. Do not move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger. | Verbal announcement. |
| **Active Threat / Violent Intruder** | Follow "Run, Hide, Fight" protocol. Lock/barricade doors, silence mobile devices, remain hidden until "All Clear" issued by ECO Lead or law enforcement. | Intercom announcement: "Code Silver — [Location]." MeridianSafe push notification. |
| **Severe Weather (Tornado/Severe Storm)** | Move to interior shelter area away from windows. Assume protective position. | Intercom announcement: "Code Black — Seek Shelter." |
| **Earthquake** | Drop, Cover, and Hold On. After shaking stops, evacuate building cautiously, avoiding hazards. | Natural warning; intercom follow-up if infrastructure intact. |
| **Hazardous Material Spill** | If trained and spill is minor, deploy spill kit and secure area. For major spills or unknown substances, evacuate affected zone. Notify Facilities immediately. | Verbal + intercom: "Code Orange — [Location]." |

#### 5.4.2 Assembly Points and Accounting

| Facility | Primary Assembly Point | Alternate Assembly Point |
|----------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Boston HQ | Fan Pier Park (grass area north of building) | District Hall lobby (weather contingency) |
| London | Finsbury Circus Gardens (center circle) | Bloomberg Arcade |
| Berlin | Monbijoupark (Mitte) | Local designated partner office (specified in ERP addendum) |
| Singapore | Raffles Place Park | Chulia Street covered walkway |
| Toronto | David Pecaut Square | Metro Hall main lobby |

**Roll Call Procedure:**

1. Floor Wardens evacuate their assigned zones, performing a sweep to ensure all personnel have exited.
2. At the Assembly Point, employees report to their Floor Warden.
3. Floor Warden conducts a headcount using the MeridianSafe mobile app "Emergency Roll Call" module, which displays the expected personnel list for that day derived from the access control system.
4. Floor Warden reports the count to the ECO Lead.
5. ECO Lead consolidates the site-wide status and reports any missing persons to emergency responders.
6. No personnel may re-enter the facility until the "All Clear" is officially given by the ECO Lead, Fire Marshal, or law enforcement commander on scene.

|------------|-----------|--------------|------------------|
| Fire Evacuation Drill | Annually (per site) | ECO Lead + VP Facilities | Yes — within 5 business days |

### 5.5 First Aid Provisions

Each Meridian facility shall maintain at least one ANSI Z308.1-2021 Class B (or local equivalent) first-aid kit, located in a clearly marked, accessible area near the main kitchen or break room. Facilities with more than 50 employees shall maintain an additional kit per 50-person increment.

The Regional Office Manager inspects each kit monthly, replenishing supplies via the approved medical supply vendor (MedSupply Global, vendor #V-0782). An automated restock alert is configured in MeridianSafe when inspection date passes without confirmation.

**First Aid Officers:** Each facility requires at minimum two trained First Aid Officers per occupied floor. Training is provided via an approved Red Cross (or equivalent) provider, with certification valid for two years. The People Operations Manager tracks certification expiration via the MeridianSafe Training Module, with auto-expiry alerts at 90 and 30 days.

### 5.6 Remote and Home Office Safety

Employees working remotely under SOP-HR-009 are subject to this WHS SOP. Key requirements:

- Employees shall submit photographs of their primary home workstation layout to the People Partner within the first 30 days of remote work enrollment, using the Home Office Self-Assessment Checklist (Form R-01).
- The People Partner reviews the submission and identifies potential hazards (e.g., trip hazards from cabling, inadequate lighting, non-ergonomic seating). Recommendations are documented in a report.
- Remote employees are responsible for maintaining a safe workspace. Meridian provides the ergonomic stipend and virtual consultation, but the employee retains responsibility for implementing recommendations.
- Remote employees must report any work-related injury within 4 hours via MeridianSafe, as per Section 5.2.2.

### 6.3 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

PPE is considered the lowest tier in the Hierarchy of Controls and is utilized when higher-order controls are infeasible or as a supplementary measure.

| Activity | Required PPE | Specification |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| R&D laboratory — chemical handling | Safety goggles, nitrile gloves, lab coat | ANSI Z87.1 (goggles); ASTM D6319 (gloves) |
| Server room / data center access | Anti-static wrist strap, safety-toe shoes (if racking equipment) | ANSI/ESD S20.20 |
| Facilities maintenance — electrical work | Arc-rated face shield, insulated gloves, arc flash suit (category per incident energy calc) | NFPA 70E |
| General office moving/stacking | Cut-resistant gloves (A4 rating), closed-toe shoes | ANSI/ISEA 105 |

PPE is provided at no cost to the employee. The Regional Office Manager maintains a PPE inventory, issuing items upon request or as identified by risk assessment.

### 6.4 Availability Controls

The MeridianSafe platform is designated as a critical safety system. The IT Operations team (see SOP-IT-105, Incident Management) maintains monitoring for the MeridianSafe application infrastructure. Availability commitments are defined per the IT Service Catalog; safety system restoration is prioritized within the incident management framework. In the event of an unplanned MeridianSafe outage, alternative reporting via direct supervisor notification and email to the Regional Office Manager is designated as the interim process. This continuity ensures that no incident report is delayed due to platform unavailability; manual tracking reconciliation is performed within two business days of service restoration.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Exception Requests

Deviations from the procedures prescribed in this SOP require a formal Exception Request (Form EX-WHS-001) submitted via ServiceNow, categorized under "WHS Exception." The request must include:

1. Specific SOP section or procedure from which deviation is sought.
2. Justification for the exception, including risk assessment of the alternative approach.
3. Scope (personnel, location, duration) of the exception.
4. Proposed compensatory controls if applicable.

### 8.2 Exception Approval Authority

| Exception Duration | Risk Level of Deviation | Approver |
|--------------------|-------------------------|----------|
| ≤ 30 days | Low or Moderate | VP Global Facilities |
| ≤ 30 days | High or Critical | CHRO |
| 31–180 days | Any | CHRO |
| > 180 days (or permanent) | Any | CHRO + CEO (concurrence) |

Approved exceptions are documented in the WHS Exception Register, maintained by the People Operations Manager, and reviewed quarterly during the WHS Governance Review. All exceptions have a defined expiry date; extensions require re-submission.

### 8.3 Escalation Pathways

The following escalation pathways apply when safety concerns are not addressed within the standard timeframes:

| Scenario | Level 1 (Immediate) | Level 2 (If unresolved after 48 hours) | Level 3 (If unresolved after 5 business days) |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Corrective action past due | Regional Office Manager | VP Global Facilities | CHRO |
| Incident investigation stalled | Investigation Lead | CHRO | CEO |
| Safety concern reported by employee with no response | Supervisor | Regional Office Manager | CHRO via confidential whistleblower line (ethic@meridianhealth.com) |
| Facility safety issue (e.g., non-functional alarm) | Regional Office Manager + Work Order submission | VP Global Facilities + CHRO | CEO + immediate shutdown of affected zone if life-safety risk |

**Urgent Life-Safety Issues:** Any employee who identifies an imminent danger has the authority to stop work, evacuate the affected area, and immediately notify the Regional Office Manager and Director of Security. No approvals are required prior to action in an imminent danger situation.

### 8.4 Dispute Resolution

In the event of a disagreement regarding incident classification, risk rating, or corrective action appropriateness, the matter is escalated to the CHRO. The CHRO may consult with an external safety professional or legal counsel as appropriate. The CHRO's determination is final and documented in the relevant MeridianSafe record.

### 9.2 Training Compliance Tracking

All training assignments are issued via Workday Learning. Completion is tracked by the People Operations Manager using the Training Compliance Dashboard.

- Email reminders are issued at assignment, at 7 days before due date, and at 3 days past due.
- Non-completion within 14 days past due triggers a notification to the employee's manager.
- Continued non-completion at 30 days past due results in temporary suspension of access to affected facilities or systems, as outlined in SOP-HR-003 (Discipline and Corrective Action).
- Training records are retained in Workday for the duration of employment plus seven (7) years.

### 9.3 Competency Verification

For practical skills-based training (First Aid, Floor Warden), competency is verified via practical assessment during the instructor-led session. Assessment checklists are retained by the instructor and uploaded to MeridianSafe. Any participant not meeting the competency standard is required to repeat the practical component within 15 days.
