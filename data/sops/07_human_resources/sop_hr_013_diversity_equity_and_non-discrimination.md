## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to codify Meridian Health Technologies, Inc.’s ("Meridian") commitment to fostering a diverse, equitable, and inclusive (DEI) workplace, to define the non-discrimination and anti-harassment policies governing all aspects of employment, and to establish the operational framework for accommodations, complaint intake, investigation, and resolution. This document operationalizes the Board-level directive established by the AI Governance Committee to ensure that the principles of fairness, transparency, and human dignity are not only embedded in our Clinical AI Platform and HealthPay Financial Services models but are equally reflected in our internal human capital practices. This SOP serves as the authoritative internal control document for ensuring compliance with applicable anti-discrimination laws across our global footprint.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies unconditionally to:
- All full-time, part-time, and temporary employees of Meridian Health Technologies, Inc., across all global offices (Boston HQ, London, Berlin, Singapore, Toronto) and remote workforce.
- All applicants for employment, interns, and independent contractors performing services on Meridian premises or through Meridian-managed digital infrastructure.
- All third-party vendors, staffing agencies, and external partners engaged in talent sourcing, screening, or management on behalf of Meridian.
- All workplace conduct occurring during business hours, at company-sponsored events, during business travel, and in any digital communication channels managed by Meridian, including Slack, Microsoft Teams, corporate email, and the Metaverse collaboration hub.

This policy applies to all employment actions including, but not limited to: recruitment, hiring, promotion, compensation, benefits, disciplinary actions, termination, training, social and recreational programs, and the use of company facilities.

## 2. Definitions and Acronyms

### 2.1 Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Diversity** | The representation of a range of human differences within the workforce, including but not limited to race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, age, social class, physical ability or attributes, religious or ethical values system, national origin, and political beliefs. |
| **Equity** | The guarantee of fair treatment, access, opportunity, and advancement for all individuals, while simultaneously identifying and eliminating barriers that have prevented the full participation of historically underrepresented groups. |
| **Inclusion** | The active, intentional, and ongoing engagement with diversity to create a culture where all employees feel welcomed, respected, supported, and valued to fully participate. |
| **Protected Characteristic** | Any trait legally protected from discrimination under applicable federal, state, or local laws, including race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, age (40 or older), disability, genetic information (including family medical history), veteran status, or any other class protected by governing law. |
| **Disability** | A physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities. This definition is interpreted broadly under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and equivalent EU directives. |
| **Reasonable Accommodation** | Any modification or adjustment to a job, the work environment, or the way things are usually done during the hiring process that enables a qualified individual with a disability to perform the essential functions of the job, or to enjoy equal benefits and privileges of employment, without causing undue hardship on the organization. |
| **Discriminatory AI Decision** | Any decision generated or materially influenced by an automated system (including those within the Clinical AI Platform or HealthPay Financial Services) that results in a less favorable outcome for an individual based on protected characteristics. |
| **Undue Hardship** | A significant difficulty or expense incurred by Meridian in providing an accommodation, assessed based on the nature and cost of the accommodation, the overall financial resources of the facility, the number of persons employed, and the impact on the operation of the facility. |
| **Adverse Action** | Any act that negatively impacts an employee's compensation, terms, conditions, or privileges of employment. |

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Non-Discrimination Policy
Meridian Health Technologies is committed to a work environment in which all individuals are treated with respect and dignity. Meridian strictly prohibits discrimination based on any protected characteristic. This policy applies to all terms, conditions, and privileges of employment, including but not limited to decisions regarding recruitment, hiring, placement, promotion, termination, layoff, recall, transfer, leaves of absence, compensation, and training. The company will not tolerate any form of unlawful discrimination, including harassment, by any manager, supervisor, co-worker, vendor, client, or customer.

### 4.2 Pay Equity Commitment
Meridian commits to equitable compensation practices. The CHRO shall direct an annual pay equity audit across all business lines and global offices. Compensation differentials exceeding 3% between groups performing Substantially Similar Work (defined by composite job architecture in Workday) that cannot be explained by legitimate, non-discriminatory factors (e.g., tenure, performance rating, geographic differential) shall be flagged for immediate remediation in the subsequent compensation review cycle.

### 4.3 Algorithmic Fairness
In alignment with the NIST AI RMF framework and the requirements for our risk-based approach, Meridian prohibits the use of any automated employment decision tools (AEDTs) or AI systems in hiring, promotion, or performance management that have not been assessed for discriminatory bias by the AI Governance Committee. All vendors providing AI-based HR tools must submit a Bias Audit report compliant with Meridian’s Vendor Security and AI Risk Assessment framework before implementation.

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Recruitment and Hiring Procedure

**5.1.1 Job Description Development**
Hiring managers must complete the *Meridian Job Requisition Form* (JRF-101) in Workday.
- **Step 1:** The manager drafts core competencies and essential functions, distinguishing between essential and marginal functions.
- **Step 2:** The text is screened through Textio's augmented writing platform to identify and remove potentially biased language (e.g., gender-coded words, ageist terminology).
- **Step 3:** A specific salary range, based on the Radford Global Compensation Database levels, is published internally and externally in compliance with the EU Pay Transparency Directive. The range must be approved by the Compensation Partner.

**5.1.2 Diverse Sourcing**
- Talent Acquisition Partners must post all requisitions to a minimum of 5 diverse job boards specific to the requisition's geography (e.g., DiversityJobs, Black Career Network, Pink Jobs, AbilityJobs). Proof of posting screenshots must be uploaded to the Workday requisition file.
- For all Director-level and above roles, the candidate slate must include at least two qualified candidates from underrepresented demographics before proceeding to the interview stage (Rooney Rule compliance).

**5.1.3 Structured Interviewing**
Interviews must follow a structured format using the standardized scorecard linked to the Workday req.
- **Panel Composition:** Interview panels for management roles must consist of at least three interviewers, ideally from diverse cross-functional teams.
- **Question Protocol:** Interviewers must use behavior-based questions from the *Meridian Structured Interview Question Bank (SIQB-2024)*. Ad-hoc questioning that deviates from evaluating the core competencies is prohibited.
- **Scoring:** Each interviewer independently scores the candidate in Workday within 24 hours of the interview. Scores are locked upon submission. Calibration meetings occur weekly within Talent Acquisition to identify disparate scoring patterns.

### 5.2 Reasonable Accommodation Procedure

This procedure governs requests for disabilities (physical/mental), pregnancy, childbirth, or sincerely held religious beliefs.

**Part A: General Process**
1.  **Initiation:** An employee or applicant submits a request via the *Accommodation Request Portal* in Workday, by emailing accommodations@meridianhealthtech.com, or verbally to any People Manager or HR Business Partner. Managers must immediately route verbal requests to HRBP.
2.  **Acknowledgement:** HR Operations acknowledges the request via Workday notification within **24 business hours**.
3.  **Interactive Process:** The assigned HR Business Partner schedules a confidential interactive process meeting with the employee within **5 business days**.
4.  **Medical Certification:** If the disability or need is not obvious, the HRBP requests the employee to have their treating provider complete the *Meridian Medical Inquiry Form (MIF-02)*. The form seeks strictly job-related information regarding functional limitations and accommodations to the essential functions. Employees have **15 calendar days** to return the form.
5.  **Evaluation:** The HRBP, in consultation with Legal and the Manager, evaluates the accommodation's effectiveness, feasibility, and whether it poses an undue hardship. The evaluation must consider:
    - Nature and cost of the accommodation.
    - Impact on facility operations.
    - Impact on other employees.
6.  **Decision Memo:** The HRBP drafts an *Accommodation Decision Memo* documenting the analysis and final decision. The decision must be rendered within **15 calendar days** of receiving complete medical documentation.
7.  **Implementation:** If approved, the Manager implements the accommodation immediately. The HRBP documents the accommodation type in Workday.
8.  **Denial & Appeal:** If denied, the employee is notified in writing specifying the reason (e.g., undue hardship, unable to perform essential function). The employee may appeal to the CHRO within **10 business days**. The CHRO or designee shall review and issue a final response within a reasonable timeframe, typically **15 business days**.

**Part B: Accommodations for EU Employees (GDPR Considerations)**
When processing medical data (MIF-02) for accommodations, the legal basis for processing under GDPR Article 9(2) is the necessity to carry out Meridian’s obligations and exercise specific rights in the field of employment law (Art. 9(2)(b) GDPR). The collected medical data constitutes special category data and is processed within the secure Vault functionality in Workday, strictly segregated from the employee’s general personnel file.

### 5.3 Complaint Reporting and Resolution Procedure

**5.3.1 Reporting Channels**
Meridian maintains a multi-channel reporting ecosystem:
- **Direct Manager:** Employees are encouraged to report concerns to their immediate manager (unless the manager is the subject).
- **HR Business Partner:** Contactable via Workday directory.
- **Integrity Helpline:** A 24/7 third-party operated service (Convercent by OneTrust) offering anonymous reporting via telephone and web portal. Available in 8 languages. Reports are assigned a unique case number for anonymous follow-up.
- **Email:** complaints@meridianhealthtech.com (routed directly to the Employee Relations team in HR).
- **AI Ombudsperson:** For concerns specifically relating to bias in AI-driven processes, employees may contact the AI Ombudsperson (ombuds-ai@meridianhealthtech.com), an independent function managed within the Chief AI Officer’s team, reporting administratively to the General Counsel.

**5.3.2 Intake and Triage**
1.  Upon receipt, all complaints are logged in the centralized Convercent case management system.
2.  The Employee Relations Director triages the case within **1 business day** to determine severity:
    - **Tier 1 (Critical):** Sexual assault, physical violence, stalking, or complaints against C-Suite executives. Escalated immediately to General Counsel and CHRO.
    - **Tier 2 (High):** Harassment, discrimination against a protected class, retaliation. Investigation launched within 48 hours.
    - **Tier 3 (Standard):** Incivility, microaggressions, non-harassing policy violations. Investigation launched within 1 week.

**5.3.3 Investigation Protocol**
1.  **Selection:** Investigators are selected from a vetted pool of internal (HR Employee Relations) or external resources. The investigator must be impartial and have no conflict of interest.
2.  **Notice of Investigation:** The Respondent and Complainant receive a formal *Notice of Investigation* document outlining the general nature of the allegations and their rights regarding representation, confidentiality, and the non-retaliation policy.
3.  **Evidence Collection:** The Investigator conducts individual interviews, collects digital evidence (Slack logs, email, badge reader data via Okta/CrowdStrike — with privacy safeguards), and reviews relevant personal records.
4.  **Temporal Scope:** Meridian’s standard retention for investigation records is the duration of employment plus 7 years. However, in practice, the specific response timelines for data subject access requests (DSARs) related to investigations are addressed on a case-by-case basis, with efforts made to respond as promptly as practical given the volume and complexity of the underlying records, which may occasionally extend beyond the standard 30-day window defined in Art. 12 GDPR.

**5.3.4 Determination and Remediation**
1.  **Report Writing:** The Investigator drafts a comprehensive report evaluating credibility and making findings based on the "Preponderance of the Evidence" standard.
2.  **Findings Letter:** Both parties are informed of the findings and any resulting actions (discipline, dismissal, no finding) relevant to them. Exact disciplinary measures against the other party are not disclosed due to privacy constraints.
3.  **Disciplinary Actions:** Range from verbal coaching and written warnings, to mandatory DEI training, to termination of employment. All terminations for cause require pre-approval by the CHRO and General Counsel.

### 5.4 AI Bias Incident Response

Given the EU AI Act classification of our Clinical AI Platform and the use of SR 11-7 governed models in HealthPay, specific procedures apply if an internal HR AI tool or an AI system used externally is found to produce discriminatory outcomes.
1.  **Stop-Loss Trigger:** If quantitative bias testing indicates an Adverse Impact Ratio (AIR) below 0.8 for any protected group in an automated HR selection tool, the tool's use must be paused immediately until remediation occurs.
2.  **Root Cause Analysis:** The VP of Clinical AI Products and the AI Governance Committee are convened within 5 business days. Technical documentation for the model, including training data provenance and feature importance weights, is reviewed. The AI Ombudsperson documents a Quality Assurance Log, noting any deviations from expected fairness metrics. The existing technical documentation generally covers the model architecture and risk tier but may not comprehensively catalog every specific item listed in Annex IV of the EU AI Act, such as a detailed description of the human oversight measures in the context of HR use cases.

## 7. Monitoring, Metrics, and Reporting

### 7.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The DEI Council and CHRO track the following quantitative metrics to assess the effectiveness of this SOP. The goal is not merely compliance but structural inclusion.

| KPI Category | Metric | Target | Reporting Frequency |
|--------------|--------|--------|---------------------|
| **Representation** | Overall Workforce Diversity Index (vs. Bureau of Labor Statistics/National Census benchmarks) | Within +/- 5% deviation | Quarterly |
| **Hiring** | Candidate Slate Diversity Rate (Underrepresented Minorities + Women) | > 60% for Dir+ roles | Monthly |
| **Advancement** | Promotion Rate Parity Index (PRPI) — ratio of promotion rates for under-rep groups vs. majority groups | Index > 0.95 | Semi-Annually |
| **Retention** | Voluntary Attrition Rate by demographic cohort | No cohort variance > 2.5% | Monthly |
| **Pay Equity** | Adjusted Pay Gap (controlled for role, tenure, location) | < 2.0% gap | Annually |
| **Response Time** | Average Time-to-Acknowledge Accommodation Request | < 24 hours | Monthly |
| **Response Time** | Average Investigation Cycle Time (Tier 1 & 2) | < 30 calendar days | Quarterly |
| **Sentiment** | Inclusion Index Score (Engagement Survey) | > 78% favorable | Annually |

### 7.2 Dashboards and Reporting
- **Executive DEI Dashboard:** Built in Tableau, feeds real-time data from Workday. Visible to C-Suite and Board of Directors.
- **EEO-1 Component Filing:** Submitted annually by General Counsel and CCO to the EEOC. A parallel Gender Pay Gap analysis is filed for the UK Gender Pay Gap Regulations.
- **EU AI Act Transparency Report:** Specific metrics regarding fairness of HR-related automated decision-making are compiled by the DPO and Chief AI Officer and submitted to the AI GC and, where required, notified to relevant authorities.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Exception Requests
Any request for deviation from the procedures in SOP-HR-013 (e.g., proceeding with a hire without a diverse slate, bypassing the structured interview scorecard) must be documented using the *Policy Exception Request Form (PERF-001)*.

- **Manager Authorization:** VP-Level approval required.
- **DEI Review:** The form is reviewed by the CHRO or their designee to ensure the exception does not establish a pattern of systemic non-compliance.
- **GDPR/Privacy Exception:** Any exception involving the disclosure of investigation data to third parties or transfer of sensitive employee data outside the EU must additionally be approved by the Data Protection Officer, Dr. Klaus Weber.

3.  **90 days overdue:** Subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.
