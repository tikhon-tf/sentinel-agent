# Standard Operating Procedure: Customer Access to AI Explanations

## SOP-COPS-015 | Version 3.1

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the operational framework, processes, and controls governing the provision of AI system explanations to customers and authorized end-users of Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. (“Meridian”) products and services. This SOP codifies Meridian’s commitment to algorithmic transparency, human oversight, and the right to meaningful explanation as mandated by the EU AI Act, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF).

The specific objectives of this SOP are to:

- Define standardized procedures for receiving, validating, processing, and fulfilling customer requests for AI-generated outputs and decisions.
- Establish a tiered explanation framework calibrated to the risk classification of the AI system involved, the nature of the decision, and the regulatory requirements of the jurisdiction.
- Operationalize Articles 13, 14, and 86 of the EU AI Act regarding transparency and the right to explanation for high-risk AI systems.
- Fulfill Meridian’s obligations under GDPR Articles 13–15, 22, and 35 regarding automated decision-making, profiling, and data subject access rights.
- Operationalize the NIST AI RMF Core functions—particularly MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE—by establishing repeatable processes for explanation generation, quality assurance, and governance.
- Ensure consistent, auditable, and defensible responses to all explanation requests across Meridian’s global operations.

### 1.2 Scope

#### 1.2.1 In-Scope Products and Systems

This SOP applies to all Meridian products and services that incorporate automated decision-making, profiling, or AI-generated outputs delivered to external customers, patients, or covered entities. The specific systems covered are:

| Product Line | AI Systems Covered | Risk Classification | Regulatory Framework |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clinical AI Platform | Clinical decision support tools, diagnostic imaging analysis (FDA 510(k) cleared, CE marked), patient risk scoring, adverse event prediction | High-Risk AI System (EU AI Act Annex III, Point 11) | EU AI Act, HIPAA, GDPR, NIST AI RMF |
| HealthPay Financial Services | Credit scoring models, fraud detection algorithms, claims adjudication engines, medical lending eligibility | SR 11-7 Model Risk Tier 1/2 | SR 11-7, SOC 2, GDPR Art. 22 |
| MedInsight Analytics | Care gap identification models, population risk stratification, outcomes prediction | Medium-Risk Analytics | HIPAA, GDPR, NIST AI RMF |
| Meridian SaaS Platform | Supporting infrastructure for all AI model serving | N/A (Infrastructure) | SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001 |

#### 1.2.2 In-Scope Request Types

This SOP covers the following categories of explanation requests:

- **Clinical AI Explanation Requests:** Explanations of diagnostic findings, risk scores, treatment recommendations, or adverse event predictions generated by the Clinical AI Platform.
- **Financial Decision Explanation Requests:** Explanations of credit decisions, loan approvals or denials, financing terms, or claims adjudication outcomes produced by HealthPay Financial Services models.
- **Analytics Explanation Requests:** Explanations of population health insights, care gap identifications, or outcome predictions that materially affect patient care or organizational decision-making.
- **Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs) with AI Components:** Requests from individuals exercising GDPR or HIPAA rights concerning automated processing of their personal data.

#### 1.2.3 Applicability

This SOP applies to:

- All Customer Operations personnel globally, including Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 support teams in Boston, London, Berlin, Singapore, and Toronto.
- Data Science and Machine Learning Engineering teams supporting explanation generation.
- Clinical AI Products team members responsible for clinical validation of explanations.
- Compliance and Legal teams responsible for regulatory review of explanations.
- Any Meridian employee or contractor who receives, processes, or escalates an AI explanation request from a customer or end-user.

#### 1.2.4 Exclusions

This SOP does not apply to:

- Internal model development, testing, or debugging activities not initiated by a customer request (see SOP-MLOPS-008: Model Development Lifecycle).
- Explanations provided to regulators or auditors as part of formal examinations (see SOP-COMP-022: Regulatory Examination Management).
- Informal inquiries or product questions that do not reference a specific AI-generated output or decision.

## 2. Definitions and Acronyms

### 2.1 Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **AI Explanation** | Information provided to a customer or data subject describing how an AI system arrived at a particular output, decision, or recommendation, presented in a meaningful and comprehensible format. |
| **Automated Decision-Making** | Decisions made solely by automated means without meaningful human intervention, producing legal effects or similarly significant effects on individuals (GDPR Art. 22). |
| **Contestability** | The ability of an affected individual to challenge an AI-generated decision and seek human review and, where appropriate, correction or reversal. |
| **Counterfactual Explanation** | An explanation format that describes the minimal changes to input features that would result in a different outcome (e.g., "If your income-to-debt ratio were 5% lower, you would qualify for Tier 1 financing"). |
| **Customer** | A healthcare provider, payer organization, or other entity that has entered into a service agreement with Meridian for the use of its products. |
| **End-User** | A natural person who ultimately uses or is subject to the outputs of Meridian's AI systems, including clinicians, patients, and financial applicants. |
| **Explanation Completeness Score (ECS)** | A quantitative metric (0–100) rating the comprehensiveness of an AI explanation against predefined criteria (see Section 7.2). |
| **Feature Attribution** | A method identifying which input features contributed most significantly to a given AI output (e.g., SHAP values, LIME, Integrated Gradients). |
| **High-Risk AI System** | As defined by EU AI Act Annex III, an AI system intended to be used for diagnosis, prognosis, or treatment decisions in healthcare settings. |
| **Human Oversight** | The ability of a qualified human to interpret, question, and, where appropriate, override AI system outputs (EU AI Act Art. 14). |
| **Meaningful Explanation** | An explanation that enables the recipient to understand the logic, factors, and reasoning behind a specific AI output sufficiently to exercise their rights, make informed decisions, or contest the outcome. |
| **Model Card** | A structured transparency document describing an AI model's intended use, performance characteristics, training data, limitations, and ethical considerations. |
| **Profiling** | Any automated processing of personal data to evaluate certain personal aspects relating to a natural person (GDPR Art. 4(4)). |
| **SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations)** | A game-theoretic approach to explaining the output of machine learning models, used as Meridian's primary technique for feature attribution. |
| **Tier 1/2/3 Support** | Meridian's Customer Operations escalation hierarchy: Tier 1 (initial contact), Tier 2 (technical specialists), Tier 3 (data science and engineering). |

## 4. Policy Statements

Meridian Health Technologies maintains the following policy commitments regarding customer access to AI explanations:

### 4.1 Right to Explanation

**POL-COPS-015.1:** All customers and authorized end-users shall have the right to request and receive a meaningful explanation of any AI-generated output, decision, or recommendation produced by a Meridian system that materially affects patient care, financial eligibility, or similar significant interests. This right shall not be waived, restricted, or conditioned except as expressly permitted by law and approved by the Chief AI Officer in consultation with the DPO.

### 4.2 Tiered Explanation Framework

**POL-COPS-015.2:** Meridian shall maintain a three-tier explanation framework (Standard, Enhanced, Comprehensive) calibrated to the risk classification of the AI system, the significance of the decision, and the regulatory requirements applicable to the requestor. Each tier shall have defined content requirements, generation procedures, and delivery timelines as specified in Section 5.

### 4.3 Transparency by Design

**POL-COPS-015.3:** All AI systems subject to this SOP shall be designed and maintained to support explanation generation throughout the model lifecycle. No high-risk AI system shall be deployed without documented explanation capabilities, validated during model acceptance testing per SOP-MLOPS-008. The explanation capability assessment shall be included in the Model Card and reviewed during Model Risk Committee meetings.

### 4.4 Human Oversight and Contestability

**POL-COPS-015.4:** Every AI-generated decision subject to this SOP shall be contestable by the affected individual. All explanations shall include clear, accessible instructions for escalating to human review. The human review process shall be meaningful—the reviewing human must have the authority, competence, and information necessary to confirm, modify, or overturn the AI-generated decision. No high-risk decision shall become final during any pending explanation or contestation period.

### 4.5 Prohibition of Explanatory Deflection

**POL-COPS-015.5:** Under no circumstances shall any Meridian employee: (a) deflect an explanation request solely on the grounds of model complexity or "black box" nature; (b) provide an intentionally misleading, incomplete, or vague explanation; or (c) refuse an explanation request that meets the intake criteria defined in this SOP. Any inability to generate a compliant explanation shall be escalated immediately to the Chief AI Officer and DPO.

### 4.6 Non-Discrimination in Explanation Access

**POL-COPS-015.6:** Explanation requests shall be processed without discrimination based on the requestor's geographic location, organization size, contract tier, or protected characteristics. SLA tiers may differ based on the technical complexity of explanation required, not the identity of the requestor.

### 4.7 Recordkeeping

**POL-COPS-015.7:** All explanation requests, responses, and related communications shall be logged, timestamped, and retained in Meridian's ticketing system (ServiceNow) for a minimum of seven (7) years, or longer if required by applicable statute of limitations or regulatory retention schedule. Each record shall contain sufficient detail to reconstruct the explanation provided and demonstrate compliance with this SOP.

### 4.8 Continuous Improvement

**POL-COPS-015.8:** Meridian shall systematically collect metrics on explanation quality, timeliness, and customer satisfaction, using these data to drive continuous improvement of explanation processes, tools, and training. The MANAGE function of the NIST AI RMF shall be operationalized through semi-annual reviews of explanation effectiveness by the AI Governance Committee.

| Field | Description | Valid Values / Example |
|---|---|---|

#### 5.1.2 Identity Verification and Authorization

Before processing any explanation request, Tier 1 Support shall verify:

1. **Customer/End-User Identity:** Confirm the requestor's identity against Meridian's customer records. For patient end-users, verify identity through the covered entity customer's patient portal or through Meridian's identity verification service (PingOne).
2. **Authorization to Explanation:** Confirm the requestor is authorized to receive the explanation:
   - For clinical explanations: the requestor must be the ordering clinician, a member of the care team, or the patient to whom the output applies.
   - For financial explanations: the requestor must be the financial applicant or their authorized representative.
   - For analytics explanations: the requestor must be an authorized user of the subscribing customer organization.
3. **Data Sharing Agreement Verification:** Confirm that any data sharing agreement (DSA), business associate agreement (BAA), or service agreement covering the requestor permits the provision of the requested explanation. Check the Meridian Contract Management System (Agiloft).

If identity verification fails, the requestor shall be notified and provided with instructions to establish or verify their identity. The ticket shall remain in `Pending Verification` status for no more than **3 business days** before automatic closure.

#### 5.1.3 Classification and Tier Assignment

Tier 1 Support shall classify each explanation request according to the following taxonomy and assign the appropriate explanation tier:

| Explanation Tier | Criteria | Example Scenarios | Responsible Team | Generation Method |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard (Tier 1)** | Low-risk model; routine decision; clear feature attribution available; customer needs basic understanding | - Basic question about a patient risk score<br>- Feature summary for a claims decision<br>- Standard explanation of a population health insight | Tier 1 Support | Automated via Meridian Explanation Portal |
| **Enhanced (Tier 2)** | Medium/high-risk model; significant decision; feature attribution requires interpretation; customer needs detailed understanding | - Clinician wants to understand why CDS flagged a case<br>- Applicant questions a financing denial<br>- DSAR with automated decision-making components | Tier 2 Support | Semi-automated using Meridian Explainability Toolkit |
| **Comprehensive (Tier 3)** | High-risk model; contested decision; regulatory or legal implication; counterfactual explanation required | - Patient contests a diagnosis recommendation<br>- DSAR requiring full Article 22 explanation<br>- Pre-litigation explanation request<br>- Regulator-specific request | Data Science + Compliance/Legal | Manual analysis with custom reporting |

If the requestor does not specify a tier, Tier 1 shall assign the tier based on the criteria above and note the rationale in the ticket.

### 5.2 Standard (Tier 1) Explanation Procedure

#### 5.2.1 Automated Explanation Generation

Standard explanation requests shall be fulfilled using the **Meridian Explanation Portal**, an internal application integrated with the Meridian Model Registry and Feature Store. The Tier 1 agent shall:

1. **Access the Explanation Portal** at `https://explanations.internal.meridianhealth.com` using their SSO credentials.
2. **Enter the Decision ID** provided by the customer or retrieved from system logs. The portal queries the model inference database (MLflow Tracking Server) and retrieves:
   - Model name, version, and deployment date.
   - Input features (with PHI masking applied automatically).
   - Pre-computed SHAP values generated at inference time.
   - Model confidence scores.
   - Decision output and any decision thresholds applied.
3. **Select the Explanation Template** appropriate to the product and customer type:
   - `CLINICAL-STANDARD`: For clinical CDS outputs.
   - `FINANCIAL-STANDARD`: For HealthPay decisions.
   - `ANALYTICS-STANDARD`: For MedInsight outputs.
4. **Generate the Explanation Document** by clicking "Generate." The portal produces a PDF (and optional HTML) containing the following sections:
   - **Summary:** Plain-language summary of the decision (2–3 sentences).
   - **Top Contributing Factors:** Top 5 input features ranked by SHAP importance, with directionality (increased/decreased risk, score, or likelihood).
   - **Model Information:** Model name, version, intended use, and general limitations (from the Model Card).
   - **Confidence Information:** Model confidence score and calibration information.
   - **Human Oversight Notice:** Standard text advising that the AI output is a decision support tool, not a clinical or financial determination, and providing instructions for human review.
   - **Contestability Instructions:** How to request a higher-tier explanation or human review.

5. **Review for Completeness:** Tier 1 shall visually review the generated document for obvious errors, formatting issues, or PHI exposure. If issues are detected, escalate to Tier 2.

#### 5.2.2 Delivery to Customer

The Standard explanation document shall be delivered to the requestor using the same channel through which the request was received, unless the requestor specifies otherwise:

- **Portal requests:** Document attached to the ServiceNow ticket; requestor notified via portal message.
- **Email requests:** PDF delivered via secure email (Mimecast Secure Messaging) with read receipt.
- **Phone requests:** Tier 1 walks the requestor through the explanation verbally, then sends the PDF via email or portal.

#### 5.2.3 SLA Requirements

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Initial acknowledgment | Within 1 business hour |
| Explanation generated | Within 4 business hours |
| Explanation delivered | Within 6 business hours |
| Ticket closure (after delivery confirmation) | Within 8 business hours |

### 5.3 Enhanced (Tier 2) Explanation Procedure

#### 5.3.1 Escalation and Acknowledgment

Upon classification as Enhanced, the Tier 1 agent shall:

1. **Reassign the ServiceNow ticket** to the `CUSTOPS-TIER2` assignment group with priority `P2 – High`.
2. **Add internal notes** summarizing: the specific explanation requested, any clinical or financial context provided by the requestor, and any jurisdictional flags (e.g., "EU Data Subject – GDPR Art. 22 applies").
3. **Send acknowledgment to the requestor** within **2 business hours** of the initial request, including:
   - Confirmation the explanation is being processed.
   - Estimated delivery timeline: **3–5 business days**.
   - Point of contact for follow-up inquiries.

#### 5.3.2 Explanation Generation Workflow

The Tier 2 agent assigned to the ticket shall:

1. **Retrieve the full inference record** from the Meridian Model Registry (MLflow) and Feature Store (Feast):
   - Complete input feature vector (PHI-masked).
   - Full SHAP or LIME explanation output.
   - Model prediction probability distribution.
   - Model version, training data characteristics, and validation metrics.
   - Any applicable fairness metrics from the model card.

2. **Interpret the Model Output** using the Meridian Explainability Toolkit. This involves:
   - Reviewing feature attribution values and ranking contributing factors.
   - Identifying any unexpected feature contributions or potential model biases.
   - Comparing the specific case to the model's overall performance distribution (e.g., "This case falls in the 92nd percentile of the model's confidence distribution").
   - For clinical explanations: consulting with Clinical AI Products if the explanation involves diagnostic implications.

3. **Contextualize the Explanation** for the specific requestor:
   - If the requestor is a clinician: frame the explanation in clinical terms, referencing relevant guidelines or evidence.
   - If the requestor is a patient: use plain language appropriate for health literacy standards (≤8th grade reading level using Flesch-Kincaid assessment).
   - If the requestor is a data subject under GDPR: include all Article 13–15 disclosures.

4. **Prepare the Enhanced Explanation Document** using the `ENHANCED-EXPLANATION-TEMPLATE` from the Meridian Document Library. The document shall contain all Standard tier content plus:
   - **Detailed Feature Analysis:** Expanded explanation of top contributing features with interaction effects identified.
   - **Confidence Calibration Analysis:** How this particular prediction relates to the model's overall calibration curve.
   - **Population Context:** Where applicable, how the individual's case compares to relevant population benchmarks (with appropriate caveats against ecological fallacy).
   - **Limitations and Uncertainties:** Model limitations specific to the requestor's context.
   - **Enhanced Human Oversight:** Detailed instructions for human review, including the name and contact of the Clinical AI Products reviewer if clinical.

5. **Quality Assurance Review (QAR):** Before delivery, the Enhanced explanation must undergo QAR:
   - **All Enhanced explanations:** Peer review by a second Tier 2 agent.
   - **Clinical explanations:** Review by a Clinical AI Products team member (Dr. Sarah Okonkwo or designate).
   - **Financial explanations:** Review by HealthPay Risk team member.
   - **EU/UK jurisdiction explanations:** Review by Compliance team (Attorney Anna Lindström or designate) for regulatory compliance.
   - QAR shall be documented in the ServiceNow ticket with reviewer name, date/time, and outcome (Approved / Approved with Edits / Rejected with Revisions).

#### 5.3.3 Delivery and Follow-Up

The Enhanced explanation shall be delivered via secure portal upload or encrypted email (as specified by the requestor) with delivery confirmation required. The Tier 2 agent shall:

1. **Schedule a follow-up call** (optional, at requestor's election) within **2 business days** of delivery to answer questions.
2. **Document delivery** in the ServiceNow ticket, including delivery timestamp, method, and any immediate feedback received.
3. **Flag the ticket** for the Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) survey process (Section 7.4).

#### 5.3.4 SLA Requirements

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 → Tier 2 escalation | Within 1 business hour of classification |
| Acknowledgment to requestor | Within 2 business hours of escalation |
| Enhanced explanation generated | Within 3 business days (72 business hours) |
| QAR completed | Within 4 business hours of explanation draft |
| Enhanced explanation delivered | Within 5 business days (120 business hours) |
| Follow-up call completed | Within 2 business days of delivery |

### 5.4 Comprehensive (Tier 3) Explanation Procedure

#### 5.4.1 Escalation Criteria and Triage

Comprehensive explanation escalation is mandatory when any of the following conditions are met:

| Escalation Trigger | Example |
|---|---|
| The request contests an AI decision with legal or significant practical effect | Patient formally disputes a denial of care recommendation |
| The request explicitly invokes GDPR Article 22 rights | "I am exercising my right under Article 22 GDPR not to be subject to solely automated decision-making..." |
| The request involves a pre-litigation or litigation posture | Attorney letter requesting "all information related to automated decision X" |
| The explanation requires manual counterfactual generation | "What would need to be different for the decision to change?" |
| The Tier 2 agent determines that the Enhanced tier cannot adequately address the request | Model output is genuinely difficult to explain with standard tools |
| Regulatory authority request | Formal inquiry from an EU Data Protection Authority or US agency |

Upon determination that Comprehensive tier applies:

1. **Tier 2 shall immediately escalate** (within 30 minutes) to the `CUSTOPS-TIER3` assignment group, copying the Compliance team (`COMPLIANCE-TEAM`) and, for clinical matters, the Clinical AI Products lead.
2. **The DPO shall be notified** (automated ServiceNow notification rule) for any GDPR-related Comprehensive requests.
3. **The Chief AI Officer shall be informed** (ServiceNow notification) for any high-visibility or regulatory requests.
4. **A kickoff meeting** (30-minute video conference) shall be convened within **1 business day** including: assigned Tier 3 Data Scientist, Compliance representative, Clinical representative (if applicable), and the VP of Customer Operations or delegate.

#### 5.4.2 Manual Explanation Generation

The Tier 3 Data Scientist shall lead the manual explanation generation, which shall include:

1. **Full Model Audit Trail Retrieval:**
   - Complete inference record from MLflow.
   - All pre- and post-processing transformations applied to input data.
   - Full SHAP explanation (local and global context).
   - Any A/B testing or shadow deployment data related to the decision.
   - Model version history and known issues from the Model Registry issue tracker.

2. **Counterfactual Explanation Generation:**
   - Identify the minimal set of input feature changes that would result in a different decision outcome.
   - Use Meridian's Counterfactual Generator (based on DiCE framework, customized for healthcare applications).
   - For each counterfactual scenario, assess plausibility and actionability (e.g., "If the patient's WBC count were 11.2 instead of 11.8, the sepsis risk score would fall below the alert threshold").
   - Present 3–5 actionable counterfactuals where possible, or document why counterfactuals are not actionable.

3. **Model Fairness and Bias Analysis:**
   - Assess the specific decision for potential disparate impact using Meridian's Fairness Assessment Toolkit.
   - Compare the decision outcome across relevant demographic or protected characteristics.
   - Document any fairness concerns found (or confirm none were found).

4. **Clinical or Financial Validation (as applicable):**
   - **Clinical:** The Clinical AI Products team shall validate the clinical reasoning reflected in the explanation against current clinical guidelines and standard of care.
   - **Financial:** The HealthPay Risk team shall validate the financial logic against SR 11-7 model risk standards and applicable fair lending regulations.

5. **Regulatory Review:**
   - The Compliance team and DPO (if GDPR-applicable) shall review the complete explanation for regulatory compliance before delivery.
   - Specific regulatory review points:
     - GDPR Art. 22(3): Confirmation that adequate safeguards are in place and documented.
     - GDPR Recital 71: Appropriate mathematical/statistical procedures are described.
     - EU AI Act Art. 86: Explanation adequately addresses the individual's right to explanation.
     - EU AI Act Art. 14: Human oversight has been meaningfully exercised.

#### 5.4.3 The Comprehensive Explanation Document

The Comprehensive explanation package shall include:

| Section | Content | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| Executive Summary | Plain-language summary of decision and explanation | Tier 3 Data Scientist |
| Full Feature Analysis | Complete feature attribution with effect magnitudes and directions | Tier 3 Data Scientist |
| Model Context | Model card extract, training data demographics, performance metrics | Tier 3 Data Scientist |
| Counterfactual Analysis | Actionable counterfactuals with plausibility assessment | Tier 3 Data Scientist |
| Fairness Assessment | Bias analysis results for this decision and comparative context | Tier 3 Data Scientist |
| Clinical/Financial Validation | Expert validation of reasoning and recommendations | Clinical / HealthPay teams |
| Regulatory Disclosures | GDPR/EU AI Act required disclosures, rights statements | Compliance / DPO |

#### 5.4.4 Delivery

1. A formal cover letter from the VP of Customer Operations or designate.
2. The complete explanation package.
3. Instructions for further contestation or escalation, including the contact information for Meridian's DPO (for EU requests) or Chief Privacy Officer (for US requests).
4. An offer for a 60-minute explanatory call within **3 business days** of delivery.

### 5.5 DSAR Processing with AI Components

When an AI explanation request arrives as part of a formal GDPR Data Subject Access Request, the following additional procedures apply:

1. **DSAR Intake:** All DSARs are received through the OneTrust DSAR Portal, managed by the Privacy Office. The Privacy Analyst shall identify any AI-related components and create a linked ServiceNow ticket with category `AI-EXPLANATION-DSAR`.

2. **Article 15 Review:** The DPO or Privacy Counsel shall determine whether the DSAR triggers GDPR Article 15(1)(h) obligations—specifically, the right to "meaningful information about the logic involved, as well as the significance and the envisaged consequences of such processing for the data subject."

3. **Article 22 Compliance:** If the AI system performs solely automated decision-making producing legal effects or similarly significant effects, the DPO shall ensure:
   - The data subject is informed of the existence of automated decision-making.
   - The logic, significance, and consequences are explained meaningfully.
   - The data subject is informed of their right to human intervention, to express their point of view, and to contest the decision (GDPR Art. 22(3)).

4. **Timeline Compliance:** DSAR response timelines under GDPR (30 calendar days, extendable to 60) shall take precedence over this SOP's standard SLA timelines. The AI explanation component must be completed in sufficient time for inclusion in the DSAR response.

5. **Documentation:** All DSAR AI explanations shall be retained with the DSAR record in OneTrust (retention: 7 years).

### 6.2 Data Protection Safeguards for Explanations

1. **PHI Masking by Default:** All explanation generation shall apply automated PHI masking via Meridian's Data Masking Service (Google Cloud DLP API integration) before any human review. Masking covers:
   - Patient names and identifiers (replaced with `[PATIENT-ID-XXXX]`)
   - Dates of birth, admission, and procedure (shifted by randomized offset ±30 days)
   - Geographic identifiers smaller than state/province level
   - Any direct identifiers as defined by HIPAA Safe Harbor method

2. **Unmasking for Authorized Recipients:** Where the explanation requires unmasked PHI for meaningful delivery (e.g., patient requesting their own explanation, clinician reviewing their own patient), the break-glass procedure shall be invoked:
   - Dual approval required: Manager of requesting team + Privacy Officer (US) or DPO (EU).
   - Approval documented in ServiceNow with business justification.
   - All unmasked access logged in Meridian's SIEM (Splunk) with alerting for anomalous patterns.
   - Unmasked documents watermarked with recipient identity and "CONFIDENTIAL – DO NOT FORWARD."

3. **GDPR Article 35 Compliance:** For any new AI system or significant change to explanation capabilities that involves processing of personal data, a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) shall be completed, approved by the DPO, and filed in the OneTrust DPIA Register before the system or change is placed into production.

### 6.3 Explanation Quality Assurance Controls

1. **Automated Completeness Check:** The Explanation Portal shall automatically calculate an Explanation Completeness Score (ECS) for each generated explanation:

   | ECS Component | Weight | Measurement |
   |---|---|---|
   | Feature attribution present | 20% | Boolean: Are top-5 SHAP features listed? |
   | Confidence information present | 15% | Boolean: Is confidence score and calibration info included? |
   | Model information present | 10% | Boolean: Model name, version, intended use? |
   | Limitations stated | 15% | Boolean: Are known model limitations included? |
   | Contestability instructions present | 20% | Boolean: Is human review process described? |
   | Language appropriateness | 20% | Automated readability score check against target level |

   Explanations with ECS < 85/100 shall be blocked from delivery and flagged for Tier 2 review.

2. **Peer Review Logging:** All QARs shall be logged in ServiceNow with reviewer identity, timestamp, and outcome. No Enhanced or Comprehensive explanation shall be delivered without a completed QAR record.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Standard Exception Types

| Exception Type | Description | Approval Authority | Maximum Extension | Documentation Required |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **SLA Extension – Volume** | Unusually high volume of requests exceeding tier capacity | VP Customer Ops | +5 business days | Volume data, staffing plan, customer notification |
| **SLA Extension – Complexity** | Particularly complex model requiring extended analysis | Chief AI Officer (or delegate) | +10 business days | Technical justification, interim customer update schedule |
| **SLA Extension – Regulatory** | Pending regulatory guidance or legal review | Chief AI Officer + DPO | Case-by-case | Regulatory context, legal hold documentation |
| **Partial Explanation** | Full explanation not technically feasible due to model limitations | Chief AI Officer | N/A (partial delivered, with documented limitations) | Technical analysis of limitations; mitigation plan |
| **Expedited Processing** | Urgent clinical or financial need requiring faster than standard SLA | VP Customer Ops or delegate (Director-level or above) | Expedited SLA: Tier 1 = 2 hours; Tier 2 = 1 day; Tier 3 = 3 days | Urgency justification, customer notification |

### 8.2 Exception Approval Process

1. **Request Initiation:** Any Meridian employee may identify the need for an exception. The request shall be documented in the ServiceNow ticket with the `Exception Requested` flag set to `true` and the specific exception type selected.

2. **Approval Routing:** ServiceNow shall automatically route the exception request to the appropriate approval authority based on the exception type, with notification to the ticket assignee's manager.

3. **Approval Decision:** The approval authority shall review and approve or deny the exception within **4 business hours** (for expedited) or **1 business day** (for all others). The decision shall be documented in the ServiceNow ticket with rationale.

4. **Customer Notification:** Upon exception approval, the ticket assignee shall notify the customer:
   - Nature of the exception.
   - Revised timeline.
   - Reason (in general terms appropriate for customer communication).
   - Point of escalation if the customer is not satisfied with the resolution.

5. **Escalation of Denied Exceptions:** If an exception is denied, the requestor may escalate to the next management level:
   - VP Customer Ops denial → Chief AI Officer.
   - Chief AI Officer denial → CEO (for material customer or regulatory matters only).

### 8.3 Technical Inability to Explain

In the rare case that a particular AI output cannot be meaningfully explained despite escalation through all tiers (e.g., genuinely opaque ensemble model producing an outlier prediction that defies available interpretability methods), the following mandatory procedure applies:

1. **Immediate Notification:** The Tier 3 Data Scientist shall notify:
   - Chief AI Officer (within 4 hours).
   - VP Customer Ops (within 4 hours).
   - DPO (for EU/UK data subjects, within 4 hours).

2. **Interim Customer Communication:** The VP Customer Ops shall ensure the customer receives an interim response within **24 hours** acknowledging the difficulty and providing:
   - A plain-language statement of what is and is not currently explainable.
   - An offer of human review and override of the AI decision.
   - A timeline for further analysis (not to exceed 15 business days).

3. **Root Cause Analysis:** The Chief AI Officer shall commission a root cause analysis to determine:
   - Why the particular output was not explainable.
   - Whether this indicates a systemic issue with the model or interpretability tooling.
   - What remediation is required (model retraining, tooling upgrade, model replacement, etc.).

4. **Model Risk Committee:** The incident shall be reported to the Model Risk Committee at its next scheduled meeting, with recommended remediation actions.
