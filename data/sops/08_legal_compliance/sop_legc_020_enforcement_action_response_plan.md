# 1. Purpose and Scope

## 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the framework for Meridian Global Technologies’ coordinated response to any enforcement action, regulatory inquiry, or formal investigation initiated by a federal, state, or international regulatory authority. The Enforcement Action Response Plan (EARP) ensures that all enforcement matters are handled with appropriate urgency, legal rigor, and cross-functional alignment to minimize legal, financial, and reputational exposure.

The specific objectives of this SOP are to:

1. Establish a clear classification schema for enforcement actions based on severity, regulatory authority, and potential organizational impact.
2. Define a standing response team structure with clearly delineated roles, decision rights, and escalation paths.
3. Provide standardized communication protocols that preserve attorney-client privilege while enabling operational coordination.
4. Mandate a structured remediation framework with defined timelines, deliverable tracking, and validation mechanisms.
5. Institutionalize a post-resolution lessons-learned process to strengthen the compliance program and reduce recurrence risk.

## 1.2 Scope

### 1.2.1 Inclusions

This SOP applies to all enforcement actions, regulatory inquiries, and formal investigations directed at Meridian Global Technologies, its subsidiaries, or any of its operational divisions by the following categories of regulatory authorities:

| Regulatory Category | Examples (Non-Exhaustive) |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Data Protection & Privacy Regulators | Federal Trade Commission, State Attorneys General, European Data Protection Authorities |
| Financial Services Regulators | Office of the Comptroller of the Currency, Federal Reserve, Consumer Financial Protection Bureau |
| Healthcare Regulators | Office for Civil Rights, Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services |
| AI & Technology Regulators | European AI Office, National AI Advisory Bodies |
| Securities Regulators | Securities and Exchange Commission, Financial Industry Regulatory Authority |
| Cross-Border Authorities | European Commission, International Data Transfer Bodies |

### 1.2.2 Activities Covered

This SOP applies to the full lifecycle of enforcement action response, including:

- Initial receipt and triage of regulatory correspondence, civil investigative demands, subpoenas, and notices of proposed action
- Classification and impact assessment
- Formation and mobilization of the response team
- Internal investigation and fact-finding
- External communications and regulator engagement
- Remediation planning, execution, and verification
- Settlement negotiation and consent decree compliance
- Post-resolution monitoring and closure reporting

### 1.2.3 Affected Personnel

All employees, contractors, temporary staff, and third-party partners of Meridian Global Technologies who may be involved in or impacted by an enforcement action response are subject to this SOP. Specific applicability extends to:

- Legal & Compliance Department personnel
- Executive Leadership Team members
- Business Unit heads and designated compliance liaisons
- Information Security & Data Governance teams
- Internal Audit and Risk Management functions
- Corporate Communications and Public Relations
- Human Resources (for personnel-related actions)

### 1.2.4 Geographic Applicability

This SOP applies globally across all Meridian operating jurisdictions. Response procedures shall be adapted to jurisdiction-specific regulatory frameworks as documented in the Regulatory Interaction Matrix maintained by the Legal & Compliance Department.

# 4. Policy Statements

## 4.1 General Response Policy

Meridian Global Technologies shall respond to all enforcement actions, regulatory inquiries, and formal investigations in a timely, thorough, and professionally appropriate manner. The organization commits to:

1. **Full Cooperation:** Cooperate with authorized regulatory authorities while protecting the organization's legal rights, including applicable privileges and protections. All regulator communications shall be truthful, accurate, and consistent with documented facts.

2. **Privilege Protection:** Assert and maintain attorney-client privilege and work product protections where applicable. Legal counsel shall be consulted before any substantive communications with regulatory authorities.

3. **Non-Retaliation:** No retaliatory action shall be taken against any employee who, in good faith, provides information to regulatory authorities or participates in an enforcement action investigation.

4. **Document Preservation:** Upon notice of any enforcement action or credible threat thereof, Meridian shall immediately initiate preservation measures to prevent the destruction, alteration, or loss of potentially relevant documents and data.

5. **Remediation Commitment:** Meridian shall address identified deficiencies through structured Corrective Action Plans with defined timelines, ownership, and verification mechanisms.

## 4.2 Classification Policy

All enforcement actions shall be classified according to the schema defined in Section 5.2 within two business days of initial notification. The CEO and General Counsel must be informed of all Level 1 (Critical) actions within 24 hours.

## 4.3 Communication Policy

All external communications related to enforcement actions shall be routed through the Communications Lead and approved by the General Counsel. No Meridian employee, except those expressly authorized, shall communicate with regulatory authorities, media, or external stakeholders regarding enforcement actions. Internal communications shall be restricted to personnel with a demonstrated need to know.

## 4.4 Remediation Policy

Every enforcement action resulting in confirmed non-compliance shall be addressed through a formal Corrective Action Plan. The CAP shall include root cause identification, specific corrective measures, implementation timeline, responsible parties, and verification criteria. CAP completion shall be validated by Internal Audit or an independent third party as specified in the action-specific response plan.

## 4.5 Lessons Learned Policy

Within 90 days of enforcement action closure, the Incident Commander shall convene a lessons-learned session and produce a formal report with findings, recommendations, and compliance program enhancement proposals. This report shall be presented to the Board or designated Board committee.

# 5. Detailed Procedures

## 5.1 Initial Notification and Triage

### 5.1.1 Notification Receipt

Any Meridian employee who receives a communication that constitutes or may constitute notice of a regulatory enforcement action, inquiry, or investigation shall immediately forward such communication to the Legal & Compliance Department via the dedicated enforcement notification email: **enforcement-notice@meridian.com**. Physical documents shall be scanned and transmitted within one business hour of receipt.

Meridian maintains a dedicated Enforcement Action Inbox monitored continuously during business hours and by an on-call rotation during non-business hours. The monitoring schedule is maintained by the Legal Operations team.

### 5.1.2 Acknowledgement and Logging

Upon receipt, the Compliance Operations Specialist shall:

1. **Timestamp receipt** in the Enforcement Action Register (EAR), a controlled spreadsheet maintained on the Legal & Compliance SharePoint site (restricted access: Legal & Compliance personnel only)
2. **Assign a unique tracking number** using the format: EA-[YYYY]-[SEQ]-[LEVEL], where:
   - YYYY = calendar year of receipt
   - SEQ = three-digit sequential number assigned chronologically
   - LEVEL = preliminary classification (1, 2, or 3) – subject to reclassification
3. **Scan and upload** all original documents to the secure Matter Management System (RMS-Legal) under the assigned tracking number
4. **Notify the CCO and Deputy GC** via encrypted email within **2 hours** of receipt during business hours or by **start of next business day** for after-hours receipt

### 5.1.3 Preliminary Assessment

Within 4 business hours of notification, the CCO or designee shall complete a Preliminary Assessment Form containing the following elements:

| Assessment Element | Details Required |
|--------------------|------------------|
| Regulatory Authority | Full name, jurisdiction, and known enforcement mandate |
| Document Type | Nature of communication (CID, NOPA, consent order, complaint, subpoena, inquiry letter, etc.) |
| Alleged Violations | Specific regulatory provisions or rules cited |
| Time Period | Date range of conduct under review |
| Business Areas Implicated | Operational divisions, products, or services involved |
| Response Deadline | Any stated deadline for response, production, or appearance |
| Preliminary Classification | Level 1, 2, or 3 per Section 5.2 criteria |
| Privilege Assessment | Whether attorney-client privilege or work product protection is available |
| Resource Estimate | Initial estimate of internal and external resources required |

### 5.1.4 Activation Decision

The CCO, in consultation with the GC, shall make an activation decision within **24 hours** of initial notification. Activation options:

- **Full Activation:** Mobilize the complete Enforcement Action Response Team as defined in Section 3. Required for all Level 1 and Level 2 actions.
- **Limited Activation:** Mobilize a subset of the response team for Level 3 actions. Minimum participants: CCO, GC representative, and affected Business Unit Lead.
- **Monitoring Only:** Place the matter in a watch status if the communication is informational or does not require active response. The matter shall be reviewed at the monthly Compliance Review meeting.

## 5.2 Action Classification

### 5.2.1 Classification Criteria

Meridian classifies all enforcement actions into three levels based on the severity and organizational impact criteria defined below.

**Table 5-1: Classification Criteria**

| Classification Level | Criteria | Examples |
|---------------------|----------|----------|
| **Level 1 — Critical** | Meets any of the following:<br/>• Potential for criminal liability<br/>• Material financial penalty exposure (> $5M estimated)<br/>• Threat to business license or operating authority<br/>• Systemic compliance failure across multiple business units<br/>• Regulatory action by a primary regulator with jurisdiction over Meridian's core operations<br/>• Significant media or public attention<br/>• Personal liability exposure for officers or directors<br/>• Required disclosure to investors or securities filings | Formal CID from DOJ; NOPA from primary banking regulator alleging systemic BSA/AML failures; OCR civil monetary penalty notice; European Commission Statement of Objections |
| **Level 2 — Significant** | Meets any of the following:<br/>• Moderate financial penalty exposure ($500K-$5M)<br/>• Compliance deficiency limited to a single business unit or product line<br/>• Remediation achievable within standard business operations<br/>• No criminal liability exposure<br/>• No personal liability for officers or directors<br/>• Limited media interest expected | Single-business-unit CID; routine examination findings elevated to enforcement; state AG inquiry on a specific product or practice |
| **Level 3 — Routine** | Meets all of the following:<br/>• Limited financial penalty exposure (< $500K)<br/>• Isolated compliance deficiency<br/>• Remediation well-defined and achievable within 90 days<br/>• No media interest expected<br/>• No systemic implications | Routine regulatory examination findings; minor reporting deficiencies; isolated consumer complaint investigation |

### 5.2.2 Classification Procedure

1. CCO completes preliminary classification within 24 hours of notification receipt
2. GC confirms or modifies classification within 48 hours
3. CEO notified of all Level 1 classifications within 24 hours of confirmation
4. Board notified of all Level 1 classifications at the next regularly scheduled Board meeting or via special notification if the Board meeting is more than 10 business days away
5. Classification documented in the Enforcement Action Register with rationale
6. Classification reviewed at each Response Team status meeting; reclassification recommended if warranted by evolving circumstances

## 5.3 Response Team Activation

### 5.3.1 Level 1 Activation — Full Response

Upon classification of a Level 1 action, the CCO shall:

1. **Convene the Core Response Team** within 12 hours via encrypted video conference or in-person meeting at the designated incident command location (Primary: Executive Conference Room A, Building 1; Alternate: Legal Department War Room, Building 3)
2. **Issue the Activation Notice** via the Meridian Incident Notification System (MINS), which triggers automated notification to all designated Core Response Team members via email, SMS, and Meridian Secure Messenger
3. **Designate the Incident Command Center** and ensure 24/7 staffing for the duration of the active response phase
4. **Engage outside counsel** within 48 hours if not already retained for the matter (retention from the pre-approved panel of regulatory enforcement counsel maintained by the GC's office)
5. **Issue the Legal Hold Notice** per Section 5.4 procedures

### 5.3.2 Level 2 Activation — Enhanced Response

1. **Convene the Core Response Team** within 24 hours
2. **Issue Activation Notice** via MINS to designated team members
3. **Designate a conference room** for coordination (no 24/7 staffing required unless circumstances warrant)
4. **Evaluate need for outside counsel** and engage within one business week if determined necessary
5. **Issue Legal Hold Notice** if the action involves document requests or potential litigation

### 5.3.3 Level 3 Activation — Standard Response

1. **Notify the GC and affected Business Unit Lead** within 2 business days
2. **Assign a Response Coordinator** from the Legal & Compliance Department to manage the response
3. **Engage outside counsel** only if specifically required by the nature of the action
4. **Issue targeted document preservation notice** if applicable

## 5.4 Document Preservation and Legal Hold

### 5.4.1 Legal Hold Initiation

Upon classification of any enforcement action that includes a document request, subpoena, or potential litigation exposure, the GC's office shall issue a Legal Hold Notice.

The Legal Hold Notice shall contain:

- **Matter Identification:** Enforcement Action tracking number and brief description
- **Preservation Scope:** Categories of documents and data to be preserved
- **Custodian List:** Named individuals and role-based categories subject to the hold
- **Suspension Instruction:** Directive to suspend routine document destruction for covered materials
- **Responsive Document Categories:** Types of documents likely to be responsive, with examples
- **Preservation Instructions:** Specific guidance on preserving electronic and physical records
- **Contact Information:** Designated Legal & Compliance contact for questions
- **Acknowledgment Requirement:** Confirmation of receipt and understanding required within 3 business days

### 5.4.2 Custodian Identification

The Response Team, led by the GC's office, shall identify all custodians likely to possess responsive documents. Identification sources include:

| Source | Method |
|--------|--------|
| Organizational Charts | Review current and historical reporting structures for implicated business units during the relevant period |
| Document Authorship Analysis | Identify personnel who authored, received, or were copied on key documents |
| System Access Logs | Identify personnel with access to relevant systems during the relevant period |
| Interview Input | Include personnel identified during initial witness interviews |
| Historical Personnel Records | Account for departed employees who may have created relevant documents |

### 5.4.3 Technical Preservation Measures

The Information Security team, under the direction of the Technical Lead, shall implement:

1. **Litigation Hold in Microsoft 365:** Place all identified custodian mailboxes and OneDrive accounts under litigation hold within 24 hours of custodian list finalization
2. **Shared Drive Preservation:** Create forensic images of identified shared network drives and SharePoint sites
3. **System Log Preservation:** Suspend log rotation for implicated systems; preserve existing logs
4. **Application Data Export:** Export relevant application data from implicated business systems to secure, access-controlled storage
5. **Physical Document Collection:** Coordinate with Records Custodian to collect and secure physical documents from identified custodians

### 5.4.4 Preservation Verification

Within 5 business days of Legal Hold issuance, the Technical Lead shall verify preservation completeness and document verification results in the Enforcement Action Register. Verification includes confirmation that no covered data has been deleted, altered, or otherwise lost since the preservation date.

## 5.5 Internal Investigation

### 5.5.1 Investigation Planning

Within 5 business days of Response Team activation, the GC and CCO shall develop an Investigation Plan that addresses:

- **Scope:** Factual allegations to be investigated, relevant time period, implicated business units, products, and personnel
- **Investigation Lead:** Designated counsel (internal or external) leading the investigation
- **Investigation Team:** Personnel assigned to specific workstreams
- **Document Collection Plan:** Specific custodians, systems, and data sources to be collected
- **Witness Interview Plan:** Prioritized list of witnesses, interview sequence, and timeline
- **Privilege Determination:** Process for identifying and logging privileged materials
- **Resource Requirements:** Staffing, technology tools (e-discovery platform, forensic tools), outside services
- **Estimated Timeline:** Milestones and completion targets
- **Budget:** Estimated internal and external costs, approved per the Enforcement Action Budget authority delegated to the GC

### 5.5.2 Document Collection and Review

Document collection shall proceed according to the collection plan. Meridian utilizes the Reveal e-discovery platform for Level 1 and Level 2 actions. Level 3 actions may utilize the native Microsoft 365 e-discovery capabilities.

**Collection Procedure:**

1. **Identification:** Identify potentially responsive data sources per the Investigation Plan
2. **Preservation Confirmation:** Confirm that Legal Hold measures are active for all identified sources
3. **Collection:** Execute forensically sound collection using approved tools and methods; maintain chain of custody documentation for all collections
4. **Processing:** Process collected materials in the e-discovery platform (de-duplication, email threading, keyword indexing)
5. **Review:** Conduct first-level review for responsiveness and privilege; conduct second-level review for substantive content
6. **Production:** Prepare responsive, non-privileged documents for production to the regulatory authority in the format specified by the authority

**Table 5-2: Document Coding Standards**

| Coding Field | Values | Description |
|--------------|--------|-------------|
| Responsiveness | Responsive / Non-Responsive / Potentially Responsive | Whether the document is responsive to the regulatory request |
| Privilege | Privileged / Non-Privileged / Redacted | Privilege status (attorney-client, work product, etc.) |
| Issue Tags | Per matter-specific issue tag list | Substantive issues addressed in the document |
| Confidentiality | Confidential / Highly Confidential / Trade Secret | Document confidentiality designation |
| PII Indicator | Contains PII / No PII | Whether the document contains personally identifiable information |

### 5.5.3 Witness Interviews

Witness interviews shall be conducted by designated counsel or investigators. Interview protocol:

1. **Notice:** Provide reasonable notice to witnesses (typically 3 business days for current employees)
2. **Upjohn Warning:** Current employees shall receive an Upjohn warning advising that counsel represents Meridian, not the individual; that communications are privileged but the privilege belongs to Meridian; and that the employee should maintain confidentiality
3. **Documentation:** Document interviews via attorney notes (not verbatim transcripts unless specifically warranted)
4. **Sequence:** Interview witnesses in order from least to most senior within a workstream, generally
5. **Follow-up:** Conduct follow-up interviews as new information emerges

### 5.5.4 Privilege Assertion and Logging

The GC shall direct the privilege review process. All potentially privileged documents shall be:

1. Identified during the review process using privilege search terms and manual review
2. Logged on a Privilege Log maintained for the matter, containing: document ID, date, author, recipients, privilege type, and basis for assertion
3. Withheld from production if privilege is confirmed
4. Subject to clawback agreement if production occurs inadvertently

### 5.5.5 Root Cause Analysis

The investigation shall include a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) that identifies the underlying causes of any non-compliance confirmed by the investigation. The RCA shall utilize the "5 Whys" methodology supplemented by fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram analysis where appropriate.

**RCA Categories for Consideration:**

- Policy and procedure adequacy
- Training effectiveness and coverage
- System and technical controls
- Staffing and resource adequacy
- Management oversight and governance
- Third-party or vendor dependencies
- Incentive structures and performance metrics
- Organizational culture factors

## 5.6 External Communication and Regulator Engagement

### 5.6.1 Communication Protocol

All substantive communications with the regulatory authority shall be:

1. **Coordinated** through the designated Regulatory Liaison (typically the GC, outside counsel, or CCO as designated in the Response Plan)
2. **Reviewed** by the GC or designee before transmission
3. **Documented** in the Enforcement Action Register with date, participants, content summary, and any commitments made
4. **Tracked** for deadlines and commitments

### 5.6.2 Written Responses

Written responses to regulatory authorities shall follow this preparation process:

1. **First Draft:** Prepared by the subject matter lead with input from the investigation team
2. **Legal Review:** Reviewed and revised by the GC or designated counsel
3. **Accuracy Verification:** Factual assertions verified by the relevant business unit lead
4. **Executive Review:** Level 1 responses reviewed by CEO and Board-designated committee; Level 2 responses reviewed by CCO and relevant VP
5. **Final Approval:** GC approval required on all responses

### 5.6.3 Oral Communications

Oral communications (meetings, teleconferences, testimony) with regulatory authorities:

1. **Pre-Meeting Briefing:** All participants briefed by counsel on scope, privilege boundaries, and communication protocols
2. **Designated Spokesperson:** A single lead spokesperson designated for each meeting
3. **Note-Taking:** Designated note-taker assigned; notes marked as attorney work product where applicable
4. **De-briefing:** Post-meeting de-briefing conducted; follow-up commitments documented

### 5.6.4 Media and Public Statements

External communications outside of direct regulator engagement (media, public statements, investor communications):

| Communication Type | Approval Authority | Coordination Requirement |
|--------------------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| Press Release | CEO + GC | Communications Lead drafts; Board notified prior to release |
| Media Response (substantive) | GC + Communications Lead | CEO informed in advance |
| Media Response (no comment) | Communications Lead | GC informed |
| Investor Communication | CFO + GC | Per Reg FD and securities law obligations |
| Employee Communication | CEO + HR Director | Communications Lead drafts |

## 5.7 Remediation Plan Development and Execution

### 5.7.1 Corrective Action Plan Development

For all enforcement actions that identify compliance deficiencies, the CCO shall lead the development of a Corrective Action Plan (CAP). CAP development timeline:

- **Level 1:** Within 30 calendar days of investigation completion
- **Level 2:** Within 45 calendar days of investigation completion
- **Level 3:** Within 60 calendar days of investigation completion

The CAP shall use the standardized Meridian CAP Template and address:

| CAP Element | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Deficiency Description | Specific description of each deficiency, mapped to the regulatory finding or internal investigation conclusion |
| Root Cause | Identified root cause(s) for each deficiency |
| Corrective Action | Specific, measurable corrective action for each deficiency |
| Responsible Party | Named individual accountable for completion |
| Implementation Date | Target completion date |
| Verification Method | How completion and effectiveness will be verified |
| Control Enhancement | Permanent control improvement to prevent recurrence |
| Interim Controls | Controls to be implemented during the remediation period |

### 5.7.2 CAP Approval

CAP approval requirements:

| Action Level | Approval Required |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Level 1 | Board (or designated committee) + GC + CEO |
| Level 2 | GC + CCO + affected Business Unit VP |
| Level 3 | CCO + Business Unit Lead |

### 5.7.3 CAP Execution Monitoring

CAP execution shall be monitored through:

1. **Bi-weekly CAP Status Meetings** for Level 1 actions; monthly for Level 2; ad hoc for Level 3
2. **CAP Dashboard** maintained on the Legal & Compliance SharePoint site, updated weekly
3. **Milestone Tracking** with automated alerts generated 7 days before each milestone deadline
4. **Escalation Triggers:**
   - Milestone > 15 days past due: escalated to CCO
   - Milestone > 30 days past due: escalated to GC
   - Milestone > 45 days past due: escalated to CEO and Board

### 5.7.4 Remediation Verification

Upon CAP completion, verification shall be conducted by:

- **Level 1:** Independent third-party assessor or Internal Audit (if approved by the regulatory authority)
- **Level 2:** Internal Audit or Compliance Testing team
- **Level 3:** Compliance Testing team or self-assessment by the business unit, validated by Legal & Compliance

Verification results shall be documented in a Remediation Verification Report and presented to the Response Team.

## 5.8 Technical Documentation

For enforcement actions involving Meridian's algorithmic models, automated decision systems, or other AI-enabled applications, technical documentation shall be prepared that describes:

- **System Architecture:** Overview of the system design, data flows, and processing logic
- **Training and Operational Data:** Description of datasets used for model development and operational deployment
- **Testing Protocols:** Documentation of tests performed, including accuracy, robustness, and bias assessments
- **Performance Metrics:** Measured performance against defined acceptance criteria
- **Operational Controls:** Human oversight mechanisms, monitoring procedures, and decommissioning criteria
- **Risk Identification:** Known risks, limitations, and residual risk assessment

This technical documentation shall be maintained in the Meridian AI Asset Register (AIMS platform) and updated in accordance with SOP-AIMS-001.

## 5.9 Settlement and Resolution

### 5.9.1 Settlement Authority

Settlement authority levels are defined as:

| Settlement Parameter | Approval Authority |
|----------------------|--------------------|
| Monetary penalty < $500K | GC + CFO |
| Monetary penalty $500K - $5M | GC + CFO + CEO |
| Monetary penalty > $5M | GC + CFO + CEO + Board |
| Injunctive relief affecting a single business unit | GC + CEO |
| Injunctive relief affecting multiple business units or corporate structure | GC + CEO + Board |
| Admission of liability | GC + CEO + Board (required in ALL cases) |
| Consent order or consent decree | GC + CEO + Board |

### 5.9.2 Resolution Documentation

All enforcement action resolutions shall be documented in a Resolution Memorandum containing:

1. Summary of the enforcement action and findings
2. Summary of the resolution terms (penalties, injunctive relief, consent order provisions)
3. Impact analysis (financial, operational, reputational)
4. CAP integration (how resolution terms are incorporated into the ongoing CAP)
5. Ongoing compliance obligations (reporting requirements, monitor appointments, etc.)
6. Lessons learned (preliminary)

## 5.10 Lessons Learned Process

### 5.10.1 Post-Resolution Review

Within 90 calendar days of enforcement action closure (as defined by the GC), the CCO shall convene a Lessons Learned Review. Participants shall include all Core Response Team members and other key participants as determined by the CCO.

### 5.10.2 Lessons Learned Report

The CCO shall produce a formal Lessons Learned Report containing:

1. **Action Summary:** Brief description of the enforcement action, timeline, and resolution
2. **Response Assessment:** Evaluation of the response effectiveness, including timelines, team coordination, and resource adequacy
3. **Root Cause Summary:** Synthesis of identified root causes
4. **Control Gaps Identified:** Specific control weaknesses that allowed the non-compliance to occur
5. **Compliance Program Enhancements:** Recommended enhancements to the compliance program
6. **Policy and Procedure Updates:** Specific SOP revisions identified
7. **Training Enhancements:** Recommended training updates
8. **Resource Recommendations:** Recommended staffing, budget, or technology investments

### 5.10.3 Lessons Learned Tracking

The CCO shall maintain a Lessons Learned Register that tracks all recommendations from enforcement action reviews and subsequent implementation. The Register shall be reviewed quarterly at the Compliance Committee meeting. Recommendations not implemented within 180 days shall be escalated to the CEO with a justification for deferral or rejection.

# 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

## 8.1 Exception Request Process

Any deviation from the procedures defined in this SOP shall be documented via an Exception Request submitted to the CCO. The Exception Request shall include:

1. **SOP Reference:** Specific section of this SOP from which deviation is sought
2. **Justification:** Business or legal rationale for the exception
3. **Risk Assessment:** Assessment of additional risk introduced by the exception
4. **Mitigating Controls:** Any alternative controls to be applied during the exception period
5. **Duration:** Time period for which the exception is requested
6. **Approval:** Per the exception approval matrix below

## 8.2 Exception Approval Matrix

| Exception Type | Approval Authority | Maximum Duration |
|----------------|--------------------|------------------|
| Extension of response deadline (< 10 business days) | CCO | One-time, 10 days |
| Modification of response team composition | CCO + GC | Duration of matter |
| Alternative investigation methodology | CCO + GC | Duration of matter |
| CAP deviation (minor: timeline adjustment < 30 days) | CCO | 30 days |
| CAP deviation (major: scope reduction, removal of corrective action) | GC + CEO | Duration of matter |
| Communication protocol deviation | GC | One-time |
| Budget overrun (< 15%) | CCO + CFO | Duration of matter |
| Budget overrun (> 15%) | GC + CFO + CEO | Duration of matter |

## 8.3 Escalation Path

If the CCO determines that an enforcement action response is not proceeding effectively, the following escalation path shall be followed:

1. **CCO to GC:** For legal strategy concerns, privilege issues, or regulator relationship concerns
2. **CCO to CEO:** For resource adequacy concerns, cross-business-unit coordination failures, or significant timeline delays
3. **GC to Board:** For material legal exposure concerns, settlement authority questions, or potential director/officer liability
4. **Any Response Team member to CCO:** Any team member may escalate concerns directly to the CCO; the CCO shall document and address all such escalations within 24 hours

## 9.2 Activation Drill

The CCO shall conduct an annual Enforcement Action Response activation drill simulating a Level 1 or Level 2 enforcement action. The drill shall test:

1. Notification receipt and triage procedures
2. Response team mobilization (time to assemble)
3. Legal Hold issuance and custodial acknowledgment
4. Document preservation execution
5. Communications protocol adherence
6. CAP development process

Drill results shall be documented in an After-Action Report and reviewed at the Compliance Committee meeting. Corrective actions identified from the drill shall be incorporated into the CAP framework as "Internal Audit Findings" (non-regulatory).

## 9.3 Training Tracking

All training completion shall be tracked in the Meridian Learning Management System (LMS). Response Team members who fail to complete required annual training within 30 days of the due date shall be reported to the CCO and may be suspended from Response Team participation until training is completed.
