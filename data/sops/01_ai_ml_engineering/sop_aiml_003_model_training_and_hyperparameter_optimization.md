# Standard Operating Procedure: Model Training and Hyperparameter Optimization

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory framework for the end-to-end execution, governance, and validation of model training and hyperparameter optimization activities across Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. The purpose of this document is to ensure that all machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) models developed, acquired, or deployed by Meridian are trained in a secure, reproducible, cost-efficient, and auditable manner that protects patient health information (PHI), maintains the integrity of our scientific methodology, and aligns with our regulatory obligations.

This SOP operationalizes the principles of reproducibility, traceability, and computational efficiency, ensuring that models transition from experimentation to production with a complete and immutable lineage of their training provenance.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to all business units, departments, and personnel involved in the development, training, retraining, fine-tuning, and hyperparameter optimization of AI/ML models that process, analyze, or generate predictions based on corporate data assets. The scope encompasses the following Meridian product lines:

| Product Line | In-Scope Activities | Classification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clinical AI Platform** | Training of patient risk scoring models, diagnostic imaging analysis algorithms, and adverse event prediction systems. | High-Risk AI |
| **HealthPay Financial Services** | Development and retraining of credit scoring, fraud detection, and medical lending underwriting models. | Regulated Financial Model |
| **MedInsight Analytics** | Training of population health models, care gap identification classifiers, and outcomes prediction engines using PHI from ~12M patients. | PHI-Processing Model |
| **Meridian SaaS Platform** | Core infrastructure ML components for anomaly detection, resource optimization, and security intelligence. | Operational AI |

This SOP is binding upon all full-time employees, contractors, interns, and third-party vendors who architect, code, train, or operationalize AI/ML models at Meridian. All such personnel are individually accountable for adherence to the procedures defined herein.

### 1.3 Out of Scope

- Statistical models built exclusively within SAS or Excel that do not leverage machine learning algorithms (refer to SOP-DS-001, Statistical Model Development).
- Third-party SaaS models where Meridian does not control the training process (refer to SOP-VRM-005, Vendor AI Risk Management).
- Pure inference operations on already-trained, frozen models (refer to SOP-AIML-008, Model Serving and Inferencing Operations).
- Foundation model pretraining from scratch on general-purpose internet corpora. This SOP governs subsequent fine-tuning on Meridian data.

## 4. Policy Statements

The following high-level policies govern all model training and hyperparameter optimization activities at Meridian Health Technologies.

### 4.1 Reproducibility Policy

Every Run that is intended to produce a production-promotable model artifact must be reproducible. Reproducibility is defined as the ability to re-execute the training pipeline and obtain a model with performance metrics within ±0.5% of the original Run's reported metrics on an identical hold-out test set. To enable this, all Runs must capture: the exact code commit hash, the Golden Dataset version, the environment (container image digest), and the full launch configuration as a Reproducibility Artifact in MLflow.

### 4.2 Data Integrity and PHI Handling

All training data must originate from a Meridian-authorized data source (Snowflake Production, Data Lake v2, or Approved Data Exchange Partner). No unapproved external datasets may be joined to PHI-containing tables without explicit DPO and CISO approval. Training on PHI must occur only within Meridian's VPC-bounded private subnets (us-east-1 or eu-west-1). Data must be de-identified to the Safe Harbor standard before being used in non-production experimental Runs unless an explicit exception has been granted.

### 4.3 Hyperparameter Optimization Governance

All hyperparameter search activity consuming more than 100 GPU-hours or 1,000 vCPU-hours requires a formal Hyperparameter Optimization (HPO) Plan approved by the Engineering Manager. The HPO Plan must define the search strategy (e.g., Bayesian, Hyperband), the search space, the early stopping criteria, and the objective metric. Random or grid search is prohibited for High-Risk AI models without documented justification for why more efficient search strategies are infeasible.

### 4.4 Compute Resource Allocation

Training compute resources are a finite and shared corporate asset. All teams must operate within their assigned Compute Quotas. Projects may not hoard resources through indefinite warm-standby clusters. The MLOps team will automatically terminate any idle interactive SageMaker notebook instance after 6 hours of inactivity. All scheduled training jobs must be submitted through the job queue; ad-hoc, unlogged manual executions on production GPU clusters are prohibited.

### 4.5 Transparency and Explainability

Model training must produce a standard set of transparency artifacts for every model version. These include: a "Model Card" describing intended use and performance across demographic subgroups, global feature importance using SHAP or a comparable method, and a record of any human intervention in the training loop. These artifacts facilitate the oversight obligations described in Meridian's AI Governance Charter.

## 5. Detailed Procedures

This section delineates the step-by-step processes for the primary operational workflows of model training.

### 5.1 Training Environment Setup

All training must occur within a governed, ephemeral, containerized environment. The process for provisioning such an environment is detailed below.

**Step 1: Project Onboarding**
- The Engineering Manager registers a new MLflow Experiment via the Meridian MLOps Service Catalog (ServiceNow catalog item: "Register ML Experiment").
- A unique `experiment_id` is generated and tied to the project's chargeback account.
- The MLOps team configures the appropriate AWS IAM roles and namespace in Kubeflow (for PHI-sensitive workloads) or SageMaker project template (for standard workloads).

**Step 2: Selecting the Base Image**
- The Data Scientist consults the "Approved Image Registry" in AWS ECR.
- For Clinical AI Platform work, the approved base image is `meridian-clinical-train/20.04-cu118-py39:latest` which includes audited versions of PyTorch 2.0, MONAI, and NVIDIA CUDA 11.8.
- For HealthPay Financial Services, the base image is `meridian-finml/20.04-xgboost15:latest` for gradient-boosted models or `meridian-finml/20.04-tf210:latest` for deep learning models.
- Custom extension of base images is permitted but must be versioned in the project repository and pass a vulnerability scan within 24 hours (Jenkins pipeline: `docker-image-security-check`).

**Step 3: Environment Launch Command**
- Training jobs are initiated exclusively via the Meridian CLI tool (`mcli`), which abstracts the underlying orchestration layer.
- Example launch command for a SageMaker job:
  ```bash
  mcli train launch --image meridian-clinical-train/20.04-cu118-py39:latest \
                    --instance-type ml.p4d.24xlarge \
                    --instance-count 4 \
                    --experiment-id 10284 \
                    --dataset-version phi-diabetes-2025-q3-v2 \
                    --code-commit $(git rev-parse HEAD) \
                    --entry-point train_diabetic_retinopathy.py
  ```
- The `mcli` tool automatically tags the cloud resources with the Experiment ID, Cost Center, and a "TTL" (Time-To-Live) after which it is eligible for automated reclamation.

### 5.2 Dataset Preparation, Versioning, and Registration

No model training shall begin without a formal dataset registration event.

**Step 1: Data Extraction and Query Audit**
- Data is queried from Snowflake using a service account tied to the Data Lake's read-replica.
- The SQL query must be committed to the project repository and reviewed by a peer.
- All queries against PHI tables are automatically logged by Imperva Data Security Fabric and auditable by the CISO.

**Step 2: Feature Engineering and Preprocessing**
- Feature engineering logic must be embedded in version-controlled Python scripts, not executed in Jupyter notebook cells destined for deletion.
- The output of the feature engineering step is a set of NumPy binary files (`.npy`) or Parquet files.

**Step 3: Generating the Golden Dataset**
- The curated feature set and labels are bundled into a single immutable dataset artifact using `mcli dataset freeze`.
- This command computes a SHA-256 hash for every file in the dataset and generates a `dataset_manifest.json` that includes all hashes, a timestamp, and the data steward's digital signature.
- **Example Golden Dataset ID:** `meridian://datasets/golden/PHI-DBT-2025-09-10/v1/manifest.json`

**Step 4: Data Splits**
- The dataset must be split into training, validation, and hold-out test sets at the point of registration.
- For longitudinal patient data, a "patient-wise" split is mandatory to prevent data leakage. The `patient_split_key` (encrypted member ID) must be supplied to the `mcli dataset freeze` command to deterministically assign patients to splits.
- The hold-out test set is stored with write-once-read-many (WORM) permissions until the final evaluation phase.

### 5.3 Model Training Execution

This is the core computational workflow.

**Step 1: Job Submission**
- The ML engineer submits the job using `mcli`. The platform queues the job if resources are insufficient.
- The training script receives all paths and parameters as environment variables, never hardcoded inside the script.

**Step 2: Intra-Training Monitoring**
- **Logging:** Key metrics (loss, accuracy, AUC-ROC per epoch) and system metrics (GPU utilization, VRAM) are streamed to MLflow and Datadog dashboards in real time.
- **Health Checks:** The Training Watchtower monitors for silent failures: loss NaN divergence, zero variance in weights, and gradient explosion. If a critical anomaly score is reached, the Watchtower automatically triggers a PagerDuty alert to the Data Scientist on call.
- **Checkpointing:** Models must checkpoint the best-performing iteration (based on validation loss) to S3 every 1,000 steps. Meridian uses a checkpoint retention window of 72 hours; all intermediary checkpoints are automatically purged after this window to manage storage costs.

**Step 3: Human-in-the-Loop Review (HITL)**
- For models classified as High-Risk AI under EU AI Act Annex III, training is not a fully automated black box.
- At predefined intervals (every N epochs or after processing specific data batches), a batch of "unusual" or "edge case" training samples is routed to a review queue in the Meridian Clinical Validation Portal.
- A designated clinical reviewer (credentialed clinician within the product team) examines these samples to confirm the validity of the ground truth label and the appropriateness of the features. This review acts as a continuous quality check on the signal the model is learning.

**Step 4: Training Completion and Artifact Registration**
- Upon successful completion, `mcli` automatically copies the final model artifact (saved model format for TensorFlow, model script + state dict for PyTorch) to the MLflow artifact store (S3 bucket: `s3://meridian-mlflow-artifacts-prod`).
- The script must programmatically call `mlflow.log_params(config_dict)` and `mlflow.log_metrics(eval_dict)` to ensure that all parameters, including default hyperparameters not actively tuned, are logged.

### 5.4 Hyperparameter Optimization (HPO) Workflow

Hyperparameter tuning is governed by a rigorous workflow to balance exploration with computational cost.

**Step 1: HPO Plan Submission**
- The Data Scientist authors an HPO Plan using template `HPO-PLAN-TEMPLATE-v3.2`, which requires:
    - **Objective Metric:** e.g., Maximize Validation AUPRC.
    - **Search Algorithm:** Must justify choice (e.g., Bayesian with Gaussian Processes, Hyperband).
    - **Search Space:** Define min, max, and distribution (uniform, log-uniform, categorical) for each hyperparameter.
    - **Budget:** Maximum number of trials or GPU-hours.
    - **Early Stopping Rule:** Method (e.g., Median Stopping Rule) and patience.
- The Plan is submitted via `mcli hpo plan-approve` and routed to the Engineering Manager and, for High-Risk AI, the Chief Medical Officer.

**Step 2: Automated HPO Execution**
- Once approved, the run is initialized. Meridian's platform uses Katib (Kubeflow) for on-premise and SageMaker Automatic Model Tuning (AMT) for AWS-native workloads.
- The HPO controller manages trial lifecycles: launching new combinations, evaluating intermediate results, and pruning unpromising trials based on the early stopping rule.

**Step 3: HPO Results Review**
- Upon exhaustion of the HPO budget, the platform generates an `HPO_Results_Report.html`.
- This report contains the top-5 performing hyperparameter combinations, parallel coordinate charts for the search space, and the correlation between each hyperparameter and the objective.
- The Data Scientist selects the champion hyperparameter set, documents the justification for selection (balancing performance gains against complexity), and records it in the MLflow Run.

**Step 4: Final Retrain with Champion HPs**
- The champion set of hyperparameters is never accepted directly from the best HPO trial. To avoid overfitting the validation set through the tuning process, the model must be retrained from scratch incorporating the full training+validation data (excluding hold-out) using the champion hyperparameters.
- This final, locked-down Run is designated as the "Release Candidate" and uniquely flagged in MLflow by adding the tag `release_candidate: true`.

### 5.5 Federated and Transfer Learning Protocols

For specific collaborative projects with partner hospitals where raw PHI cannot be centralized:

1.  **Topology Approval:** The VP of Engineering and CISO must approve the federated learning topology. The topology must ensure differential privacy guarantees (ε budget ≤ 2.0).
2.  **Model Initialization:** A global model is initialized at Meridian's central server. The architecture (but no weights) is distributed to partner nodes.
3.  **Local Training:** Each institution trains the model on its local data for a fixed number of epochs.
4.  **Secure Aggregation:** Only encrypted gradient updates are sent back to the central Meridian aggregation server using SecAgg+ protocol.
5.  **Persistence:** The global model, after aggregation, is versioned in MLflow with the tag `federated_learning: true` and the full lineage of participating nodes (anonymized IDs) and rounds.

### 6.1 Technical Controls

| Control ID | Control Name | Description | Enforcement Mechanism |

| **TC-03** | PHI Training Network Boundary | All training jobs processing raw or pseudonymized PHI must run in isolated private subnets with no egress to the internet. | AWS Security Groups and NACLs; AWS Config rule `restricted-instance-tenancy` actively monitors for violations. |

| **TC-05** | Secret Zero Storage | API keys, database credentials, and other secrets shall never be hardcoded in training scripts or configuration files. | HashiCorp Vault dynamic secrets injected as ephemeral Kubernetes secrets at launch. |
| **TC-06** | Resource Hard Limits | Every training namespace has a `ResourceQuota` specifying maximum GPU and memory. Attempts to exceed are blocked at the scheduler level. | Kubernetes ResourceQuota; AWS Service Quotas per account. |

## 7. Monitoring, Metrics, and Reporting

Model training is a continuous operational activity that requires real-time monitoring and routine management reporting.

### 7.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The following KPIs are tracked for all model training activities. Performance against these KPIs is displayed on the "AI Engineering Central" Grafana dashboard.

| KPI ID | KPI Name | Target | Measurement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **KPI-01** | Reproducibility Success Rate | >99.5% | Successful re-runs from artifact / total requested reproducibility audits, per quarter. |
| **KPI-02** | GPU Cluster Utilization | 65% - 75% peak | `dcgm_gpu_utilization` metric aggregated across all non-DR nodes. Below 40% triggers an underutilization review. |
| **KPI-03** | PHI Containment Violations | 0 per month | Number of times AWS Config detects a training job with a PHI dataset tag running outside a PHI-authorized subnet. A critical incident. |
| **KPI-04** | Experiment Time-to-First-Run | < 4 hours | Median time from a Data Scientist requesting a new Experiment to their first successful `mcli train launch`. |
| **KPI-05** | HPO Efficiency Ratio | > 2x Random Search | The performance improvement of the HPO strategy vs. an equivalent budget random search, as quantified by Vizier benchmarks. |
| **KPI-06** | Idle Instance Waste | < 5% of total monthly compute | Aggregate cost of instances running with <10% GPU utilization for >1 hour after training. Automatically stopped, but the cost is wasted. |

### 7.2 Reporting Cadence

**Daily Operational Report (Automated):**
- A daily summary email pushed at 07:00 local time to all Engineering Managers.
- Lists all failed or aberrant training jobs from the past 24 hours, their root cause (if automatically determined), and the current queue depth for GPU-accelerated jobs.

**Monthly MLOps Governance Review:**
- A formal report compiled by the MLOps team and delivered to the CAIO, VP of Engineering, and VP of IT Operations.
- Includes trend analysis of KPIs, cost breakdowns by business line, and a rolling 12-month capacity forecast.
- Identifies models that have been trained but not promoted to production within 90 days (potential "shelfware").

**Quarterly AI Risk Posture Report:**
- An executive summary delivered to the Board AI Governance Committee.
- Summarizes reproducibility audit results, bias monitoring trends on trained models, and aggregated HITL findings from the Clinical AI Platform training events.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

While strict adherence to this SOP is expected, a formal exception process exists to handle unusual cases without compromising governance.

### 8.1 Exception Request Process

1.  **Identification:** The Data Scientist or Engineering Manager identifies a valid business need to deviate from a specific provision of this SOP (e.g., use of a non-standard optimizer not in the approved base image, an emergency fix requiring bypassing full HPO).
2.  **Documentation:** The requester submits a "SOP-AIML-003 Exception Request" form (Form ID: FRM-AIML-003-EX) in ServiceNow. The form must detail:
    - The specific clause of the SOP for which the exception is sought.
    - A technical justification.
    - An assessment of the risk introduced.
    - Proposed compensating controls and a plan for remediation to bring the project back into compliance.
3.  **Risk Assessment:** The Model Validation Lead performs an independent risk assessment within 48 hours (or 4 hours for a P1 emergency).
4.  **Approval Matrix:**

| Risk Level | Exception Type Example | Approval Required |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Low** | Non-standard Python library within a major framework | Engineering Manager |
| **Medium** | Use of public cloud GPU for non-PHI data due to on-premise capacity exhaustion | VP of Engineering + CISO |
| **High** | Training on PHI outside VPC boundary; Bypassing HITL review step | Chief AI Officer + CISO + Chief Medical Officer (if clinical) |
| **Critical** | Modification to the Golden Dataset hash for a deployed clinical model | CEO + CISO + General Counsel |

### 8.2 Emergency Escalation Path

If a critical production model is degrading and requires an immediate, out-of-policy retraining (e.g., a severe fraud detection model drift detected mid-day):

1.  The VP of Financial Services or Chief Medical Officer declares a "Code Red Model Degradation" event.
2.  The MLOps team manually overrides the job queue to prioritize the emergency retraining job with the highest scheduling priority.
3.  A retroactive HPO Plan and exception form must be filed within 24 business hours. The model trained under emergency conditions is tagged with `emergency_retraining: true` in MLflow and is subject to an expedited validation review within 5 business days before it can receive a Long-Term Production designation.
