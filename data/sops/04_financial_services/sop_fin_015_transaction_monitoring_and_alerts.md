## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the enterprise-wide framework for real-time and batch transaction monitoring, alert generation, and investigative response within Meridian Health Technologies, Inc.’s HealthPay Financial Services business line. The purpose of this SOP is to:

- Detect and prevent fraudulent, unauthorized, or anomalous financial transactions across the HealthPay platform, which processes approximately $4.2 billion in healthcare payments, patient financing, medical lending, and claims automation annually.
- Ensure compliance with applicable supervisory guidance, including the Federal Reserve’s SR 11-7 model risk management framework, SOC 2 trust services criteria, and internal risk appetite statements.
- Define consistent, repeatable procedures for alert investigation, case management, escalation, and regulatory filing, including Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) where applicable.
- Establish accountability for model performance monitoring, threshold tuning, and independent validation of transaction monitoring models and rules.
- Protect Meridian Health Technologies' clients, patients, and financial partners from financial crime, reputational harm, and operational losses.

### 1.2 Scope

This SOP applies to:

| In Scope | Out of Scope |
|---|---|
| All payment transactions processed through the HealthPay Financial Services platform, including patient payments, provider disbursements, medical lending originations and servicing, claims automation, and financing transactions. | Clinical AI Platform transaction logging (governed by SOP-AI-042). |
| Automated monitoring rules, machine learning-based anomaly detection models, and manual review processes administered by the Financial Services Operations team. | General ledger reconciliation and accounting controls (governed by SOP-FIN-008). |
| All employees, contractors, and third-party service providers with access to transaction monitoring systems, alert queues, or case management tools. | Physical security of data centers (governed by SOP-SEC-011). |
| Transaction monitoring systems hosted on the Meridian SaaS Platform (AWS us-east-1 and eu-west-1 regions). | Customer-facing fraud dispute resolution (governed by SOP-CUST-019). |
| Credit scoring models, fraud detection models, and lending decision models subject to SR 11-7. | |

## 2. Definitions and Acronyms

### 2.1 Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Alert** | A system-generated notification indicating that a transaction or pattern of transactions has met or exceeded predefined risk thresholds or deviates from established behavioral baselines. |
| **Alert Fatigue** | A condition in which the volume of alerts generated by monitoring systems exceeds the investigative capacity of the operations team, leading to desensitization and potential missed true positives. |
| **Case** | A structured investigative record created in the case management system to document the review, analysis, and disposition of one or more related alerts or suspicious activities. |
| **False Positive Rate (FPR)** | The proportion of generated alerts that, upon investigation, are determined not to represent suspicious, fraudulent, or anomalous activity. Expressed as a percentage. |
| **Model Risk Tier** | Classification assigned to each transaction monitoring model based on materiality, complexity, and business criticality per SR 11-7 guidance. Tier 1 represents highest risk; Tier 3 represents lowest. |
| **Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)** | A regulatory filing submitted to the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) or equivalent authority when a financial institution detects activity that may constitute money laundering, fraud, or other financial crimes. |
| **Transaction Monitoring Model** | Any quantitative or rules-based system used to assign risk scores, generate alerts, or classify transactions. Includes both deterministic rules and machine learning models. |
| **Tuning** | The process of adjusting monitoring rule parameters, model thresholds, or risk scoring weights to optimize detection effectiveness while managing alert volumes. |
| **True Positive Rate (TPR)** | The proportion of actual fraudulent or suspicious transactions correctly identified by the monitoring system. |

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Risk-Based Monitoring

Meridian Health Technologies deploys a layered, risk-based transaction monitoring framework commensurate with the size, complexity, and risk profile of the HealthPay Financial Services business. Monitoring intensity scales with transaction value, product type, geographic corridor, and customer risk rating. All payment transactions are subject to automated screening; high-risk transactions are escalated for mandatory human review.

### 4.2 SR 11-7 Model Risk Management

All transaction monitoring models—including rules-based systems, statistical models, and machine learning models—are subject to the full lifecycle requirements of SR 11-7 model risk management guidance, specifically:

- **Section II.B (Model Development, Implementation, and Use):** Each model shall have documented design objectives, theoretical foundations, data inputs, assumptions, and limitations prior to production deployment.
- **Section II.C (Model Validation):** Independent validation shall be conducted by qualified individuals not involved in model development. Validation frequency shall be based on model risk tier: Tier 1 models validated annually; Tier 2 models validated biennially; Tier 3 models validated every three years.
- **Section III.A (Governance):** The Model Risk Governance Committee shall review and approve all model material changes, validation findings, and remediation plans.
- **Section III.B (Policies and Procedures):** Comprehensive Documentation of Model Risk (CDMR) packages shall be maintained for each model and updated upon material change.
- **Section IV (Model Inventory):** A complete, current inventory of all transaction monitoring models shall be maintained in the enterprise model risk management system.

### 4.3 Alert Investigation Timeliness

All generated alerts shall be triaged by severity level. Alert investigation SLAs are defined based on alert priority and shall be measured from the timestamp of alert generation in the HealthPay Guard TMS:

| Alert Priority | Initial Triage SLA | Investigation Completion SLA | SAR Filing Decision SLA |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 – Critical | 30 minutes | 4 hours | 8 hours |
| P2 – High | 2 hours | 24 hours | 48 hours |
| P3 – Medium | 8 hours | 72 hours | 5 business days |
| P4 – Low | 24 hours | 10 business days | N/A (no SAR requirement unless escalated) |

### 4.4 SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria Alignment

The transaction monitoring program supports SOC 2 trust services criteria as follows:

- **Security:** Access to the HealthPay Guard TMS is controlled through Okta SSO with multi-factor authentication and role-based access controls. System access is logged and retained in Datadog for 365 days.
- **Availability:** The HealthPay Guard TMS is designed for high availability. Monitoring processes are designed with continuity in mind, and critical monitoring functions are replicated across availability zones. Recovery activities are prioritized based on business impact during service interruptions.
- **Processing Integrity:** Transaction monitoring ensures that payment processing is accurate, complete, and timely. Anomaly detection models identify processing errors, duplicate transactions, and data integrity issues.
- **Confidentiality:** Investigative case files containing PHI or sensitive financial data are encrypted at rest (AWS KMS) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Access to case details is restricted to authorized investigative personnel.
- **Privacy:** Personal data processed during investigations is handled in accordance with GDPR, HIPAA, and Meridian Privacy Policy (POL-PRIV-001). Data minimization principles are applied to investigative data collection.

### 4.5 Zero Tolerance for Internal Fraud

Meridian Health Technologies maintains a zero-tolerance policy for internal fraud, including circumvention of transaction monitoring controls, manipulation of alert dispositions, or unauthorized disclosure of investigative information. Any suspected internal malfeasance shall be immediately escalated to the Chief Compliance Officer and Chief Human Resources Officer per SOP-HR-022.

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Monitoring Rules and Model Management

#### 5.1.1 Monitoring Rule Lifecycle

All transaction monitoring rules, whether deterministic or model-based, shall follow a standardized lifecycle:

**Phase 1: Rule Proposal**
- Business stakeholder or FIU analyst documents proposed rule in the HealthPay Rule Change Request (RCR) template in Jira.
- Proposal includes: rule objective, risk scenario addressed, proposed logic, expected alert volume, and initial thresholds.
- RCR is submitted to the Transaction Monitoring Governance Forum for initial review.

**Phase 2: Impact Analysis**
- FIU Director (or designee) conducts backtesting using 12 months of historical transaction data.
- Analysis includes: expected alert generation volume, projected FPR, resource impact on investigation team, and estimated detection coverage for target risk scenario.
- For model-based rules (ML models), MLOps Engineering provides additional technical feasibility assessment and computational resource requirements.

**Phase 3: Governance Approval**
- RCR with impact analysis presented to Transaction Monitoring Governance Forum.
- Forum members: VP of Financial Services, Chief Compliance Officer, Director of Financial Intelligence, VP of Engineering (David Park), and CISO (Rachel Kim) or delegate.
- Approval requires consensus; dissenting opinions are documented and escalated to CFO.

**Phase 4: Technical Implementation**
- Approved rules are implemented in HealthPay Guard TMS by MLOps Engineering or TMS Administrators.
- Rules are deployed to shadow mode for minimum 14 days to validate alert volumes against projections.
- Shadow mode results reviewed and signed off by Lead Investigator before production activation.

**Phase 5: Ongoing Monitoring**
- All active rules are subject to continuous performance monitoring per Section 7.
- Underperforming rules (FPR > 90% or TPR degradation > 25% from baseline) are flagged for immediate review.

#### 5.1.2 ML Model-Specific Requirements (SR 11-7)

For transaction monitoring functions implemented via machine learning models (currently: Healthcare Provider Fraud Detection Model v2.3, Anomalous Payment Pattern Model v1.8, Medical Lending Application Fraud Model v3.1), additional requirements apply:

| Requirement | Tier 1 (Provider Fraud) | Tier 2 (Anomalous Payment) | Tier 3 (Lending Fraud) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Independent Validation Frequency | Annual | Biennial | Every 3 years |
| CDMR Required | Yes – Full | Yes – Standard | Yes – Abbreviated |
| Concept Drift Monitoring | Continuous (Kubeflow) | Continuous (Kubeflow) | Monthly batch |
| Explainability Documentation | SHAP values for every alert | SHAP values for every alert | Feature importance report quarterly |
| Outcome Analysis | Monthly | Quarterly | Bi-annual |
| Champion/Challenger Testing | Required | Recommended | Optional |
| Validation by | External independent consultant or Internal Audit | MRM function (CAIO office) | MRM function (CAIO office) |

#### 5.1.3 Alert Threshold Configuration

Alert thresholds shall be configured in HealthPay Guard at three levels:

**Level 1 – Rules-Based Thresholds:**
- Amount-based: Single transaction > $50,000; Cumulative daily > $250,000; Cumulative weekly > $1,000,000.
- Velocity-based: >10 transactions from single account in 60 minutes; >50 transactions from single device ID in 24 hours.
- Geographic: Transactions originating from or directed to OFAC-sanctioned jurisdictions; high-risk corridors identified by FinCEN advisory.
- Pattern-based: Structuring detection (multiple transactions just below reporting threshold); rapid loan payoff and re-origination.

**Level 2 – Risk Score Thresholds:**
- HealthPay Guard assigns each transaction a composite risk score (0-1000) derived from multiple sub-models.
- P1 Alert: Score ≥ 850
- P2 Alert: Score 700-849
- P3 Alert: Score 500-699
- P4 Alert: Score 300-499 (informational; auto-escalate if customer is high-risk)

**Level 3 – Contextual Modifiers:**
- Customer risk rating (high/medium/low per CDD program).
- Product risk weighting (medical lending > claims automation > patient payments).
- Time-of-day anomalies (transactions outside business hours for provider type).
- Device and network fingerprint anomalies.

### 5.2 Alert Investigation Process

#### 5.2.1 Alert Triage (Initial Assessment)

Upon alert generation, the assigned Financial Crimes Investigator (rotating assignment via PagerDuty schedule) performs initial triage:

**Step 1: Alert Acknowledgment**
- Investigator acknowledges alert in HealthPay Guard within SLA window for alert priority.
- Acknowledgment timestamps are automatically recorded; failure to acknowledge within SLA triggers escalation to Lead Investigator.

**Step 2: Entity Identification**
- Investigator identifies all parties to the transaction: originating account, beneficiary account, provider (if applicable), patient/guarantor, and any intermediaries.
- Cross-reference against Meridian Customer Master (Snowflake) and watchlist screening results (automated via HealthPay Guard integration with LexisNexis).

**Step 3: Contextual Data Collection**
- Investigator gathers contextual data from source systems:
  - **From Snowflake:** 12-month transaction history for all involved parties, account age, product utilization patterns.
  - **From Salesforce:** Customer profile, KYC/CDD records, any prior SAR or alert history.
  - **From MedInsight Analytics (if PHI access authorized):** For provider-focused investigations, claims pattern data, peer comparison analytics (note: access requires documented business justification and CISO approval per HIPAA minimum necessary standard).

**Step 4: Preliminary Disposition**
- Investigator makes one of the following preliminary dispositions:
  - **Escalate to Full Investigation:** Sufficient indicators merit deeper review.
  - **False Positive – Close:** Alert clearly triggered by legitimate activity; documented rationale required.
  - **Consolidate with Existing Case:** Alert relates to entity or pattern already under investigation.

#### 5.2.2 Full Investigation Procedure

When an alert is escalated to full investigation:

**Step 1: Case Creation**
- Investigator creates a new Case record in HealthPay Guard Case Manager.
- Case is assigned a unique Case ID (format: HP-CASE-YYYY-NNNNNN).
- All associated alerts are linked to the case.
- Case classification is assigned: Potential Fraud | Potential Money Laundering | Potential Sanctions Violation | System Anomaly | Other.

**Step 2: Enhanced Due Diligence**
- Investigator conducts enhanced review of all involved parties:
  - Beneficial ownership verification (for entity customers).
  - Adverse media screening (via LexisNexis integration).
  - Social media and open-source intelligence (OSINT) where relevant, documented per Privacy Policy.
  - Prior SAR review: Has Meridian or any consortium partner filed on this entity?
- For medical lending cases: Review of loan application documentation, income verification, and provider relationship.

**Step 3: Transaction Pattern Analysis**
- Investigator uses HealthPay Guard visualization tools to map transaction flows.
- Identification of network relationships: common devices, IP addresses, banking destinations, or provider affiliations.
- Temporal pattern analysis: identify structuring patterns, velocity anomalies, or behavioral deviations from peer group.

**Step 4: Evidence Documentation**
- All evidence is documented in the Case Manager, including:
  - Screenshots with metadata (timestamp, source system).
  - Narrative summary of findings.
  - Links to source data in Snowflake (read-only query results preserved).
  - Chain of custody documentation.

**Step 5: Disposition Determination**

| Disposition | Criteria | Required Approvals |
|---|---|---|
| **No Further Action** | Investigation found no evidence of suspicious activity; legitimate business purpose confirmed. | Lead Investigator (Priya Narayan) |
| **Refer to SAR Committee** | Reasonable grounds to suspect suspicious activity meeting SAR filing thresholds. | Lead Investigator + Director of Financial Intelligence |
| **Refer to Law Enforcement** | Imminent threat, ongoing crime, or law enforcement inquiry. | Chief Compliance Officer + General Counsel |
| **Account Restriction** | Sufficient risk to warrant temporary restrictions pending investigation completion. | VP of Financial Services + CCO |
| **Model False Positive – Feedback** | Alert generated due to model error or rule logic flaw. | MLOps Engineering review |

#### 5.2.3 SAR Filing Process

When the SAR Committee determines that a Suspicious Activity Report is warranted:

**Step 1: SAR Committee Convening**
- Standing SAR Committee members: Chief Compliance Officer (Chair), Director of Financial Intelligence, General Counsel (Maria Gonzalez), VP of Financial Services, Lead Investigator (non-voting presenter).
- Committee may be convened asynchronously via secure Slack channel (#sar-committee) for urgent matters, or via scheduled weekly review for non-urgent cases.
- Quorum requires CCO plus two other voting members.

**Step 2: SAR Narrative Preparation**
- Lead Investigator prepares SAR narrative using the standard Meridian SAR Template.
- Narrative includes: subject identification, suspicious activity description, transaction details, involved accounts, and basis for suspicion.
- Narrative reviewed by Director of Financial Intelligence and General Counsel for legal sufficiency before filing.

**Step 3: SAR Filing**
- SAR is filed electronically through the FinCEN BSA E-Filing System.
- Filing deadline: 30 calendar days from initial detection (may be extended to 60 days if suspect identification requires additional time).
- HealthPay Guard records the SAR filing reference number, filing date, and filer identity.
- SAR filing is logged in the Confidential Regulatory Filings Register (access restricted to CCO, GC, and SAR Committee members).

**Step 4: Post-Filing Actions**
- Any account restrictions or enhanced monitoring placed on subject entities.
- 90-day follow-up review scheduled; continuing suspicious activity may require supplemental SAR.
- SAR information is NOT disclosed to the subject of the SAR (prohibited under BSA "no tipping off" provisions).

#### 5.2.4 Case Closure and Retention

**Closure Requirements:**
- All investigative steps documented in Case Manager.
- Disposition rationale clearly articulated.
- Quality review completed by Lead Investigator or designee (100% review for P1/P2 cases; 20% random sampling for P3; 5% for P4).
- All evidence artifacts attached to case record.

**Retention:**
- Case records retained for 7 years from date of closure per regulatory requirements.
- SAR filings and supporting documentation retained for 7 years from filing date.
- Records are archived to AWS S3 Glacier Deep Archive after 2 years; retrievable within 48 hours.

### 5.3 Threshold Tuning and Model Optimization

#### 5.3.1 Scheduled Tuning Cycle

The FIU conducts a comprehensive threshold tuning review quarterly:

| Q1 (Jan-Feb) | Q2 (Apr-May) | Q3 (Jul-Aug) | Q4 (Oct-Nov) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full rules inventory review | Anomalous Payment Model tuning | Provider Fraud Model tuning | Lending Fraud Model tuning |
| Alert volume trend analysis | SAR conversion rate analysis | Peer benchmarking | Annual program effectiveness review |

#### 5.3.2 Tuning Procedure

**Step 1: Data Preparation**
- Extract 12-month alert and investigation data from HealthPay Guard.
- Calculate per-rule metrics: alert volume, FPR, TPR, SAR conversion rate, investigator time-per-alert.
- Segment analysis by alert priority, customer segment, and product type.

**Step 2: Threshold Adjustment Analysis**
- For each rule exceeding FPR threshold (90%), analyze score distributions to identify optimal cutoff adjustment.
- Simulate threshold changes using historical data to project impact on detection coverage and alert volumes.
- Document proposed adjustments with supporting analysis in the Threshold Tuning Workbook (maintained in SharePoint).

**Step 3: Governance Review and Approval**

| Adjustment Magnitude | Approval Authority |
|---|---|
| Minor (<10% threshold shift, no expected detection impact) | Director of Financial Intelligence |
| Moderate (10-25% threshold shift) | VP of Financial Services |
| Significant (>25% threshold shift or removal of detection coverage) | Transaction Monitoring Governance Forum |

**Step 4: Implementation and Validation**
- Approved threshold changes implemented in HealthPay Guard by TMS Administrator.
- 30-day post-implementation review conducted to validate projections.
- Changes documented in the Transaction Monitoring Change Log.

#### 5.3.3 Emergency Threshold Adjustment

In the event of a critical system issue (e.g., alert storm causing operational paralysis, critical false negative identified), the VP of Financial Services or CCO may authorize emergency threshold suspension:

1. Emergency authorization via phone/chat with documented rationale within 24 hours.
2. Emergency change logged; post-implementation review required within 5 business days.
3. Full governance review and formal approval at next Transaction Monitoring Governance Forum.

## 6. Controls and Safeguards

### 6.1 Access Controls

Access to the HealthPay Guard Transaction Monitoring System is governed by the principle of least privilege and role-based access controls (RBAC):

| Role | HealthPay Guard Permissions | Provisioning Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Financial Crimes Investigator | View alerts, create/update cases, view transaction data (masked PHI unless elevated access granted), run reports | Director of Financial Intelligence |
| Lead Investigator | All Investigator permissions + case approval, quality review, investigator assignment | Director of Financial Intelligence + CISO |
| FIU Director | All permissions + rule configuration, threshold adjustment, SAR committee participation, user provisioning | CISO |
| MLOps Engineer | Model deployment, performance monitoring, technical configuration (no access to case data or investigative findings) | VP of Engineering + CISO |
| Compliance Officer | Read-only access to all dashboards, case records, SAR filing history | CCO + CISO |
| Internal Auditor | Time-limited read-only access for audit purposes, coordinated through CISO | CISO (upon Internal Audit request) |
| System Administrator | Full technical administration (no business access to investigate or disposition cases) | VP of IT Operations (Samantha Torres) |

**Access Provisioning Process:**
1. Access request submitted through Okta workflow with manager approval.
2. CISO or designee reviews and approves based on documented business need.
3. Role provisioning is automated through Okta SCIM integration.
4. Access is reviewed when employees change roles or departments.

### 6.2 Segregation of Duties

The following segregation of duties controls are enforced programmatically within HealthPay Guard:

- **Model Developers cannot disposition alerts** generated by models they developed. (Enforced by RBAC; MLOps Engineers lack case management permissions.)
- **Investigators cannot tune rules** or adjust thresholds. (Enforced by RBAC; configuration permissions restricted to FIU Director role.)
- **SAR Committee Members cannot investigate their own referrals.** (Enforced by workflow; system prevents case assignment to committee members.)
- **Quality Reviewers cannot review their own cases.** (Enforced by workflow assignment logic.)

### 6.3 Data Protection and Privacy

- **Encryption:** All transaction data, alert records, and case files are encrypted at rest using AWS KMS with customer-managed keys. Data in transit is encrypted using TLS 1.3.
- **Data Minimization:** Investigative case records shall contain only the minimum necessary PHI required to complete the investigation. Full PHI access beyond masked fields requires documented justification and CISO approval.
- **Audit Logging:** All access to case records, alert dispositions, and SAR filing records is logged to an immutable audit trail in Datadog. Logs are retained for 365 days online and 7 years in archival storage.
- **Cross-Border Data Transfer:** Investigative data involving EU data subjects is processed in the AWS eu-west-1 region. Cross-border transfers are governed by Meridian's Standard Contractual Clauses and GDPR compliance framework (SOP-PRIV-007).

### 6.4 Change Management

All changes to transaction monitoring rules, models, alert thresholds, or system configuration are subject to the Meridian Change Management Policy (SOP-IT-005):

- Changes are documented in Jira with risk assessment and rollback plan.
- Changes to Tier 1 or Tier 2 models require Model Risk Governance Committee approval.
- Emergency changes follow the expedited process documented in Section 5.3.3.
- All production changes require peer review before deployment.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Exception Types

| Exception Category | Description | Approval Authority |
|---|---|---|
| **SLA Exception** | Inability to meet alert investigation SLA due to volume spikes or resource constraints | VP Financial Services (pre-approved surge capacity protocol; post-hoc notification for unanticipated volumes) |
| **Access Exception** | Temporary elevation of data access (e.g., unmasked PHI required for complex investigation) | CISO + Chief Privacy Officer |
| **Rule Suspension** | Emergency deactivation of monitoring rule due to alert storm or system performance impact | VP Financial Services + CCO (Section 5.3.3) |
| **Model Deferral** | Postponement of scheduled model validation due to resource constraints | Chief AI Officer (max 90-day deferral; one-time only per model) |
| **SAR Filing Extension** | Extension beyond 30-day regulatory filing deadline for complex cases | CCO (documents justification; files within 60-day statutory maximum) |

### 8.2 Escalation Protocol

Escalations shall follow a tiered path based on severity and urgency:

**Tier 1 – Operational Escalation (Business Hours):**
1. Investigator identifies issue → Notifies Lead Investigator via Slack #fiu-ops.
2. Lead Investigator assesses and resolves or escalates to Director FIU within SLA timeframe.
3. Director FIU escalates to VP Financial Services if issue impacts program effectiveness.

**Tier 2 – Urgent Escalation (After Hours / Weekends):**
1. On-call investigator contacts Lead Investigator via PagerDuty.
2. Lead Investigator escalates to Director FIU (Interim: Robert Liu) via phone.
3. Director FIU determines if CCO or executive notification is required.

**Tier 3 – Executive Escalation:**
1. VP Financial Services and CCO notified immediately for any of the following:
   - Suspected internal fraud or employee malfeasance.
   - Law enforcement contact (subpoena, search warrant, inquiry).
   - System compromise or data breach affecting investigation integrity.
   - Material regulatory concern or examination finding.
2. General Counsel (Maria Gonzalez) notified for any matter with legal implications.
3. CISO (Rachel Kim) notified for any matter with security implications.

### 8.3 Exception Documentation

All exceptions shall be documented in the Exception Register (maintained in HealthPay Guard or SharePoint) with:
- Exception type and category.
- Date of occurrence and expected resolution date.
- Detailed business justification.
- Approving authority.
- Mitigating controls in place during exception period.
- Root cause analysis (if applicable).
- Lessons learned and preventive actions.

The Exception Register is reviewed quarterly by the Transaction Monitoring Governance Forum.
