# Standard Operating Procedure: Data Breach Response and Notification
**SOP-DGP-010 | Version 5.3**

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish a standardized, enterprise-wide framework for the detection, investigation, containment, notification, and post-incident remediation of data breaches involving Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. (“Meridian”) information assets. This SOP ensures that Meridian meets its legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations across all operating jurisdictions, with particular emphasis on the comprehensive requirements of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the breach notification obligations under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), and the security incident response criteria specified within the System and Organization Controls (SOC 2) framework. This document provides the operational blueprint for Meridian’s coordinated response to events that compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of protected data.

### 1.2 Scope

#### 1.2.1 In-Scope Entities and Personnel
This SOP applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, interns, and third-party service providers (collectively "Personnel") who have access to or manage Meridian Information Systems, including those within the following business lines and support functions:
- Clinical AI Platform
- HealthPay Financial Services
- MedInsight Analytics
- Meridian SaaS Platform
- Information Technology and Security Operations
- Legal and Compliance
- Human Resources
- Customer Operations

#### 1.2.2 In-Scope Data and Systems
This SOP governs all incidents involving:
- **Protected Health Information (PHI)** as defined by HIPAA, processed across the MedInsight Analytics platform and Clinical AI Platform.
- **Personal Data** as defined by GDPR, Article 4(1), pertaining to identified or identifiable natural persons located in the European Economic Area (EEA), including patients, healthcare provider staff, and Meridian employees based in the EU.
- **Financial Services Data** subject to SR 11-7 and Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) safeguards, processed by HealthPay Financial Services.
- **Meridian Confidential Information**, including proprietary algorithms, source code, and business strategy documents.
- **System Logs and Audit Trails** generated by the Meridian SaaS Platform, hosted on AWS (us-east-1, eu-west-1) and Azure (DR).

#### 1.2.3 Out of Scope
- Routine security events that do not result in unauthorized access, acquisition, or disclosure of data (e.g., unsuccessful port scans, blocked phishing attempts).
- Service disruptions caused by planned maintenance where no data compromise occurs.

## 4. Policy Statements

4.1 **Zero-Delay Escalation:** Any Meridian Personnel who becomes aware of a suspected or confirmed data breach must report the incident via the official PagerDuty security alert channel or the Meridian Ethics & Security Hotline within 15 minutes of discovery. Telephone cascades are not a substitute for a PagerDuty ticket.

4.2 **Preservation First:** Upon suspicion of a breach, the integrity of affected systems shall be preserved immediately. Virtual machine snapshots, container state captures, and full memory dumps shall be taken before any containment action that might alter volatile data.

4.3 **GDPR Accountability:** Meridian adheres to the principle of accountability under GDPR Article 5(2). All breach response activities shall be documented to demonstrate compliance. The DPO shall maintain a comprehensive Breach Register.

4.4 **Confidential Attorney-Client Privilege:** All root cause analysis documents generated post-incident shall be marked “Privileged and Confidential Attorney Work Product” and routed through the Office of the General Counsel to maximize legal protection.

4.5 **No Retaliation:** Meridian prohibits retaliation against any Personnel who reports a data breach in good faith, including breaches caused by their own inadvertent error, in accordance with whistleblower protections.

4.6 **Sub-processor Integration:** Pursuant to GDPR Article 28, any breach at a sub-processor (e.g., AWS, Snowflake) must be reported to Meridian without undue delay. This SOP governs Meridian’s subsequent handling and notification obligations upon receiving such a report.

4.7 **SOC 2 Change Management:** All modifications to the incident response process or the underlying systems during containment and remediation must follow a documented change request, acknowledging the need for expedited response while maintaining traceability.

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Phase 1: Detection and Intake (0-2 Hours)

#### 5.1.1 Discovery Channels
Events may be discovered through one of the following channels, all of which aggregate into the Datadog SIEM dashboard:
- **Automated Alerts:** CrowdStrike Falcon, AWS GuardDuty, Okta Impossible Travel, Datadog Log Anomaly Detection, LangSmith prompt injection detection (AI systems).
- **Manual Reports:** Personnel reports via PagerDuty, email to security@meridian.health, or the Ethics Hotline (managed by NAVEX Global).
- **External Notifications:** Sub-processor alerts (AWS Health Dashboard, Azure Service Health), partner notifications, or direct communication from a Data Protection Authority.

#### 5.1.2 Triage Procedure (Level 1 ISIRT Analyst)
1.  **Acknowledge Alert:** Acknowledge the PagerDuty alert within 5 minutes of dispatch. If the alert fires against an AI model drift monitor (LangSmith), also tag the AI Governance Committee channel.
2.  **Validate Incident:** Distinguish between a false positive (e.g., authorized penetration test, known system noise) and a genuine security event.
3.  **Create Incident Record:** Open an incident in Jira Service Management under the project `SECIR`. The ticket must have the format `SECIR-YYYY-MM-DD-NNN`.
4.  **Initial Severity Classification:** Assign a preliminary Severity Level (S1-S4) based on the following criteria:

| Severity | Criteria | Data Volume Threshold | Regulatory Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S1 – Critical** | Active lateral movement, ransomware deployment, exfiltration of >1 GB, breach of PHI > 500 individuals, breach of GDPR Special Categories of Data (Art. 9). | > 1,000 Records | High risk to data subject rights and freedoms. GDPR Art. 34 notification likely. |
| **S2 – High** | Confirmed unauthorized access to a database partition, exfiltration detected, Business Associate compromise. | 100 – 1,000 Records | Risk present. Art. 33 notification to DPA likely. |
| **S3 – Medium** | Malware infection on a single endpoint containing mixed data, lost/stolen encrypted corporate laptop with PHI. | < 100 Records | Low risk if encryption key is managed by HSM. |
| **S4 – Low** | Unauthorized access by an employee to data outside their role (insider snooping), lost/stolen device with no PHI/PII. | < 10 Records | Unlikely to result in high risk. |

### 5.2 Phase 2: Containment and Evidence Preservation (Parallel with Phase 3)

#### 5.2.1 Short-Term Containment (Operations Team)
- **Network Isolation:** If a specific instance is compromised, the VP of IT Operations may order immediate network segmentation via AWS Security Group modification.
- **Account Disablement:** Okta push MFA shall be used to lock suspect user accounts. Active Directory objects must be disabled, not deleted.
- **System Shutdown (Last Resort):** Powering off a system is only permitted with explicit approval from the CISO due to loss of forensic artifacts. If approved, capture a snapshot of the volatile state using `Velociraptor` or AWS EBS Snapshot before shutdown.

#### 5.2.2 Forensic Acquisition (VP of IT Operations / Engineering)
1.  **Order of Volatility:** Collect evidence following the IETF RFC 3227 order: (1) Registers/Cache, (2) Routing Table/ARP Cache, (3) Temporary Files, (4) Disk, (5) Backup Media.
2.  **Chain of Custody:** A Form DGP-010-F1 (Digital Chain of Custody) must be initiated in DocuSign for every piece of evidence collected. The form must be shared with the OCI.
3.  **AWS Forensics:** For S3 bucket data breaches, enable S3 Object Lock with legal hold on the affected bucket. Do not rotate or delete CloudTrail logs; export them to a segregated, immutable Glacier Vault account.

### 5.3 Phase 3: Assessment and Risk Analysis

#### 5.3.1 Data Inventory and Mapping
The DPO, in collaboration with the Data Governance team, shall execute the following within 24 hours:
- **Data Classification Audit:** Identify the exact tables, JSON schemas, or EHR fields compromised. Reference the data catalog in Collibra.
- **Data Subject Volume:** Execute a distinct count query. For GDPR purposes, this must distinguish between EU data subjects and other nationalities.
- **Cross-Border Transfer Impact:** Determine if the breach affects data transferred under Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs).

#### 5.3.2 GDPR Article 33 Assessment (The 72-Hour Clock)
**Critical Time Marker:** The 72-hour notification window to the competent Supervisory Authority (SA) begins from the moment "awareness" is established. Meridian defines “awareness” as the moment a Level 1 ISIRT Analyst confirms a data breach and changes the Jira ticket status to "Confirmed Breach."

1.  **Nature of the Breach:** Document categories of personal data affected (e.g., name, MRN, diagnosis, genetic data).
2.  **DPO Contact Point:** Dr. Klaus Weber, DPO, klaus.weber@meridian.health, +49 30 123 4567.
3.  **Likely Consequences:**
    - **High Risk Scenario:** If the breach includes Special Category Data (Art. 9) such as genomic data from the Clinical AI Platform OR financial data enabling identity fraud (HealthPay), the risk is automatically deemed “High.”
    - **Low Risk Scenario:** Data encrypted with AES-256-GCM where the key was not compromised (confirmed by HashiCorp Vault audit logs).
4.  **Measures Taken:** Document all steps from Sections 5.2 and 5.3.

**Interim Notification (Art. 33(4)):** If the full information is not available within 72 hours, Meridian shall file an initial notification containing the partial information. The phased notification must be sent, and the delay must be justified. The General Counsel and DPO must co-sign this interim filing.

#### 5.3.3 Notification Decision Matrix (GDPR Art. 34)

| Risk Level (Art. 34) | Duty to Notify Data Subject? | Notification Deadline | Method of Communication |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Risk** | **Yes, without undue delay.** | ≤ 24 hours from DPA notification (unless law enforcement requests delay). | Proactive direct communication (email, SMS, in-app notification). If direct contact is disproportionate, a public communication notice on meridian.health/news. |
| **Medium/Low Risk** | **Not required** unless the DPA orders it, but DPO may recommend voluntary notification for transparency. | N/A | N/A |
| **Risk Mitigated** | **No.** (e.g., exfiltrated data was encrypted with a perfectly secured key). | N/A | N/A |

**Template Execution:** Notifications must be drafted using the GDPR Notification Template (Appendix B) and merged via Twilio SendGrid.

### 5.4 Phase 4: Eradication and Recovery

#### 5.4.1 Vulnerability Remediation
The VP of Engineering must implement permanent fixes. This may include:
- Patching zero-day vulnerabilities.
- Rotating all secrets stored in the compromised context via HashiCorp Vault Dynamic Secrets.
- Decommissioning or re-imaging compromised AMIs.

#### 5.4.2 SOC 2 Change Management Procedure
All remediation actions that alter the production environment (e.g., re-architecting a VPC, upgrading database versions) require a change request in Jira Service Management.
- **Emergency Path:** Changes can be executed immediately if approved by the CISO. Post-implementation documentation, including a full risk assessment, must be completed within 24 hours.
- **Note:** The Developer submitting the code remediation path is authorized to approve the emergency deployment of that code upon production review by the VP of Engineering.

#### 5.4.3 System Validation
Before returning to operation:
1.  Run the full CrowdStrike Falcon scan on all recovered instances.
2.  Validate data integrity against the last known clean blockchain-anchored snapshot from Snowflake Time Travel.
3.  The CISO grants formal "Return to Service" approval via a status update in the #brit-ops Slack channel.

### 5.5 Phase 5: Notification Execution

#### 5.5.1 HIPAA Notification Procedure
In the event of a breach involving PHI, the notification process shall follow the timeline requirements established by regulation. Meridian shall notify Covered Entities within a timeframe that permits them to meet their own regulatory obligations. The Customer Operations team, led by Michael Chang, shall leverage Salesforce Health Cloud to segment impacted customers and send BAA-compliant notifications through DocuSign CLM. All correspondence is stored as a record of notification.

#### 5.5.2 State-Level Breach Notification
In addition to federal requirements, the legal team must consult the U.S. state breach notification statutes (referencing the IAPP US State Breach Law Map). Notification timelines and content requirements (including credit monitoring offers) must be customized per state. Where HealthPay data is involved, supplementary notifications to payment card networks may be required per contract.

#### 5.5.3 Media and Press Statements
If the breach impacts more than 500 individuals in a single state, a media statement is required. Meridian’s external PR firm of record (Edelman) shall draft the release. The release must not contain specifics that could further endanger the affected system (e.g., specific open ports or unpatched software versions).

### 5.6 Phase 6: Post-Incident Activity

#### 5.6.1 After-Action Review (AAR)
Within 14 calendar days of breach closure, the CISO shall facilitate a blameless post-mortem meeting with all core BIRT members. The output is a formal After-Action Review Report (Appendix D) containing:
- **Chronological Timeline:** A minute-by-minute account.
- **Root Cause Analysis:** 5 Whys or Ishikawa diagram.
- **Impact Assessment:** Actual harm vs. estimated risk.
- **Process Gaps:** Failures in the IRP itself.

#### 5.6.2 Continuous Improvement
The After-Action Review report outcomes shall be fed into the Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) log in Jira. Action items are assigned to the SOC 2 Steering Committee for tracking until closure.

## 6. Controls and Safeguards

### 6.1 Technical Controls
| Control ID | Control Description | Technology Provider |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **T-01** | **Data-at-Rest Encryption:** All data in scope is encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM. Database writes are encrypted via AWS KMS Customer Managed Keys (CMKs). | AWS KMS, HashiCorp Vault Transit Engine |
| **T-02** | **Data-in-Transit Encryption:** All inter-service communication within the Meridian Mesh requires TLS 1.3. mTLS is enforced between critical health data endpoints. | Istio Service Mesh, Let’s Encrypt (public), Meridian Private CA (internal) |
| **T-03** | **Immutable Logging:** AWS CloudTrail logs are stored centrally in a dedicated AWS Organizations account. Object Lock governance mode applied for legally required retention periods. | AWS CloudTrail, Glacier, CrowdStrike Falcon Discover |
| **T-04** | **DLP (Pre-Exfiltration):** Netskope Cloud DLP blocks outbound uploads of structured datasets containing patterns matching SNOMED-CT codes and MRNs unless encrypted under a managed key. | Netskope, AWS Macie (classification) |
| **T-05** | **AI-Specific Guardrails:** LangSmith traces are monitored for prompt injection patterns. Clinical AI model weights are stored encrypted in AWS SageMaker, access requires JIT credential brokering. | LangSmith, SageMaker, Okta Ascend |
| **T-06** | **SOC 2 Monitoring Infrastructure:** The SaaS platform infrastructure health and security events are continuously monitored. Dashboards are displayed in the SOC. | Datadog, PagerDuty |

### 6.2 Administrative Controls
| Control ID | Control Description |
| :--- | :--- |

| **A-02** | **Vendor Risk Management:** All sub-processors handling PHI or PII must hold SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001 certifications. Evidence is tracked in OneTrust VRM. |

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Policy Exceptions
Exceptions to this SOP (e.g., delaying containment for active intelligence-gathering by the FBI) require approval via the Risk Acceptance form in LogicGate Risk Cloud. Exceptions must specify the duration and mitigating controls. The DPO and CISO must jointly approve any exception that impacts GDPR Article 33 notification timelines.

### 8.2 Notification Escalation Path
If the DPO determines that the Article 33 notification to the Berlin Data Protection Authority (lead supervisory authority) may be delayed, the following escalation applies:
1.  **Step 1 (Hour 68):** DPO notifies Maria Gonzalez (GC) and Thomas Anderson (CCO).
2.  **Step 2 (Hour 70):** GC notifies Dr. Sarah Chen (CEO) and the Board’s Audit Committee Chair.
3.  **Step 3 (Hour 72):** CEO authorizes filing of interim incomplete report with the Berlin Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (BlnBDI), citing the specific operational barriers.

### 8.3 Disputed Notifications
If a BIRT member disputes the risk assessment (e.g., Engineering believes data is provably encrypted and poses no risk, while Compliance demands notification), the GC acts as the tie-breaker. The decision and the dissenting opinion are recorded in the Jira ticket for audit purposes.
