# Standard Operating Procedure: Data Retention and Disposal

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the unified framework for the retention, preservation, and secure disposal of all Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. ("Meridian") data assets across all business units and geographies. The purpose is to ensure compliance with applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations while minimizing data storage costs and risk. This SOP operationalizes the data lifecycle principles of the Meridian Data Governance Framework, specifically the requirements for data minimization and storage limitation.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all data created, received, processed, stored, or transmitted by Meridian in any format, whether electronic or physical, and regardless of location. This includes, but is not limited to:

- **Structured Data:** Production databases (Snowflake, PostgreSQL), data warehouses, data marts, and analytical data stores supporting the Meridian SaaS Platform, Clinical AI Platform, HealthPay Financial Services, and MedInsight Analytics.
- **Unstructured Data:** Documents, spreadsheets, presentations, emails, images (including DICOM files), audio, video, and logs stored on Meridian systems, including AWS S3 buckets, network file shares, and local workstations.
- **System Data:** System logs, application logs, network logs, security event logs, and observability data within Datadog and PagerDuty.
- **Physical Records:** Paper files, printed images, contracts, and hardware containing persistent storage (e.g., hard drives, SSDs, backup tapes).
- **Backups and Archives:** All data copies maintained for business continuity and disaster recovery purposes in AWS (primary, us-east-1, eu-west-1) and Azure (DR).
- **AI/ML Artifacts:** Training datasets, validation datasets, model weights, inference logs, embeddings in Pinecone, and outputs generated by Clinical AI Platform and MedInsight Analytics models.

### 1.3 Out of Scope
This SOP does not cover the retention of intellectual property related to source code, which is governed by the Engineering Lifecycle Policy (SOP-ENG-015). However, the data processed *by* that source code is within scope.

### 1.4 Applicability
This SOP applies to all Meridian employees, contractors, temporary workers, interns, and third-party vendors who access, manage, or control Meridian data. Compliance with this SOP is a condition of employment and contractual engagement. Non-compliance will be managed per the Disciplinary Action Policy (SOP-HR-008).

## 2. Definitions and Acronyms

| Term | Definition |

| **Retention Period** | The specific, pre-defined period of time during which a specific category of data must be preserved in its original format and accessible. |
| **Retention Schedule** | The master document that categorizes all Meridian data types and assigns their specific retention periods and disposal methods. |
| **Secure Disposal** | The definitive destruction of data using a method approved in Section 5.5 of this SOP. |
| **Storage Limitation** | The principle that personal data shall be kept in a form which permits identification of data subjects for no longer than is necessary (GDPR Art. 5(1)(e)). |

## 4. Policy Statements

4.  **Data Classification First:** All data shall be classified by the responsible Data Owner upon creation or acquisition according to the Data Classification Standard (SOP-DGP-001). No data shall be retained without an assigned classification and disposal date.
5.  **Legal Compliance:** Meridian shall retain all Personal Data and PHI for the shortest time necessary to fulfill the specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes for which it was processed. No personal data shall be retained solely on the basis that it *may* have value in the future.
6.  **Defined Retention Schedule:** All Meridian data shall be managed per the Master Retention Schedule (Appendix A). Ad hoc or indefinite data retention is strictly prohibited.
7.  **Legal Holds Supersede Disposal:** Upon issuance of a Legal Hold by General Counsel, all scheduled disposal for the specified data is immediately and indefinitely suspended. Disposal shall resume only upon written release from the General Counsel.
8.  **Prohibition of Logical Deletion:** The act of "deleting" a record via application soft-delete functionality (i.e., setting a deleted_flag = TRUE) is explicitly prohibited as a final disposal action. Logical deletion constitutes retention. All disposal actions must be physical and irreversible.
9.  **Segregation of Duties:** No single individual may classify data, approve an exception to its retention period, and execute its final disposal. A minimum of two authorized roles is required to approve and execute a destruction process for any data set containing PD or PHI.
10. **Privacy by Design:** All new systems and third-party data ingest pipelines must implement technical enforcement of the retention schedule prior to promotion to Production. This is a Go/No-Go criteria for the Architecture Review Board (SOP-ENG-003).

## 5. Detailed Procedures

This section outlines the step-by-step operational workflow for managing the data lifecycle, from initial classification through to certified disposal.

### 5.1 Data Classification and Schedule Assignment
The basis for a defensible disposal program is accurate, consistent classification at the point of creation.

1.  **Data Creation/Acquisition:** A Data Owner (VP or Director) identifies a new data source or dataset during a project kickoff (e.g., a new MedInsight Analytics report pulling from a Snowflake table, a new Clinical AI model training dataset from an AWS S3 bucket).
2.  **Classification Form Submission:** The Data Owner, or their designated delegate, completes the "Data Asset Registration & Classification" form within the Data Governance module of ServiceNow. The form requires:
    - **Data Asset Name & Description:** (e.g., "MedInsight Chronic Condition Cohort - US-East-1").
    - **System of Record:** The authoritative system (e.g., Snowflake, AWS S3).
    - **Data Classification:** (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted) per SOP-DGP-001.
    - **Regulatory Tagging:** Mandatory multi-select tags (GDPR, HIPAA, CCPA, MDR, SOX, None). If "None" is selected, a 250-character written justification is required.
    - **Proposed Retention:** Data Owner proposes a retention period based on the "Meridian Standard Justifications" table (below). The proposal must be tied to a specific business or regulatory justification, not a generic "might be needed."

**Table 5-A: Meridian Standard Justifications for Retention**

| Justification ID | Justification Type | Example Reasoning | Maximum Default Period (No Exception) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| J-REG-01 | Statutory Requirement | MDR requires implantable device data retention for 15 years post-commercialization. | As specified by statute + 1 year audit buffer. |
| J-REG-02 | HIPAA Accounting | HIPAA §164.528 requires accounting of disclosures for 6 years. | 7 years from date of creation. |
| J-BIZ-01 | Active Business Process | HealthPay transaction data needed for client billing reconciliation. | Term of active contract + 3 years. |
| J-BIZ-02 | Software Lifecycle | Application-level debug logs for a major release. | 180 days post-release. |
| J-LEG-01 | Statute of Limitations | General commercial litigation risk in Massachusetts. | State statute of limitations (6 years) + 2 years. |

3.  **Automated Validation:** Upon form submission, ServiceNow performs a real-time lookup against the **Master Retention Schedule (MRS)** [See Appendix A]. If the proposed retention period exceeds the MRS default for the assigned regulatory tags by more than 12 months, the workflow automatically escalates to the DPO Team for review.
4.  **DPO Approval:** A DPO Analyst (Dr. Weber's team) reviews the escalation. They will either:
    - **Approve with Justification:** Approve the non-standard period, documenting the specific legal or business rationale in the audit log.
    - **Reject and Reset:** Reject the proposal and reset the retention period to the MRS default, notifying the Data Owner with a 3-business-day SLA for resolution.
5.  **Final Cataloging:** Once approved, the data asset is registered in the Meridian Data Catalog (Alation). Alation automatically maps the new asset to its applicable disposal schedule and tags it with a `auto_disposal_eligible` flag of `True` or `False` based on regulatory constraints.

### 5.2 Ad-Hoc Physical Record Disposal
For personnel who maintain physical records (e.g., HR contracts, signed business agreements).

1.  **Designated Collection Point:** Personnel do not dispose of physical records containing Confidential or Restricted data in standard recycle bins. Secure physical collection consoles (Shred-it, model BIN-1000) are installed in the Legal, HR, and Finance departments.
2.  **Console Deposit:** A Meridian employee places the document(s) into the locked console. The employee logs the action by keying in their employee ID on the console's digital panel and selecting the data classification level.
3.  **Vendor Collection Cycle:** The approved vendor (Iron Mountain, Contract #IM-2023-8891) performs a secure collection every 21 calendar days. For bins reaching 80% capacity, a sensor triggers an ad-hoc collection request via the IT Infrastructure team.
4.  **Mass Destruction Event:** Annually, during the "Clean Desk and Data January" initiative, IT Infrastructure deploys high-volume 96-gallon secure destruction carts to each floor. The process for depositing documents is identical, using personal access fobs for identification.

### 5.3 Legal Hold (Litigation Hold) Procedure
This procedure suspends all other procedures in this SOP.

1.  **Trigger and Hold Notification (General Counsel):** Upon receiving notice of pending or reasonably foreseeable litigation, Maria Gonzalez (General Counsel) or her designated Deputy creates a "Legal Hold" case in the Legal Hold Management System (Exterro Legal Hold).
2.  **Custodian Identification:** The Legal Hold notice identifies specific custodians (named employees) and specific data assets (e.g., "All emails of Jane Doe, VP of Clinical Products, from 2024-01-01 to 2026-01-15"; "All data in Snowflake schema `CLINICAL_TRIAL_ATHENA_002`").
3.  **Automated Custodian Lock:** Upon issuance, Exterro sends an automated notification to the custodian and their direct manager. The notice explicitly states the scope of the hold and the prohibition against deletion, modification, or disposal of any covered data, regardless of scheduled disposition.
4.  **Technical Hold Enforcement (IT Infrastructure):**
    - **Microsoft 365:** Exterro API call triggers an in-place preservation hold on the custodian's Exchange Online mailbox and OneDrive for Business account.
    - **Snowflake:** Exterro API instructs the data governance integration layer to temporarily revoke the `DROP` and data masking lifecycle privileges on the specified schemas and tables, changing the disposition from `auto_disposal_eligible: True` to `Legal Hold: True`.
    - **AWS S3:** Exterro applies a S3 Object Legal Hold (via `s3:PutObjectLegalHold` IAM action) on the specified S3 buckets or prefixes. This governance mode prevents any user, including the root account, from deleting the object version until the hold is removed.
    - **Backups:** IT Infrastructure immediately places all relevant backup tapes/volumes into a "legal hold" pool separate from the standard rotation, ensuring they are not overwritten.
5.  **Custodian Acknowledgement:** Within 5 business days, each named custodian must electronically acknowledge receipt and understanding of the hold notice in Exterro. A 48-hour reminder is sent; failure to acknowledge within 7 calendar days triggers an automated notification to the General Counsel and the custodian's VP.

### 5.4 Operational Retention Enforcement
Daily and weekly automated processes ensure the schedule is technically enforced.

- **Auto-Indexing:** Every Sunday at 01:00 UTC, the Data Catalog (Alation) scans all snowflake tables and S3 prefixes. It compares the `last_accessed_date` and `creation_date` against the assigned MRS retention period. Data sets that have met or exceeded their retention period and are tagged `auto_disposal_eligible: True` are automatically moved to a quarantine bucket (`s3://meridian-data-quarantine-us-east-1`) with a 14-calendar-day "soft recovery" window.
- **14-Day Quarantine and Final Notice:** Upon movement to quarantine, a task is auto-generated in Jira Service Management and assigned to the Data Owner. The task states: "Data asset [name] has reached its retention horizon and is in a 14-day recovery window. This asset will be irreversibly destroyed on [date]. If you require retention extension, you must file a formal exception under Section 8. No action confirms acceptance of disposal."
- **Final Irreversible Disposal:** If no approved exception is granted by the end of the 14-day window, the quarantine process automatically triggers the certified disposal procedure (Section 5.5).

### 5.5 Secure Disposal and Certificate of Destruction
This is the final, irreversible step.

1.  **Disposal Method Selection:** The IT Infrastructure execution script, informed by the Snowflake/AWS tags, selects the appropriate NIST 800-88 Rev. 1 compliant disposal method from Table 5-B.
2.  **Two-Person Integrity Rule:** Execution of a disposal script against any production data tagged as `Confidential` or `Restricted` requires simultaneous command execution authorization by two separate individuals: one from IT Infrastructure and one from InfoSec. Both must enter their unique HSM-backed disposal authorization key. This process is conducted live, on a screen shared via a recorded Zoom session.
3.  **Execution and Verification:** The method is applied. For crypto-erasure, the key manager (AWS KMS or HashiCorp Vault) is used to securely delete the customer-managed key (CMK). After execution, an attempt is made to read the data. A confirmation of "Access Denied: Key Not Found" or "Object Not Found" is the required successful result.
4.  **Certificate of Destruction (CoD) Generation:** Once verification is complete, the IT Infrastructure automated system generates a Code of Destruction. This is a digitally signed, immutable PDF stored in the Meridian Audit Vault (Amazon S3 Glacier Deep Archive, access restricted to GC, CPO, and CISO). The CoD must contain all fields listed in Table 5-C.

**Table 5-B: Approved Disposal Methods**

| Media / Data Type | Approved Method (NIST 800-88) | Standard | Verification Mechanism |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| AWS S3 Standard / IA | Crypto Erasure (Secure deletion of the envelope-wrapped key via AWS KMS) | Clear/Purge | Confirm inability to decrypt, then delete object version. |
| Snowflake DB Tables | Crypto Shredding (Secure deletion of underlying table encryption keys via Snowflake key hierarchy) | Purge | Query metadata layer for "Table Dropped; Recovery Key Expunged." |
| AWS EBS Volumes (EC2) | Physical Destruction of underlying media by AWS, with a Meridian-issued secure wiping of the logical volume via `shred` command. | Purge | AWS `DiskServiceScheduledEvent` log + Meridian `shred` exit code 0. |
| Physical Hard Drives (SSD/HDD) | Shredding by Iron Mountain. Media must be reduced to a size no larger than 2mm nominal edge length. | Destroy | Video recording of the shredding process with unique asset serial numbers verified orally by two Meridian witnesses. |
| Backup Tapes (LTO) | Degaussing using an NSA-listed degausser, followed by physical incineration by Iron Mountain. | Destroy | Signed witness log from the degaussing event + Iron Mountain Incineration Certificate. |
| Paper Records | Cross-cut shredding (P-5 standard particle size ≤ 30mm²), followed by pulping by Iron Mountain. | Destroy | Monthly Iron Mountain Consolidated Destruction Report. |

**Table 5-C: Required Fields on the Certificate of Destruction**

| Field | Source / Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CoD Unique ID** | Auto-generated GUID. |
| **Data Asset Name(s)** | List from the Data Catalog (e.g., "MedInsight Chronic Condition Cohort - US-East-1"). |
| **Disposal Reference** | The SOP authorizing the destruction (this SOP, SOP-DGP-002) and the approved exception/work order number. |
| **Method of Destruction** | Specific method from Table 5-B (e.g., "AWS KMS Crypto-Shred"). |
| **Legal Hold Verification** | A statement: "I, [Execution Lead Name], confirm that a check was run in Exterro on [Timestamp] and confirmed NO ACTIVE LEGAL HOLDS exist against the enumerated data assets." |
| **Date and Time of Disposal** | ISO 8601 timestamp of when the technical steps were completed. |
| **Executed By** | Digital signatures of the two authorized employees performing the Two-Person Integrity Rule. |
| **Approved By** | Digital signature of the Chief Privacy Officer (Dr. Klaus Weber) or their delegate. For data released from a hold, the signature of General Counsel (Maria Gonzalez) is also required. |

## 6. Controls and Safeguards

Meridian implements a multi-layered control environment to ensure the integrity of the retention and disposal process.

### 6.1 Technical Controls
- **Immutable Audit Logs:** All actions taken on retention-affecting data (creation, deletion, classification change, lifecycle policy application) are logged via AWS CloudTrail and sent to a separate, immutable log account controlled solely by the InfoSec team. These logs are retained for 10 years (J-SOX-03).
- **Versioning and Object Lock:** All production AWS S3 buckets storing PD and PHI have S3 Object Versioning enabled, with a default retention period of 90 days before a delete marker can be applied. This prevents accidental or malicious permanent deletion before a certified disposal event.
- **HSM-Backed Disposal Keys:** The cryptographic keys for the Two-Person Integrity disposal scripts are generated and stored within a FIPS 140-2 Level 3 Hardware Security Module (AWS CloudHSM). Access requires multi-factor authentication and approval from the CISO.
- **Automated Data Scrubbing:** Non-production environments (development, testing, staging) are automatically scrubbed of all production data and provisioned with synthetic, auto-generated test data weekly via a DataSecOps pipeline. Direct cloning of production into non-prod is permanently blocked at the AWS Organization Service Control Policy (SCP) level.

### 6.2 Administrative Controls
- **Annual Retention Audit:** The DPO Team, in conjunction with the Internal Audit department, performs an annual audit of a statistically significant random sample of data assets against the Master Retention Schedule. Findings of "over-retention" beyond the defined schedule must be remediated within 90 calendar days.
- **Quarterly Access Review:** Data Owners must conduct a quarterly review of user access to their data assets, specifically reviewing who has the `data_disposal_approver` role. An access recertification campaign is run centrally via SailPoint.
- **Vendor Due Diligence:** All third-party records storage and destruction vendors (e.g., Iron Mountain) must undergo an annual InfoSec and privacy audit, including on-site inspection of their shredding and incineration facilities. Their SOC 2 Type II report is reviewed and cross-referenced against their Statement of Work obligations.

### 6.3 Physical Controls
- Access to physical records storage areas (in the Boston HQ basement) requires dual-factor authentication (badge + biometric), CCTV recording with 90-day retention, and an escorted visitor log for all non-authorized personnel.
- Secure consoles for paper disposal are constructed of 1.6mm thick steel, feature an anti-fishing baffle, and are bolted to the floor.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Requesting a Retention Exception
A business-justified request to retain data beyond its MRS-assigned period may be submitted by a Data Owner. Retention "just in case it's valuable" is not a valid justification.

**Procedure:**
1.  **Submission:** The Data Owner submits an "Exception - Data Retention" request in the ServiceNow DG module. The request must contain:
    - Precise identification of the data asset.
    - The specific extended retention period requested.
    - A valid business justification tied to a specific contract, active project, or business requirement with a defined end date.
    - Acknowledgement of increased storage costs to be charged to their cost center.
2.  **DPO Risk Assessment:** The CPO team performs a privacy impact assessment on the exception. They evaluate the extended risk against the principle of Storage Limitation. The DPO team has a 5-business-day SLA to complete their assessment.
3.  **Approval Matrix:** The exception is routed based on the extended period requested beyond the standard MRS term:

| Extension Period | Approver Required |
| :--- | :--- |
| ≤ 6 months | Data Owner (Self-approve via automated workflow, logged for audit) |
| 6 to 12 months | Chief Privacy Officer (Dr. Klaus Weber) |
| > 12 months | Joint Approval: Chief Privacy Officer (Dr. Klaus Weber) AND General Counsel (Maria Gonzalez) |

4.  **Technical Implementation:** If approved, the exception is not indefinite. The IT system (Alation) receives the request, sets a new hard, un-editable disposal date in the metadata, and triggers a new workflow task to the owner 30 days prior to the *new* date, requiring a re-certification. No exception is permanent.

### 8.2 Escalation Paths
- **Disputes on Legal Hold Scope:** A custodian believing the scope of a legal hold is overly broad must continue to preserve all data while their counsel raises the issue directly with General Counsel (Maria Gonzalez).
- **Failure of Automated Disposal:** If an automated disposal script fails, a Priority 2 (P2) ticket is automatically opened in the IT Infrastructure Jira queue and the on-call DBA is paged. A manual disposal with the Two-Person Integrity Rule must be executed within 48 hours.
- **Confirmed Unauthorized Data Disposal:** A P1 incident is declared. The CISO and CPO are immediately paged. The forensic response procedure from the Incident Response Plan (SOP-IS-004) is activated immediately.
