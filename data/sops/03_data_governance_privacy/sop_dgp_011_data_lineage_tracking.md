# Standard Operating Procedure: Data Lineage Tracking

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish the framework, responsibilities, and operational processes for end-to-end data lineage tracking across all Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. (“Meridian”) business lines and technology platforms. Effective data lineage tracking ensures that the origin, movement, transformation, and consumption of data—particularly Protected Health Information (PHI), personally identifiable information (PII), and model training data—are cataloged, traceable, and auditable throughout the data lifecycle. This SOP operationalizes Meridian’s commitments under the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0), the EU AI Act, and SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria.

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to:

- **All Business Lines**: Clinical AI Platform, HealthPay Financial Services, MedInsight Analytics, and the Meridian SaaS Platform.
- **All Data Domains**: Structured and unstructured data, streaming and batch data, PHI/PII, synthetic data, model training and fine-tuning datasets, feature stores, and inference outputs.
- **All Environments**: Production, staging, development, and disaster recovery environments across AWS (us-east-1, eu-west-1) and Azure DR regions.
- **All Personnel**: Full-time employees, contractors, consultants, and third-party vendors who design, develop, deploy, or maintain data pipelines, models, or applications that process Meridian data.
- **All Stages of the Data Lifecycle**: Ingestion, transformation, storage, retrieval, model training, inference, archival, and deletion.

### 1.3 Out of Scope
- Data lineage for non-production synthetic test data generated by the QA team, unless that synthetic data is derived from production schemas or distributions that could present re-identification risk.
- Lineage tracking within third-party SaaS tools that do not expose APIs for metadata extraction; in such cases, a manual boundary entry is required per Section 5.7.

## 2. Definitions and Acronyms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Data Lineage** | A record of the origin, movement, transformation, and destination of a data element or dataset across systems and processes over time. |
| **Technical Lineage** | Lineage captured at the column, table, or file level through automated parsing of code, query logs, and pipeline metadata. |
| **Business Lineage** | High-level, human-readable mapping of data flows between business processes, applications, and organizational units. |
| **Horizontal Lineage** | Cross-system lineage tracing data movement between disparate platforms (e.g., Snowflake → SageMaker → Pinecone). |
| **Vertical Lineage** | Intra-system lineage tracing data transformations within a single platform (e.g., SQL transformations within Snowflake). |
| **Lineage Graph** | A directed acyclic graph (DAG) representing data assets as nodes and transformations/movements as edges. |
| **Data Asset** | Any discrete collection of data, including tables, views, feature groups, model artifacts, log streams, and API responses. |
| **Transformation Node** | A processing step that alters data structure, content, or format, including ETL/ELT jobs, model training runs, and feature engineering steps. |
| **Impact Analysis** | The process of identifying all downstream consumers, models, reports, or decisions affected by a proposed change to an upstream data asset. |
| **Root Cause Analysis (RCA)** | The process of tracing upstream from a detected anomaly or error to identify the originating data source or transformation. |
| **PHI** | Protected Health Information as defined under HIPAA. |
| **PII** | Personally Identifiable Information as defined under GDPR and applicable state laws. |
| **AI Act** | Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 laying down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence. |
| **AI RMF** | NIST Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework 1.0 (NIST AI 100-1). |
| **SOC 2** | AICPA System and Organization Controls 2, specifically the Security, Availability, and Confidentiality criteria. |
| **CIA** | Chief AI Officer (Dr. Marcus Rivera). |
| **DPO** | Data Protection Officer (Dr. Klaus Weber). |
| **DGC** | Data Governance Committee, a sub-committee of the AI Governance Committee. |

Each business unit will designate at least one Data Steward responsible for:
- Validating business lineage accuracy for their domain.
- Approving changes to business glossary terms associated with data assets.
- Serving as the first point of contact for impact analysis questions within their domain.

- Reviewing lineage completeness metrics and prioritizing remediation efforts.
- Overseeing the alignment of business lineage with technical lineage.

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Mandatory Lineage Capture
All production data pipelines, ETL/ELT jobs, model training workflows, and feature engineering processes must generate and persist technical lineage metadata automatically at the time of execution. Manual post-hoc lineage documentation is permitted only for legacy pipelines pending migration, as specified in Section 5.8.

### 4.2 Lineage Granularity
Technical lineage must be captured at the column level for all structured data stores containing PHI, PII, or data used as inputs to high-risk AI systems as classified under the EU AI Act Annex III. Table-level lineage is the minimum acceptable granularity for all other data assets.

### 4.3 Immutable Lineage Records
Lineage metadata, once recorded, shall be append-only and immutable. Lineage records must be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years from the date of last access of the associated data asset, or as required by the applicable data retention schedule (SOP-DGP-004), whichever is longer.

### 4.4 EU AI Act Technical Documentation
For each high-risk AI system, technical documentation shall include a description of the data lineage covering training, validation, and testing datasets. This documentation must demonstrate traceability from source data through all preprocessing, labeling, and augmentation steps.

### 4.5 Impact Analysis Requirement
A documented impact analysis using the automated lineage graph is required prior to:
- Schema changes in production databases (table drops, column renames, data type changes).
- Changes to feature engineering logic in model pipelines.
- Decommissioning of any data source feeding a high-risk AI system.
- Migration of data assets between cloud regions.

### 4.6 Data Provenance for Model Training
All datasets used for training, validation, and testing of AI/ML models must include verifiable provenance metadata, including source system identifiers, extraction timestamps, and transformation lineage.

## 5. Detailed Procedures

### 5.1 Automated Technical Lineage Capture

#### 5.1.1 Ingestion Layer Lineage
All data ingestion into the Meridian data platform (Snowflake, S3 data lake) must be instrumented to capture the following lineage metadata at rest:

| Metadata Field | Source | Format |
|----------------|--------|--------|
| `source_system_id` | Configuration file | e.g., `EHR_EPIC_PROD_01` |
| `extraction_timestamp` | Ingest framework | ISO 8601 UTC |
| `ingestion_pipeline_run_id` | Apache Airflow | UUID from `dag_run.conf` |
| `row_count_ingested` | Ingestion validation | Integer |
| `schema_version` | Schema registry | Semver string (e.g., `2.1.0`) |
| `data_classification` | Data Catalog (Collibra) | `PHI`, `PII`, `PUBLIC`, `INTERNAL` |

**Procedure:**
1. The Data Platform Engineering team configures each Airflow DAG with a `LineageOperator` that fires after successful ingestion.
2. The `LineageOperator` writes a lineage event to the OpenLineage-compatible metadata store (Marquez, deployed in `us-east-1`).
3. Upon write failure, the ingestion DAG retries the lineage write three (3) times at 60-second intervals. If all retries fail, the ingestion is marked with `lineage_status: DEGRADED` and an automated Jira ticket is created in the `DG-LINEAGE` project.
4. The `lineage_status: DEGRADED` condition triggers a P3 alert in PagerDuty routed to the Data Platform on-call rotation.

#### 5.1.2 Transformation Lineage Capture
All SQL transformations executed within Snowflake, dbt models, and Spark jobs on EMR must leverage the SQL parser integration shipped with the Marquez deployment.

**Procedure for dbt Transformations:**
1. Every dbt project repository (`meridian-dbt-models`) must include the `dbt-marquez` integration package as a dependency in `packages.yml`.
2. CI/CD pipelines in GitHub Actions must execute `dbt docs generate` on every pull request merge to `main`.
3. The generated `manifest.json` is parsed by a Lambda function (`meridian-lineage-parser-prod`) that extracts column-level lineage for all models tagged with `lineage:critical` or `lineage:standard`.
4. Column-level lineage for models tagged `lineage:best_effort` is parsed but errors are non-blocking.

**Procedure for Spark Jobs:**
1. All Spark jobs submitted to EMR must use the Meridian-provided bootstrap script that enables the Spark OpenLineage integration listener.
2. The listener emits RunEvent and DatasetEvent messages to the Marquez API endpoint at `https://lineage.internal.meridianhealth.com/api/v1`.
3. Job failures that result in partial lineage are automatically flagged for review in the Data Observability dashboard (Monte Carlo).

### 5.2 Business Lineage Documentation

#### 5.2.1 Business Process Mapping
Each business unit VP shall maintain a current-state business lineage diagram for their domain using the approved tool (Collibra Data Lineage, accessed via SSO). These diagrams must be reviewed and re-certified annually during the Q1 compliance cycle.

#### 5.2.2 Linking Business and Technical Lineage
Collibra Data Lineage surfaces the Marquez technical lineage graph and overlays business glossary terms. Data Stewards are responsible for tagging data assets in Collibra with the appropriate business glossary term. The automated nightly synchronization job (`collibra-marquez-sync`) propagates these tags.

### 5.3 Lineage Capture for AI/ML Training Pipelines

This procedure operationalizes NIST AI RMF Playbook guidance (GOVERN 3.2, MAP 4.3) and is mandatory for all models classified as high-risk under Meridian’s AI Classification Standard (SOP-AI-008).

**Procedure:**
1. **Dataset Registration**: Before initiating a training run, the Data Scientist or MLOps Engineer registers the training dataset in the Meridian Feature Store (Feast on AWS) with a unique `dataset_urn`.
2. **Source Pointers**: The `dataset_urn` registration must include lineage back to the source table(s) or file path(s) in the data lake. This linkage is automated if the dataset is materialized from a registered Snowflake query.
3. **Training Run Lineage**: The MLflow tracking server (deployed in EKS `us-east-1`) is configured with a lineage plugin. Upon `mlflow.start_run()`, the plugin captures:
   - The `dataset_urn` of training, validation, and test splits.
   - The Docker image SHA256 digest of the training container.
   - Hyperparameter set hash.
4. **Model Artifact Lineage**: Upon `mlflow.register_model()`, the lineage plugin creates an edge in Marquez linking the training dataset(s) to the registered model version in the Meridian Model Registry.
5. **Lineage Card Generation**: Upon successful model registration, the system automatically generates a **Model Lineage Card** (a populated Markdown template in the model's MLflow artifacts) summarizing provenance. This card is required as an input to the Model Release Review Board (MRRB) per SOP-MLOPS-003.

### 5.4 Visualization and Exploration

Lineage graphs are accessible via two interfaces:
- **Collibra Data Lineage (Primary)**: For Data Stewards, Privacy Office, and Compliance. Provides full business and technical lineage with searching, filtering, and impact analysis capabilities.
- **Marquez UI (Technical)**: For Data Engineers and MLOps. Provides raw technical lineage, DAG visualization, and API access. Access is restricted to members of the `data-platform-engineering` and `ml-platform` Okta groups.

### 5.5 Impact Analysis Procedure

This procedure is mandatory per Section 4.5. The Change Owner must complete the following steps **no fewer than five (5) business days** before the scheduled production change for changes affecting high-risk AI systems, or three (3) business days for all other changes.

**Steps:**
1. **Initiate Change**: Create a Change Request in ServiceNow using the `DGP-Data-Lineage` template (Form ID: `dgp_lineage_change_v2`).
2. **Identify Asset**: Enter the fully qualified data asset name (e.g., `meridian_prod.clinical.cohorts.diabetes_cohort_v3`).
3. **Run Automated Impact Analysis**: In Collibra, right-click the asset node and select **Impact Analysis → All Downstream (Full Depth)**. Export the resulting report as a PDF.
4. **Review Downstream Consumers**: The Change Owner reviews the list of downstream tables, views, dashboards, models, and API endpoints.
5. **Notified Stakeholders**: The Change Owner must notify the owners of all downstream assets that are in the `critical` tier (as defined in the Data Classification Policy, SOP-DGP-001). Notification is sent via ServiceNow, which automatically creates a `Data Impact Notification` task for each downstream owner.
6. **Attestation**: Each notified downstream owner must acknowledge the notification within the standard notification window (3 business days for non-critical, 5 business days for critical). If a downstream owner objects, the change is placed on hold and escalated per Section 8.
7. **Closure**: The attached Impact Analysis Report PDF must be attached to the ServiceNow Change Request before the change is approved.

### 5.6 Lineage for Deletion and Archival

When a data asset is archived or deleted per the Data Retention Policy (SOP-DGP-004), the lineage graph is not destroyed. Instead:
- Archived assets are marked with `status: ARCHIVED` and a tombstone node is inserted preserving the last-known upstream and downstream connections.
- Deleted assets are marked with `status: DELETED`. The tombstone node retains metadata: deletion timestamp, deletion request ID, and authorizing policy reference.

### 5.7 Manual Lineage for Boundary Systems

For legacy systems or third-party tools that cannot emit OpenLineage events, a manual lineage procedure applies:
1. The system's Data Steward creates a "Boundary Asset" in Collibra.
2. The Data Steward manually defines the input and output columns for the boundary asset.
3. The boundary asset is reviewed by the DGC quarterly.
4. A migration plan to automated lineage must be on the owning team's fiscal year roadmap. Exceptions beyond one fiscal year require a waiver per Section 8.

### 5.8 Lineage for High-Risk AI Systems — EU AI Act Artifacts

For each AI system registered in the Meridian EU AI Act Inventory (maintained by the VP of Clinical AI Products), a **Data Provenance Appendix** shall be produced and included with the system's technical documentation.

**Appendix Contents:**
- A summary diagram showing the lineage graph from source data to training/validation/test datasets.
- A table enumerating each data source, its classification (e.g., public, internal, PHI), and the data processing agreement (DPA) or data use agreement (DUA) governing its use.
- Documentation of all preprocessing, labeling, and augmentation logic as captured by the Marquez transformation lineage.
- A log of all data quality checks performed on the datasets, referenced to SOP-DGP-009 (Data Quality Management).

The AI Governance Committee reviews the completeness of these appendices quarterly against the AI RMF MAP category controls.

## 6. Controls and Safeguards

### 6.1 Access Controls

Access to the lineage metadata store (Marquez API and underlying PostgreSQL database) and the Collibra Lineage view is governed by the Identity and Access Management Policy (SOP-CISO-002).

- **Read Access**: Granted to Data Stewards, members of the DGC, platform engineers, individual contributors on product teams (limited to lineage for assets within their registered domain), Internal Audit, and the Privacy Office.
- **Write Access (API)**: Restricted to service accounts associated with Airflow, Spark EMR clusters, MLflow, and the Collibra synchronization service. Human users do not have direct API write access to Marquez.
- **Administrative Access**: Restricted to the Data Platform Engineering Lead and the VP of IT Operations.
- **Okta Group Mapping**: Access is enforced via Okta SSO integration with Collibra and via IAM roles for Marquez API access. Group membership is reviewed quarterly.

### 6.2 Incident Response for Lineage Integrity

Any event indicating corruption, tampering, or significant loss of lineage metadata must be reported per the Incident Response Plan (SOP-CISO-003). A lineage-specific playbook runbook is maintained in the `meridian-runbooks` GitHub repository (`lineage_integrity_incident_v1.2.md`).

Key response steps:
1. **Containment**: Isolate affected pipeline. Freeze write access to the lineage store segment.
2. **Investigation**: Reconstruct timeline using CloudTrail logs for Marquez API calls and Collibra audit logs.
3. **Remediation**: If lineage is lost, a backfill job is executed from the source system logs (Airflow metadata DB, Snowflake `QUERY_HISTORY`). This backfill must be validated by a Data Steward.

### 6.3 Change Management

All changes to the lineage capture infrastructure (Marquez deployment, Collibra connectors, Airflow `LineageOperator` code) must follow the standard IT Change Management process (SOP-ITIL-001). The DGC must be informed of any change that affects the completeness or accuracy of lineage graphs across multiple business units.

## 7. Monitoring, Metrics, and Reporting

### 7.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The following KPIs operationalize NIST AI RMF Monitor 4.3 (Periodic Review of Trustworthiness Characteristics):

| KPI ID | Metric | Target | Measurement Tool | Reporting Cadence |
|--------|--------|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| LIN-01 | **Lineage Completeness** — % of production data assets with automated technical lineage | ≥ 95% | Collibra + Marquez reconciliation | Monthly |
| LIN-02 | **Column-Level Lineage Coverage** — % of PHI/PII columns with column-level lineage | ≥ 98% | Collibra Column Lineage report | Monthly |
| LIN-03 | **Impact Analysis Turnaround** — median time from Change Request to completed impact analysis | ≤ 24 hours | ServiceNow dashboard | Quarterly |
| LIN-04 | **Lineage Graph Latency** — 99th percentile time from pipeline execution to lineage graph availability | ≤ 5 minutes | Datadog APM (Marquez API traces) | Monthly |
| LIN-05 | **Lineage Accuracy** — % of spot-checked lineage paths verified as correct by Data Stewards | ≥ 99% | Quarterly lineage audit (manual) | Quarterly |
| LIN-06 | **High-Risk AI Provenance Completeness** — % of registered high-risk AI systems with a complete Data Provenance Appendix | = 100% | AI Governance Committee audit | Quarterly |

### 7.2 Dashboards

The Data Platform Engineering team maintains two dashboards:
1. **Operational Lineage Health** (Grafana, `Lineage Monitoring` dashboard): Real-time metrics for Marquez API latency, error rates, and backlog queue depth. Alert thresholds are configured per SOP-SRE-001.
2. **Governance Lineage Scorecard** (Collibra BI Suite, refreshed nightly): Reports LIN-01 through LIN-05 aggregated by business unit. The DGC reviews this scorecard at each monthly meeting.

### 7.3 Quarterly Lineage Audit

The Data Governance & Privacy Team conducts a quarterly audit of lineage accuracy. A random sample of 50 data assets is selected. The team traces the lineage path end-to-end from origin to consumption and verifies it against the pipeline code. Results are published in the quarterly AI Governance Report submitted to the Chief AI Officer and the DPO.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Exception Categories
Policy exceptions are categorized as:
- **Technical Exception**: A system cannot emit automated lineage on the standard timeline. Example: a legacy mainframe feed.
- **Business Exception**: A use case does not require the standard lineage granularity. Example: table-level lineage is accepted for a low-criticality reporting table.

### 8.2 Exception Request Process
1. The requester completes the **Data Lineage Exception Request Form** (available on the Meridian intranet, Form ID: `DGP-011-EXC`), detailing the asset in question, the specific policy section for which exception is sought, a technical or business justification, and a proposed compensating control.
2. The requester’s VP reviews and signs the form.
3. The form is submitted to the Chief Compliance Officer (Thomas Anderson) for approval.
4. The CCO, in consultation with the DPO (Dr. Klaus Weber) and CISO (Rachel Kim) if the exception involves PHI or PII, approves or denies the request within ten (10) business days.
5. An approved exception is registered in the Meridian Governance Risk and Compliance (GRC) platform (Archer) with an expiration date no later than twelve (12) months from approval.
6. The Data Governance & Privacy team documents the approved exception in Collibra, labeling the affected data assets with an exception tag and the GRC ticket ID.

### 8.3 Escalation Path for Impact Analysis Disputes
If a downstream consumer objects to a change during the impact analysis attestation (Section 5.5, Step 6):
1. The Change Owner and the objecting stakeholder meet to resolve the objection within three (3) business days.
2. If unresolved, the matter escalates to the respective Business Unit VPs.
3. If still unresolved, it escalates to the **Data Governance Committee** for binding resolution at its next monthly meeting.
4. An emergency DGC meeting may be convened at the discretion of the Chair (DPO) if the change is time-critical. The DGC Chair’s decision in such an emergency meeting is final.
