# STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
## Automated Decision-Making Transparency

### 1. Purpose and Scope

#### 1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is to establish the governance framework, operational controls, and transparency mechanisms for all automated decision-making (ADM) systems and processes deployed across Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. This SOP defines how the organization identifies, documents, communicates, and governs decisions made without meaningful human intervention that produce legal effects or similarly significant impacts on individuals.

This SOP operationalizes Meridian’s commitment to responsible AI governance by codifying the requirements of the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) as the primary control framework, with particular emphasis on the MAP, MEASURE, and MANAGE functions. It establishes a single, auditable standard for ADM transparency that applies uniformly across all business lines, geographies, and product offerings.

#### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to:

- **All Business Lines**: Clinical AI Platform, HealthPay Financial Services, MedInsight Analytics, and the underlying Meridian SaaS Platform.
- **All Decision Types**: Decisions produced solely by automated means (no human in the loop), decisions where automated output is a principal factor (human in the loop with constrained override authority), and decisions where automated profiling informs subsequent manual processes.
- **All Data Subjects**: Patients, healthcare provider users, loan applicants, insured members, employees, and contractors whose personal or professional data is processed by Meridian ADM systems.
- **All Geographies**: This is a global SOP. Where jurisdictional requirements diverge, the most protective standard defined herein controls, unless explicitly superseded by a documented jurisdiction-specific addendum approved by the Chief Privacy Officer and General Counsel.
- **All System Lifecycle Phases**: Design, development, pre-deployment validation, production operation, and decommissioning of ADM systems.

#### 1.3 Out-of-Scope Exclusions
- Fully manual decision-making processes where technology serves only as a passive repository of information (e.g., a PDF stored in SharePoint that a human reads and independently acts upon).
- Spreadsheet-based analyses performed by individuals where no automated system triggers a workflow or decision outcome.
- Business-to-business procurement decisions made by Meridian regarding its own vendors, which are governed by SOP-PROC-003 (Vendor Procurement and Contracting).

### 4. Policy Statements

Meridian Health Technologies, Inc. adheres to the following high-level policy commitments governing all ADM activities:

**PS-1: Transparency by Default.** All ADM systems deployed in production shall be accompanied by a publicly accessible, plain-language Transparency Notice, approved by the CPO, describing the logic, data types, and consequences of the automated decision. This notice shall be made available to individuals prior to or at the point of data collection where feasible, or at the first point of interaction with the ADM system.

**PS-2: Meaningful Information.** Every affected individual has the right to obtain meaningful information about the logic involved in any ADM process that produces consequential decisions concerning them. Meridian shall provide, within ten (10) business days of a verifiable request, a detailed explanation of the specific decision made, the principal factors that influenced the outcome, and the role of any human judgment in the final determination.

**PS-3: Human Intervention Mandate.** For all consequential decision systems, Meridian shall design, implement, and maintain a documented Human Intervention Point (HIP). This HIP must be accessible to the affected individual upon request, staffed by qualified personnel, and capable of overriding the automated outcome with a reasoned, documented alternative.

**PS-4: Prohibition of Solely Automated Adverse Decisions without Intervention.** No consequential decision that results in an adverse outcome (denial, restriction, penalty, or limitation of access, credit, or treatment) shall be executed solely by automated means without offering the affected individual access to the HIP process described in PS-3.

**PS-5: Impact Assessment Mandate.** All new ADM systems, or material modifications to existing ADM systems, must undergo a documented impact assessment, inclusive of algorithmic fairness and bias evaluation, prior to production deployment. The assessment must specifically evaluate disparate impact on protected characteristics.

**PS-6: Data Minimization.** ADM systems shall only process the minimum categories and volume of personal data necessary to achieve the defined, legitimate purpose. Data inputs must be justified in the system’s ADM Registration and approved by the CPO.

**PS-7: Accountability and Auditability.** All ADM decisions, overrides, and system outputs shall be logged immutably for a minimum retention period of seven (7) years from the date of decision, as governed by SOP-DGP-007 (Data Retention and Archiving).

### 5. Detailed Procedures

This section constitutes the operational core of the ADM Transparency program. Every ADM system, regardless of business unit or technical implementation, must proceed through the following life cycle stages.

#### 5.1 ADM System Identification and Registration

**5.1.1 Trigger Events.**
Registration is triggered when any of the following occurs:
- A new vendor contract involves processing Meridian data through an automated decision engine.
- An internal development project (Agile Epic or equivalent) includes logic that produces a score, classification, recommendation, or automated action that directly affects an individual.
- An existing manual process is augmented with an ML model that outputs a decision recommendation or automated action.
- An existing ADM system undergoes a material model retraining that changes the feature weights by more than 15% or adds new feature categories.

**5.1.2 Registration Procedure.**
1. The Business Unit ADM Owner completes the ADM System Registration Form (Appendix A) in the GRC platform (Archer).
2. The form must contain:
   - System name, business unit, and ADM Owner.
   - Clear statement of the specific decisions automated and their legal or significant effects.
   - Enumeration of all personal data categories used as model features.
   - Identification of the lawful basis for processing each category.
   - Description of the logic, including model type (e.g., gradient boosted trees, deep neural network, logistic regression) and the principal factors.
   - Confirmation of HIP design or documented justification for its absence.
   - Link to completed DPIA and any supplemental algorithmic fairness assessments.
3. Upon submission, the GRC platform automatically routes the registration to:
   - VP, Data Governance (Reviewer)
   - CISO (Reviewer for security architecture)
   - CPO (Approver)
4. CPO approval, rejection, or request for modification shall be issued within ten (10) business days.
5. Approved registrations are assigned a unique **ADM-ID** (format: ADM-YYYY-NNNN) and added to the authoritative ADM Inventory.

#### 5.2 Transparency Notice Development and Publication

**5.2.1 Notice Requirement Mapping.**
For each registered ADM system, the CPO determines the required Transparency Notice tier:

| **Tier** | **Criteria** | **Notice Requirement** |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Produces Consequential Decisions; Solely automated. | Full Public Transparency Notice + Individual Right to Meaningful Information + Mandatory HIP |
| **Tier 2** | Produces Consequential Decisions; Human in the loop with constrained override. | Full Public Transparency Notice + Individual Right to Meaningful Information + Mandatory HIP |
| **Tier 3** | Supports decisions but does not independently produce legal or similarly significant effects (e.g., workflow routing, clinician nudges). | Light Transparency Notice |
| **Tier 4** | Internal administrative processing only. | Internal System Documentation |

**5.2.2 Full Public Transparency Notice (Tier 1 & 2) Template.**
The notice, published on the Meridian Trust Center at `https://trust.meridianhealth.ai/transparency/<ADM-ID>`, shall include:

- **Plain Language Description**: A non-technical explanation of what the system does. (e.g., “This system helps our clinical reviewers determine whether a requested medical procedure meets our guidelines for medical necessity.”)
- **The Logic Involved**: A description of the type of model, the principal categories of data used, and how those factors are weighted or considered. (e.g., “The system uses a rules-based engine combined with a predictive model. It considers the submitted procedure code, the patient’s diagnosis history as recorded in our systems, established clinical protocols, and utilization patterns within the relevant geographic region.”)
- **Significance and Envisaged Consequences**: A clear statement of the outcome. (e.g., “The system outputs a recommendation (‘Approve,’ ‘Deny,’ or ‘Refer for Manual Review’). A ‘Deny’ recommendation means the automated analysis suggests the procedure does not meet medical necessity criteria. This recommendation is then reviewed by a licensed clinical professional.”)
- **Human Intervention Description**: How an individual can obtain human intervention, the qualifications of the human reviewer, the expected timeline for review, and the process for submitting additional information.

**5.2.3 Notice Publication Workflow.**
1. Product Manager drafts notice using approved template language in collaboration with Privacy Engineer.
2. General Counsel reviews for legal accuracy.
3. CPO approves final notice text.
4. Web Governance publishes the notice to the Meridian Trust Center, linked from the primary service interface (e.g., patient portal, loan application dashboard).
5. Publication is verified by the VP, Data Governance within 24 hours of approval.

#### 5.3 Responding to Requests for Meaningful Information

This procedure operationalizes the individual’s right to obtain specific, intelligible information about an ADM decision affecting them.

**5.3.1 Request Intake.**
- Individuals may submit requests via the dedicated Privacy Request Portal (`https://privacy.meridianhealth.ai`), by telephone (1-800-MER-DATA), or by written correspondence addressed to the CPO.
- All requests are logged in the ServiceNow case management system with a unique case ID.
- The case is automatically routed to the Privacy Operations team.

**5.3.2 Request Verification.**
- Within three (3) business days, Privacy Operations verifies the identity of the requestor using multi-factor challenge-response methods commensurate with the sensitivity of the underlying data.
- Verification methods are documented in SOP-DGP-005 (Data Subject Access Request Handling).

**5.3.3 ADM Decision Analysis.**
1. Once verified, the Privacy Operations specialist identifies the relevant ADM system(s) from the ADM Inventory using the individual’s interaction record.
2. The specialist queries the ADM system’s audit log and decision record using the individual’s unique identifier (Meridian Universal ID - MUID) to retrieve the specific decision event.
3. The specialist assembles a **Meaningful Information Package** consisting of:
   - **Decision Snapshot**: Date, time, and outcome of the automated decision.
   - **Input Data Summary**: List of categories of personal data about the individual that were processed by the ADM system. For each category, specify whether the data was self-disclosed, observed, or derived.
   - **Logic Explanation**: A plain-language, counterfactual-friendly explanation of the principal factors that influenced the decision, drawing from the approved Transparency Notice and validated by the Privacy Engineer for fidelity to the actual model behavior. This explanation must enable the individual to understand what they could change to achieve a different outcome, if applicable.
   - **Human Intervention Options**: Clear, step-by-step instructions for requesting human review, including expected timelines and any supporting documentation the individual should provide.
4. The package is reviewed by the Privacy Engineer and approved by the CPO or delegate.
5. The approved package is delivered to the individual securely through their chosen channel within ten (10) business days of verified request. If the complexity of the request reasonably requires an extension, the individual shall be notified within the initial ten (10) business days, and the package shall be delivered within thirty (30) business days total.

#### 5.4 Human Intervention Point (HIP) Operation

**5.4.1 HIP Design Specifications.**
For all Tier 1 and Tier 2 ADM systems, the HIP must meet the following minimum specifications:
- **Access Channel**: Integrated directly into the user interface that delivers the automated decision. A button or link labeled “Request Human Review” must be presented at the point of decision.
- **Reviewer Qualification**: The human reviewer must possess qualifications, training, and authority sufficient to independently analyze and, if warranted, override the automated decision. For clinical ADM systems, the reviewer must hold an active, unrestricted license in the relevant medical specialty. For financial ADM systems, the reviewer must hold underwriting authority as defined in SOP-FIN-014.
- **Review SLA**:
  - Urgent clinical matters: **4 hours** from receipt of request.
  - Non-urgent clinical matters: **3 business days**.
  - Financial decisions (credit, loan): **5 business days**.
  - All other consequential decisions: **10 business days**.
- **Override Authority**: The human reviewer has the authority to override the automated decision and record the rationale. Overrides must be tracked and reported monthly.

**5.4.2 Override Documentation.**
Every override must be recorded in the system audit log with the following minimum data elements:
- MUID of the affected individual.
- ADM-ID of the system.
- Date and time of the automated decision.
- Date and time of the human review.
- Name and role of the human reviewer.
- Outcome of the human review (Upheld / Modified / Reversed).
- Detailed rationale for modification or reversal.
- Any additional data considered that was not available to the automated system.

#### 5.5 Profiling and Impact Assessment

All systems that perform profiling as defined in Section 2 must undergo an expanded impact assessment inclusive of the NIST AI RMF playbook.

**5.5.1 Profiling Impact Assessment Procedure.**
1. The Business Unit ADM Owner initiates a profiling impact assessment in Archer at the same time as ADM Registration.
2. The assessment must specifically address, at minimum, the following areas mapped to the NIST AI RMF Map and Measure functions:
   - **Validity and Reliability (MAP 1.5)**: Documentation of model accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 scores across defined demographic subgroups. Threshold for subgroup performance disparity is a 5% absolute difference in F1 score. Systems exceeding this threshold require a documented mitigation plan approved by the CPO.
   - **Safety and Security (MAP 2.2, MAP 2.3)**: Adversarial testing results, data poisoning resilience measures, and fail-safe design documentation.
   - **Fairness and Bias (MAP 3.1, MAP 3.4)**: Quantitative bias analysis using the Fairlearn or equivalent toolkit. Metrics reported: Demographic Parity Difference (threshold: ≤ 0.10), Equalized Odds Difference (threshold: ≤ 0.08), Conditional Demographic Disparity (threshold: ≤ 0.12). Systems exceeding these thresholds are blocked from production deployment until the VP, Data Governance and CPO approve a risk acceptance.
   - **Explainability and Interpretability (MAP 3.3)**: SHAP or LIME values for key features must be documented and available to the Privacy Engineering team.
   - **Privacy Preservation (MAP 4.1, MAP 4.2)**: Demonstration that training data has been de-identified or pseudonymized in accordance with SOP-DGP-018.
3. The completed assessment is reviewed by the CISO and Privacy Engineer, and approved by the CPO.
4. For Tier 1 and Tier 2 systems, the fairness and bias metrics report is published on the internal Meridian Trust Dashboard and updated quarterly.

### 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

#### 8.1 Exception Types

Exceptions to provisions in this SOP are categorized as:

- **Temporary Exception (≤ 90 days)**: For emergency production fixes or short-term operational necessity. Must have a defined remediation plan with a committed deadline.
- **Conditional Permanent Exception**: For low-risk Tier 3 or Tier 4 systems where a specific control is determined to be technically inapplicable (e.g., fairness metrics on a non-ML rules engine).

#### 8.2 Exception Request Procedure

1. The requesting party (must be Director level or above) submits an **ADM Exception Request** via the ServiceNow GRC module.
2. The request must specify:
   - The ADM-ID of the affected system.
   - The specific control or procedure from this SOP for which an exception is sought.
   - A detailed justification of the business need and, critically, an assessment of the residual risk to data subjects.
   - Proposed compensating controls, if any.
   - Duration of the exception request.
3. The request is routed concurrently to:
   - VP, Data Governance (Risk Assessment).
   - CISO (Technical / Security impact assessment).
   - CPO (Privacy and individual rights impact assessment).
4. Within five (5) business days, the CPO and General Counsel jointly review the aggregated assessments and issue a final determination: **Approved**, **Approved with Conditions**, or **Denied**.
5. All approved exceptions are logged in the Archer GRC ADM Inventory record for that system and reviewed at each Quarterly ADM Risk Review. Temporary exceptions automatically expire; continuation requires a new request.

#### 8.3 Escalation Path

| **Issue Category** | **Escalation Level 1** | **Escalation Level 2** |
|---|---|---|
| Failure to meet an SLA for Meaningful Information delivery. | VP, Data Governance | Dr. Klaus Weber (CPO) |
| Unauthorized deployment of an ADM system (not in inventory). | CISO — treated as a Severity 2 security incident per SOP-SEC-001 | Maria Gonzalez (GC) for contractual/regulatory risk |
| Override rate exceeding 25% sustained over two reporting months. | VP, Data Governance | Joint review: CPO, BU ADM Owner, ML Engineering Lead |
