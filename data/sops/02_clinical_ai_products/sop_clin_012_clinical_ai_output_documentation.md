# Standard Operating Procedure: Clinical AI Output Documentation

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) establishes the mandatory framework for the generation, formatting, documentation, quality assurance, and archival of all outputs produced by Meridian Health Technologies’ Clinical AI Platform. In an environment where algorithmic outputs directly influence patient care pathways, the integrity, transparency, and traceability of every AI-generated inference are paramount. This SOP operationalizes the regulatory mandates of the EU AI Act (particularly for high-risk AI systems), the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF), and HIPAA by embedding rigorous documentation controls directly into the AI output lifecycle. The goal is to ensure that every clinical prediction, risk score, or diagnostic suggestion is auditable, explainable, contestable, and clinically contextualized, thereby safeguarding patient safety, enabling effective human oversight, and ensuring compliance with our ISO 13485-based Quality Management System for Software as a Medical Device (SaMD).

### 1.2 Scope
This SOP applies to all AI/ML models classified as high-risk under the EU AI Act within the Clinical AI Platform business unit. This includes, but is not limited to, models for diagnostic imaging analysis (FDA 510(k)-cleared and CE-marked), patient risk scoring (e.g., 30-day readmission, sepsis onset, adverse event prediction), and clinical decision support tools deployed across Meridian’s 340+ hospital and clinic partners. The scope covers both synchronous (API-driven, at point-of-care) and asynchronous (batch, population-level) AI inferences. All Meridian personnel involved in the design, development, deployment, monitoring, clinical integration, and customer support of these models are subject to this SOP. This includes, without limitation, the Clinical AI Engineering and MLOps teams, Clinical Informatics, Clinical Quality Assurance, Customer Operations (Clinical SaaS Support), the Office of the Chief Medical Officer, and the AI Governance Committee. This SOP is binding on all full-time employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who interact with the Clinical AI Platform’s output pipelines.

### 1.3 Scope Exclusions
This SOP does not govern:
- AI models in the HealthPay Financial Services business unit (covered under SOP-FIN-001, SR 11-7 Model Risk Management).
- Population health analytics models within MedInsight Analytics that are not classified as high-risk AI (covered under SOP-ANL-005).
- Pre-production, experimental models not yet subject to change control (these are governed by SOP-ENG-003, AI Development Lifecycle).
- The raw data and instructions used to produce an output (covered by SOP-CLIN-008, Model Input Data Quality & Provenance).

## 2. Definitions and Acronyms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **AI Output (Inference)** | The discrete, machine-generated result produced by an AI model in response to an input. This includes primary predictions, scores, segmentation masks, and any derived textual or numerical data point presented to an end-user. |
| **Logit** | The raw, unnormalized score or vector of scores output by a model’s final layer before any activation function (e.g., softmax, sigmoid) is applied. It is the foundational quantitative basis for a confidence score. |
| **Confidence Score** | A calibrated, normalized estimate, derived from a Logit or statistical process, quantifying the model’s uncertainty regarding a specific inference. Formatted strictly as a percentage (0.00% to 100.00%) or a calibrated probability [0.0–1.0]. |
| **Explanation (XAI)** | A human-interpretable, local explanation of a specific AI Output, generated by an XAI method (e.g., SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients for imaging), articulating the key input features that drove the prediction. |
| **Clinical Contextualization** | The mandatory process of wrapping an AI Output with relevant, patient-specific clinical metadata (e.g., latest relevant labs, current medications, time window of input data, model limitations and intended use statement) to enable a qualified clinician to safely interpret the result. |
| **Human Oversight Measure (HOM)** | A control mechanism, mandated by EU AI Act Art. 14, designed to be used by a natural person. In this SOP, HOMs are the procedural controls that ensure a clinician can interpret, override, and contest an AI Output. |
| **Output Record** | The complete, immutable, self-contained digital artifact (JSON object) that packages an AI Output along with all mandatory documentation tiers, as defined in Procedure 5.3. |
| **Intended Use Population (IUP)** | The specific patient cohort for which a given model version carries an approved CE marking and/or FDA 510(k) clearance, formally defined in the model’s Technical Documentation. An output generated for a patient outside the IUP is an Out-of-Scope Inference. |
| **Drift Metric** | A quantitative measure comparing live production data or model outputs against a validated baseline, used to detect functional performance degradation. Key drift metrics for each model are defined in their respective Model Cards per SOP-CLIN-003. |

## 4. Detailed Procedures

This section constitutes the core operational workflow. All steps are mandatory unless explicitly stated otherwise.

### 4.1 General Output Workflow
The following sequential steps shall be executed for every synchronous and asynchronous AI inference:

1.  **Input Validation:** The input payload is validated against the model’s published contract (schema). If validation fails, no inference is run, and a standardized error code is returned (Procedure 5.1.2).
2.  **Inference Execution:** The model server executes the forward pass. The raw Logits must be captured and temporarily stored in-memory for the duration of the pipeline execution.
3.  **Post-Processing & Calibration:** The raw Logits are transformed into the final `Prediction` (e.g., probability, class label, segmentation mask) using the approved calibration curve and post-processing logic compiled with the model.
4.  **Output Record Assembly (Mandatory):** The `Output Record` JSON is assembled. No incomplete record shall be persisted. The record must include all six Documentation Tiers as specified in Procedure 5.3.
5.  **Human Oversight Measure (HOM) Gateway:** For all synchronous outputs, the fully assembled `Output Record` is transmitted to the clinical workstation. The end-user interface SHALL NOT display a primary prediction without simultaneously and prominently displaying the Confidence Score and the Clinical Contextualization statement. The interface must present a mandatory "Accept / Override / Contested" action button for the clinician (See Controls, 6.1).
6.  **Persist and Dispatch:** The complete `Output Record` is atomically written to the immutable audit log in Amazon DynamoDB. An anonymized subset is simultaneously dispatched to the monitoring data warehouse (Apache Iceberg) for population-level drift analysis.

### 5.2 Generating Core Documentation Tiers
Each AI-generated inference must be accompanied by the following two foundational documentation components, generated dynamically at runtime.

#### 5.2.1 Confidence Score Generation
- **Direct Extraction:** For classification models, the `Confidence Score` must be the calibrated probability output for the predicted class. It is strictly prohibited to use a simple softmax maximum alone without temperature scaling. Meridian uses Platt scaling or Isotonic Regression, implemented in the `calibration_service`.
- **Reporting Format:** Confidence scores must be reported as a percentage rounded to one decimal place (e.g., "Confidence: 87.3%"). For regression tasks (e.g., Length of Stay), a Prediction Interval (PI) with a configurable default of 95% shall be reported.
- **Low-Confidence Thresholding:** If the calibrated confidence score falls below a model-specific **Confidence Floor**, defined in the model’s Technical Documentation and configurable via the LaunchDarkly feature flag `[model-id]-output-flag`, the prediction is suppressed. The user interface must display a standard clinical notification: **"Result Withheld: Confidence Below Clinical Threshold."** The raw data and explanation must still be logged to the audit trail for post-hoc QA.

#### 5.2.2 Explanation (XAI) Generation
Meridian employs a modular XAI framework. The method used (**SHAP, LIME, or Integrated Gradients**) must be pre-registered in the model’s `metadata.json` and cannot be changed without a full model re-validation.
- **Quantitative Explanation:** For tabular models (e.g., Sepsis Risk Score), the `ShapleyValueGenerator` service must return the top 3 positive and top 3 negative contributing features, their raw values, and their SHAPley values.
- **Qualitative Explanation (Textual):** The raw SHAP values must be passed to the `Contextualizer` service, which queries the patient’s FHIR data and the `Explanation-Template` table to generate a human-readable string in the clinician’s language.
    - *Example:* "This High Sepsis Risk score was primarily influenced by the patient's elevated Serum Lactate (3.5 mmol/L), a significant drop in Systolic Blood Pressure to 88 mmHg, and a current temperature of 38.9°C, compared to the IUP baseline."

### 5.3 Structuring the Complete Output Record
Every output record shall be structured as a flat JSON object containing six mandatory Documentation Tiers. The MLOps pipeline (Apache Kafka -> Stream Processor) must reject any inference that fails to assemble any of these tiers, retry three times, and then permanently dead-letter the inference.

**Tier 1: Core Prediction Block (`prediction_core`)**
- `inference_id`: [UUIDv7, deterministic]
- `model_id`: [string]
- `model_version`: [string]
- `prediction_type`: [enum: `BINARY_CLASSIFICATION`, `MULTICLASS`, `REGRESSION`, `SEGMENTATION_MASK`]
- `prediction_value`: [float, string, or array]
- `threshold_applied`: [string] (e.g., `SEPSIS_ALERT_V2`)

**Tier 2: Uncertainty & Explanation Block (`uncertainty_and_xai`)**
- `confidence_score`: [float, 0.0-1.0]
- `confidence_method`: [enum: `PLATT_SCALING`, `ISOTONIC_REGRESSION`, `ENSEMBLE_VARIANCE`]
- `xai_method`: [enum: `SHAP_KERNEL`, `INTEGRATED_GRADS`, `LIME_TABULAR`]
- `top_feature_1 (positive)` / `shap_value` / `feature_raw_value`
- `top_feature_3 (negative)` ... etc.
- `human_readable_explanation`: [string, localized]

**Tier 3: Clinical Contextualization Block (`clinical_context`)**
- `patient_metadata_snapshot_id`: [FK to Input Provenance Table]
- `intended_use_population_statement`: [string]
- `key_contextual_labs_and_vitals`: [JSON array of structured FHIR resources]
- `model_limitation_statement`: [string] (e.g., "Model V2.1 is validated for patients 18-85 years. For pediatric patients, inference is provided for research context only and must not inform clinical decisions.")
- `out_of_scope_flag`: [boolean] – **Generated programmatically** by checking patient age and documented comorbidities against the approved IUP.

**Tier 4: Processing Metadata Block (`processing_meta`)**
- `inference_timestamp_utc`: [ISO 8601]
- `total_latency_ms`: [int]
- `pipeline_version`: [string, SemVer]
- `input_data_hash_sha256`: [string]

**Tier 5: Audit & Traceability Block (`audit`)**
- `organization_id`: [string]
- `requesting_clinician_id`: [pseudonymized UUID]
- `patient_id`: [pseudonymized UUID, linked to master patient index]
- `workstation_session_id`: [UUID]

**Tier 6: Human Oversight Feedback Block (`human_feedback`)**
- `final_clinician_action`: [enum: `ACCEPTED`, `OVERRIDDEN_WITH_VALUE`, `CONTESTED`]
- `overridden_value`: [nullable, string/float]
- `clinician_free_text_rationale`: [nullable, encrypted string]
- `action_timestamp_utc`: [nullable, ISO 8601]

### 5.4 Asynchronous (Batch) Output Documentation
For nightly batch inference processes (e.g., 30-day readmission risk for the next day’s census):
- All six tiers outlined in 5.3 remain mandatory, with the exception that Tier 6 (`human_feedback`) is nullable.
- Tier 3 (`clinical_context`) must include the **temporal staleness metric**: the maximum gap in hours between the input data fetch timestamp and the batch execution timestamp for each patient. A warning flag `"data_freshness_warning": true` must be set if this gap exceeds 6 hours.
- Output Records are written in bulk to S3 as a Parquet dataset, then loaded into Snowflake tables `CLINICAL_AI.BATCH_OUTPUTS` for customer-facing dashboards. An immutable copy is simultaneously written to a Write-Once-Read-Many (WORM) compliant S3 bucket for audit retention.

## 6. Controls and Safeguards

### 6.1 Technical Controls
| Control ID | Description | Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| **TEC-012-01** | **Immutable Audit Trail:** All `Output Records`, including `human_feedback` actions (Tier 6), must be written to an append-only, immutable log. | Amazon DynamoDB with Point-in-Time Recovery (PITR) enabled and Stream-based replication to the audit data lake. IAM role `ClinicalAI-Output-Prod-DB` is the sole writer. |
| **TEC-012-02** | **Output Schema Validation:** Every Output Record must be validated against the published JSON Schema before being dispatched or persisted. | An AWS API Gateway request validator enforced by a dedicated `output-schema-registry` microservice, which fetches the active schema for a given `model_id` and `version`. |
| **TEC-012-03** | **FHIR Resource Proxying & PHI Minimization:** The `Contextualizer` service must only access approved FHIR resource types as defined in an allow-list for each model. It must not pull in free-text clinical notes unless explicitly approved in the model’s risk assessment. | A fine-grained Apache Ranger policy on the Hadoop/HDFS-based Data Lake, enforced by the OPA service. All `patient_id` fields in the `audit` tier are pseudonyms (tokenized). Access to the re-identification key requires a quorum break-glass procedure logged in HashiCorp Vault. |
| **TEC-012-04** | **Adversarial Defense Header:** Any XAI output that detects a high adversarial perturbation (measured by a rapid, low-impact change to an otherwise static high-impact feature) must append a warning: `"Warning: Potentially anomalous input pattern detected. Exercise heightened clinical scrutiny."` | A dedicated `AnomalyShield` microservice, running concurrently with the XAI generator, performs a rapid Local Intrinsic Dimensionality (LID) check on the input vector. |
| **TEC-012-05** | **EU AI Act Art. 14(4) Human-Machine Interface Constraint:** The FHIR Subscription API delivering the output to the EHR front-end SHALL NOT present the `prediction_value` as a pre-populated order or a pre-checked checkbox. The clinician must perform a positive, unscripted action (e.g., typing, selecting from a distinct dropdown) to "Accept" the inference. | Enforced in the Meridian Clinical UI (React FHIR Component Library) and enforced via our certified EHR integration partner's App Orchard interface. |

### 6.2 Administrative Controls
- **Change Management:** Any alteration to an approved model’s post-processing calibration curve, XAI method, or Tier 3 clinical context template must follow the Change Control Process in SOP-CLIN-001. A change to the clinical context template is classified as a "Major" change and requires CMO (Dr. Priya Patel) approval.
- **Access Control:** Access to the `output-schema-registry` admin console and DynamoDB audit table is governed by the Principle of Least Privilege, reviewed quarterly. Access requests use the standard JSM-IT ticket and require VP of Clinical AI approval.
- **Documented Intended Purpose:** The legally required Intended Purpose statement (EU AI Act Art. 13) for each model is maintained in the Tier 3 template library by the CIS. The CIS, not engineering, is the sole approver for any changes to the text of a model’s `intended_use_population_statement`.

| **Out-of-Scope Flagged** | `out_of_scope_inference_rate` | **< 0.1%** of total inferences | Datadog; Real-time alert to Clinical AI Product team |

### 7.2 AI Output Quality Review (Monthly)
Dr. Aisha Okafor, in collaboration with the Clinical QA Lead, shall conduct a monthly review. A random sample of 150 Output Records (100 synchronous, 50 batch) will be audited for:
1.  Plausibility of the Confidence Score relative to the Explanation.
2.  Clinical safety and accuracy of the generated Tier 3 clinical context narrative.
3.  Correct functioning of the HOM gateway (comparing raw acceptance vs. override rates).
A formal report, the "AI Output Assurance Report," must be published to the `#clinical-ai-governance` channel within the first five business days of the following month.

## 8. Exception Handling and Escalation

### 8.1 Deviation Handling
**8.1.1 Minor Technical Deviation (e.g., Tier 4 latency spike):**
If the `total_latency_ms` breaches the OLA but the Output Record is complete, a `P3` (Low) issue is automatically logged in Jira. The MLOps Engineer on-call is responsible for investigation within the next business day. No retroactive documentation is required.

**8.1.2 Major Documentation Deviation (e.g., incomplete Output Record):**
Any violation of the mandatory six-tier rule triggers a `P1` (Critical) incident via PagerDuty. The Clinical AI MLOps Manager must initiate the Incident Response Protocol (SOP-IT-001). The VP of Clinical AI (Dr. Okafor) must approve any permanent exception to the required documentation format. Exceptions are granted in writing, stored in a "SOP Deviations" register in Confluence, and must include a dated risk assessment and a specific expiration date. Temporary emergency exceptions can be authorized by the CTO for a maximum of 24 hours.

### 8.2 Clinical Dispute Escalation
**8.2.1 "Contested" Feedback Workflow:** When a clinician selects the "Contested" action in the HOM interface (Tier 6), the following automated workflow is triggered:
1.  A `P2` (High) ticket is created in the `CLINICAL-SAFETY` Jira project.
2.  The complete `Output Record`, along with a 60-minute window of patient FHIR history pre- and post-contestation, is attached to the ticket.
3.  The ticket is automatically assigned to the Clinical Informatics Specialist (CIS) on duty.
4.  The CIS has a **4-hour response SLA** to acknowledge and review the contestation.
5.  If the CIS determines the AI output was clinically inappropriate or erroneous, the matter is escalated to the CMO (Dr. Priya Patel) for a potential Adverse Event report and mandatory assessment under the EU AI Act Art. 62 (Serious Incident Reporting).
